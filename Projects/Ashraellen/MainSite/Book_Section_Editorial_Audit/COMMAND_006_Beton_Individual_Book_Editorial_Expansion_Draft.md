## Core editorial position for BETON

`БЕТОН` is not just “the first book of MONOLITH”.

It is the first state of the trilogy:

```text
БЕТОН — первая стадия МОНОЛИТА: затвердевшая стабильность,
где порядок становится тюрьмой, память — угрозой,
а первая трещина возникает внутри человека раньше,
чем на стене системы.
```

The public page must present `БЕТОН` as:

- the first volume of `МОНОЛИТ`;
- a philosophical anti-dystopian novel;
- a literary investigation of stability as pressure;
- a story about memory, edited reality, internal crack, system noise, and the beginning of collapse.

Do not make the page sound like a grant application.
Do not use dry institutional language.
Do not turn the page into a lecture, manifesto, therapy page, spiritual teaching, political pamphlet, or academic abstract.

Keep the temperature of `БЕТОН`:

```text
бетон, стабильность, порядок, стена, память, дефект,
исправление, шум, трещина, Департамент Смыслов.
```

## Required draft blocks

Prepare public-facing draft text for the following blocks.

### 1. Hero / entry into the book

Provide:

- optional updated kicker;
- optional updated lead;
- one strong short formula for the book;
- suggested CTA labels if needed.

Preserve the identity:

```text
БЕТОН
Том I трилогии МОНОЛИТ
```

### 2. О книге

Prepare 3–5 paragraphs.

Explain `БЕТОН` as the first volume of `МОНОЛИТ`, but do not repeat the series page too mechanically.

This block should answer:

- what kind of book this is;
- what state of the world it captures;
- why stability matters;
- what the first crack means.

### 3. Без спойлеров

Prepare 3–5 concise paragraphs.

Describe the premise without revealing plot turns.

Must include, if organically possible:

- stability as prison;
- memory as danger;
- the Department of Meanings / Департамент Смыслов;
- noise / calibration / correction;
- the first inner crack.

### 4. Художественно-исследовательская рамка

Prepare 3–5 paragraphs.

Explain what `БЕТОН` investigates through fiction:

- system control;
- edited memory;
- normality as pressure;
- stability as violence;
- human perception under institutional calibration;
- the beginning of collapse as an inner event.

Do not write this as a grant text.

### 5. Темы / смысловые узлы

Prepare 6–9 short nodes.

Each node must have:

```text
Title — short explanation.
```

Possible nodes:

- стабильность;
- память;
- трещина;
- шум;
- исправление;
- Департамент Смыслов;
- страх;
- порядок;
- стена;
- редактирование реальности.

Choose the strongest 6–9.

### 6. Для кого эта книга

Prepare a concise reader-facing block.

The book may be for readers who need:

- philosophical dystopia;
- psychological / social pressure rather than decorative cyberpunk;
- fiction about memory and systems;
- a cold story where the first rebellion is not a battle, but a crack in perception.

### 7. Место в трилогии

Prepare a block explaining:

- `БЕТОН` is Stage I of `МОНОЛИТ`;
- it precedes `ЖИЖА` and `ГАЗ`;
- it establishes the wall, the norm, the correction mechanism, and the first crack;
- it must not summarize the whole trilogy too much.

### 8. Где читать / издания

Prepare a neutral public-facing block based on existing links on the page.

Do not invent availability.

Check existing page for current purchase / reading buttons and preserve only verified links.

Mention language/status only if already supported by current page or existing site context.

### 9. Что важно не перепутать

Prepare 4–6 short statements.

Required boundaries:

- `БЕТОН` is not a standalone political pamphlet;
- not decorative cyberpunk about gadgets;
- not only a social thriller;
- not an instruction for rebellion;
- it is the first volume of an artistic/literary study of how stability becomes a system of pressure.

### 10. Existing material to preserve

Identify existing phrases / blocks from `ru/books/monolith/beton/index.html` that should be preserved.

Especially check and preserve if present:

```text
Бетон начинается не со стены.
Он начинается с привычки называть тюрьму стабильностью.
```

Also preserve verified buttons / links to available editions.
