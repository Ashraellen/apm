## 2. Accepted decisions

### 2.1. Telegram / public channels wording

Decision: Telegram should be listed in the professional dossier, but honestly as a Russian-language channel, not as an international/multilingual channel.

Final English wording:

```text
Public channels:
• YouTube — multilingual video archive
• Instagram — visual public field
• Telegram — Russian-language notes and project updates
```

Final Russian sense:

```text
Публичные каналы:
• YouTube — многоязычный видеоархив
• Instagram — визуальное публичное поле
• Telegram — заметки и обновления проекта на русском языке
```

Rejected wording:

```text
русскоязычные заметки и обновления исходного языка
```

Reason: it sounded awkward and overly bureaucratic. The intended meaning was simply that Telegram contains Russian-language project notes and updates.

### 2.2. `— mark of presence` is a canonical non-translatable seal

Decision: the text under the site symbol must remain exactly:

```text
— mark of presence
```

It stays in English on every language version of the site, even if the page is not in English.

Reason: this is not an interface label. It is a brand seal/signature attached to the Ashraellen symbol.

Rejected/avoided translations discussed:

- `— знак присутствия`
- `— печать присутствия`
- `— след присутствия`
- `— метка присутствия`

These can be understood as internal translations, but they should not replace the site text.

Important note: an attempted helper-script localization idea was started but stopped. The repository was checked afterward; no localization block was committed. The canonical state remains English-only.

### 2.3. `Ashraellen` at the top of pages is always Latin-script

Decision: the brand name at the top of pages must always remain:

```text
Ashraellen
```

Do not translate, transliterate, decline or localize it in the page header/brand mark.

This applies regardless of page language.

Together, the two permanent non-translatable brand elements are:

```text
Ashraellen
— mark of presence
```
