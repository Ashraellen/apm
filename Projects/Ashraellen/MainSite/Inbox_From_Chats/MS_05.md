## 1. Summary

This chat collected a positioning frame for Ashraellen’s books, especially the trilogy “МОНОЛИТ” / “MONOLITH”, as artistic-philosophical research rather than ordinary fiction or entertainment literature.

The central need: explain on the website, in grant/institutional contexts, and possibly in author/project pages that Ashraellen works in a non-standard form: instead of writing dry philosophical treatises, he uses novels as research instruments and narrative laboratories.

Core formulation developed in the chat:

> My books are artistic-philosophical research presented in the form of novels. I study mechanisms of perception, power, language, social environment and inner transformation. Instead of an academic treatise, I create an artistic model in which the reader does not merely encounter an idea, but experiences it from within.

Another strong formula:

> I write novels as research instruments — not to entertain the reader, but to reveal within the reader the processes that an ordinary philosophical text can only describe.

## 2. Accepted decisions

- Do not define the books bluntly as “scientific research” in the narrow academic sense.
- Use the more accurate and stronger phrase: “artistic-philosophical research” / “literary-philosophical research” / “practice-based artistic research”.
- Present the novels as a method of investigation, not only as literary works.
- The novels should be described as model environments or laboratories where philosophical, social, psychological and perceptual mechanisms are tested through plot, characters, symbolic systems and pressure from the fictional world.
- The website should avoid framing Ashraellen as simply a novelist, blogger, spiritual teacher, influencer or motivational author.
- The correct public frame: Ashraellen creates literary-philosophical research through novels, satire, visual formulas, public texts, multilingual archive and artistic practice.

## 3. Done / created / updated

A working explanation was prepared for future site/grant use:

> I work at the border of literature, philosophy, social diagnosis and research into perception. My books are not novels only in the entertainment sense. For me, the novel is a form of research laboratory where the human being can be studied not through abstract theses, but through situation, environment, pressure, language, fear, body and choice.
>
> The trilogy “MONOLITH” investigates three states of human and social reality: solidification, liquefaction and evaporation of meaning. “BETON” shows how personality and society harden inside a system. “SLUDGE” investigates the collapse of boundaries, habituation to deformation and the transformation of the unacceptable into the normal. “GAS” moves toward invisible influences: image, contour, power, reflection and the semantic atmosphere that a person already breathes without noticing.
>
> I deliberately do not choose the form of a dry philosophical treatise, because a treatise often addresses only the intellect. A novel acts deeper: it allows the reader not simply to understand an idea, but to pass through it internally. I do not explain the mechanism from outside; I place the reader inside a model where the mechanism begins to operate on the reader themselves.
>
> This approach can be called artistic-philosophical research: I study not only ideas, but how ideas become an environment, how language becomes breathing, how fear becomes architecture, and how habit becomes a form of power.
