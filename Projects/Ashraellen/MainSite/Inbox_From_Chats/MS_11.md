## 1. Summary

This chat continued the Ashraellen.com main website build and focused on making the site institution-ready rather than merely presentable. Main areas of work:

- Privacy pages were brought into the common site design and language structure.
- Contact pages were created and later clarified across all project languages.
- Russian professional dossier was expanded into a grant/institutional working page, then cleaned and baked directly into HTML.
- English professional dossier was made the main international version.
- Professional dossier pages were fully rewritten across all project languages without shortcuts or temporary JS overlays.
- Telegram roles were clarified site-wide: `@AshraellenLive` is the direct contact; `@ashraellenchannel` is the public content channel.
- X/Twitter profile bio wording was discussed and translated into English.

## 2. Accepted decisions

### 2.1 Privacy pages

- Privacy Policy should exist as a full page on all language versions.
- `/privacy.html` and language privacy pages should follow the general site design, not look like temporary legal pages.
- Privacy link should be a small lower-right link on language entry pages.
- Privacy menu should be compact: language-specific equivalent of `Entry | Dossier` / `Вход | Досье`.
- Privacy typography should be smaller and calmer than the first version.

### 2.2 Contact pages

- Contact should be a top-right small button from language entry pages, similar in logic to Professional Dossier / Privacy.
- Contact page menu should remain minimal: `Entry | Dossier` / localized equivalent.
- Contact page should not use the expanded menu `Entry | Research | Public | Books | Dossier | Language`; this was considered too heavy.
- Contact pages use the background file `assets/backgrounds/contact-bg.webp`.
- Contact pages use `assets/contact.css` and `assets/contact-scale.css`.
- The Telegram distinction is canonical:
  - `@AshraellenLive` — direct Telegram contact.
  - `@ashraellenchannel` — public Telegram channel for texts, notes, updates and project materials.
- Contact pages must not list `@AshraellenLive` as a public channel.

### 2.3 Professional dossier

- Professional dossier should become an institution-facing and grant-friendly landing page, not merely an author bio.
- English professional dossier should be the main international version.
- Russian version can remain the working/reading version for internal review and style decisions.
- Professional dossier should include:
  - author / project positioning;
  - position of observation;
  - method of observation;
  - selected works;
  - current project seeking support;
  - technical infrastructure;
  - expected outcomes;
  - what the audience will receive;
  - public response;
  - contact.
- Equipment and subscriptions may be mentioned, but not as a shopping list. They should be framed as production infrastructure needed for video, audio, translation, archiving, website and publication work.
- Detailed equipment lists, storage lists and account/subscription lists are better reserved for specific grant budgets, not the public dossier.
- “Expected outcomes” should be written as connected text, not as cards.
- “What the audience will receive” should be explicit: people receive access to precise, non-simplified forms of observation, not ready-made recipes.
- The phrase “оформление проекта в понятную форму для фондов, издателей и институций” was judged too internal and replaced conceptually by a clearer public structure for readers, viewers, publishers, research partners and cultural institutions.
- Avoid overusing “research” / “исследование” and “mechanisms” / “механизмы”; use observation, work, practice, approach, structures, processes, etc.
- Avoid unstable subject phrasing: after “Ashraellen — working name of Nikolai Kostyshev,” use “the author,” “the project,” or “Nikolai Kostyshev (Ashraellen)” where clearer.
- Link labels with arrows like “Open research section →” looked like placeholders; final labels should be shorter and calmer.

### 2.4 X / public profile wording

Accepted English wording for a short X profile line:

```text
Ashraellen
I write about where interpretation ends and reality begins.
Books · observations · philosophy without ready-made recipes.
```

For X category selection, from visible options the best available category was “Education” because the project is closer to books, philosophy, public thought and observation than to entertainment or commerce.

## 7. Positioning / grants

The professional dossier was turned into a grant/institutional landing page with the following core position:

- Ashraellen is an independent artistic/philosophical/literary research project by Nikolai Kostyshev.
- The work is framed as artistic observation and literary research, not as a blog, therapy, motivation, spiritual instruction or infobusiness.
- The project combines books, essays, videos, public formulas, multilingual archive and website infrastructure.
- Support is framed as enabling a stable production cycle:

```text
observation → text → publication → translation → public response → archive
```

Technical infrastructure is framed as necessary production infrastructure, not personal luxury.

Expected outcomes include:

- developed multilingual platform;
- structured public archive;
- books and literary projects as artistic knowledge;
- translations/adaptations;
- video/audio/subtitles/short formats;
- sustainable working cycle.

Public value is framed as:

- access to precise, non-simplified forms of observation;
- recognition of anxiety, self-deception and loss of inner clarity;
- no motivational rhetoric, ideology or ready-made recipes.
