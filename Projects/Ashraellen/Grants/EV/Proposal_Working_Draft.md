# Proposal Working Draft — Emergent Ventures

Purpose:
Prepare the main Emergent Ventures proposal for Ashraellen.

Hard constraint:
The proposal must be self-contained and adapted to EV. It should not read like a generic cultural grant application.

Working target:
Up to 1500 words unless the current official form specifies otherwise.

EV tone:
Clear, direct, unusual, founder-driven, intellectually alive, practical.

Core argument to develop:
Ashraellen is a multilingual public research platform using literary, satirical, visual, video and archive-based forms to make hidden mechanisms of self-deception, interpretation, attention collapse, loss of reality and inner autonomy visible to a broad public.

Possible structure:

1. Who I am
   - Nikolai Kostyshev, working publicly as Ashraellen.
   - Independent writer and artistic researcher living in Poland.
   - Russian is the primary language of thought and writing; other languages function as access channels.

2. What I am building
   - A multilingual public knowledge platform and artistic-philosophical research archive.
   - Books, novels, satire, essays, video, visual formulas, sound and public responses are instruments of one inquiry.

3. Why it matters
   - Many people live inside interpretation, fear, inherited scripts, information noise and digital exhaustion.
   - Existing academic, therapeutic and media systems often do not reach them at the moment of recognition.
   - Ashraellen creates forms in which mechanisms can be recognised from within.

4. Why this is unusual
   - It does not offer motivation, doctrine, therapy or ideology.
   - It works through recognition rather than instruction.
   - It combines literary depth, satire, public philosophy, multilingual access and digital archive infrastructure.

5. What funding would unlock
   - Sustained working time.
   - Completion and publication of major works.
   - Translation and adaptation of key materials.
   - Website/archive development.
   - Production infrastructure for video, audio and visual forms.

6. What outcomes are expected
   - Completed and published works.
   - A stronger multilingual archive.
   - Regular public outputs without forcing artificial numerical promises.
   - A reproducible working cycle: observation, text, publication, translation, archiving, public feedback.
