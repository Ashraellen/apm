# Main proposal draft

My name is Nikolai Kostyshev. I work publicly under the name Ashraellen. I am an independent writer and artistic researcher living in Poland. My primary creative language is Russian, but the project is built as a multilingual public knowledge platform, with English, Polish and other languages used as access channels.

I am building Ashraellen as a long-term independent literary and philosophical research project exploring self-deception, perception, inner freedom, information noise, and the human search for meaning. The project uses novels, satire, essays, public formulas, video, sound, visual forms and a multilingual digital archive as different instruments of one inquiry: how people lose contact with reality through interpretation, fear, inherited scripts, ideological pressure, digital exhaustion and external narratives — and how those mechanisms can become visible from within ordinary experience.

The project does not offer therapy, motivation, spiritual instruction or ideology. It creates forms of observation.

This distinction matters. Many people today do not experience confusion only as a lack of information. They experience it as an overload of interpretations. They live inside reactions, inherited phrases, algorithmic noise, political pressure, private fear, cultural scripts and emotional automatisms. A person may know many facts and still be unable to distinguish an event from their reaction to it, a thought from reality, or pain from the worldview built around that pain.

Ashraellen is designed to make those hidden mechanisms recognizable.

The method is artistic research. Fiction is not used as decoration or illustration. A novel can work as a long-form instrument for observing how fear, belief, language and self-deception operate inside human experience. Satire can reveal mechanisms that direct explanation often cannot reach. A short public formula can capture a moment of recognition. A video can transmit rhythm, pressure and state. A multilingual archive can preserve the work outside the disappearing flow of social media and make it accessible to different audiences.

Several parts of the project already exist.

The MONOLITH trilogy is a dystopian and philosophical fiction cycle about control, memory, dehumanization, war, system collapse and the transformation of human beings into material. Error 404: God Not Found is a philosophical-satirical novel cycle about heavenly bureaucracy, human complaint, the demand for cosmic justice and the collapse of the question “Why me?” Church of Saint Whine is a satirical-philosophical project about burnout, complaint, self-deception, fake motivation and the culture of helplessness. Alongside these larger works, I develop short public formulas, essays, videos and multilingual website pages that present Ashraellen as a public research archive rather than a single book, channel or brand.

The unusual part is not that I use many formats. The unusual part is that these formats are not separate products. They are parts of one public recognition system. The same human mechanisms can be approached through a dystopian novel, a satirical ritual, a short formula, a video, a philosophical essay, or a public response from a viewer who suddenly recognizes themselves in a sentence.

I agree with the mainstream view that attention, trust and the ability to distinguish reliable information from noise are foundational public goods in a digital society. I also agree that loneliness, anxiety, mental exhaustion and loss of meaning are serious contemporary problems. My disagreement is not with that diagnosis, but with the narrow assumption that only academic, therapeutic or institutional language can address it. Ashraellen tests whether literature, satire, public philosophy and artistic research can reach people at the moment when they recognize their own mechanisms from within.

Funding from Emergent Ventures would create a 12-month acceleration period. The support would not fund an idea that exists only on paper. It would give concentrated time and production infrastructure to a project that already has books, public channels, a website, multilingual structure, conceptual method and a growing body of work.

During the 12 months, I would focus on four practical outcomes.

First, I would complete and prepare key literary works and essays for broader publication, including continued development of the MONOLITH universe and Error 404: God Not Found. Second, I would strengthen the multilingual archive on Ashraellen.com, especially the English-facing research, method, professional and publication pages. Third, I would produce a stable cycle of public outputs: short formulas, essays, videos, audio materials and satirical forms that translate the research into recognizable public language. Fourth, I would build a more sustainable production infrastructure: reliable hardware, editing tools, translation and language support, hosting, backup, formatting and publication workflow.

The requested budget is USD 65,000 for 12 months. This includes protected working time, production equipment, software and AI tools, translation and editorial support, website and archive development, publication preparation, and a small contingency. The budget is lean and founder-driven. It is intended to unlock sustained work, not institutional overhead.

The project can become sustainable through several paths: book sales, paid editions, speaking and consultation formats, licensing/adaptation of texts and visual formulas, reader support, commissioned essays or lectures, and possible future institutional partnerships. But the project needs one concentrated period to become clear, organized and accessible enough for those paths to open realistically.

What I am asking for is not a cultural grant in the conventional sense. I am asking for support to accelerate an unusual public knowledge project built outside standard academic, media and therapeutic systems. Ashraellen is difficult to classify because it sits between literature, philosophy, satire, artistic research, digital archive and public education. That is also why it may have value.

People often do not change because someone explains life to them. They change when they recognize the mechanism that has been operating inside them without a name.

Ashraellen is an attempt to give such mechanisms form.
