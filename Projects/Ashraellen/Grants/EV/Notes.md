# Notes — Emergent Ventures

Purpose:
Collect EV-specific decisions, risks, positioning notes and working observations.

## Current decisions

1. EV is treated as a separate institutional workspace, not as a copy of the general grant pack.
2. The application should be adapted to EV as an unusual, high-upside, founder-driven public knowledge project.
3. The proposal must be self-contained.
4. Supporting documents and links are useful, but they must not carry the main argument.
5. Avoid presenting Ashraellen as a blog, YouTube project, spiritual teaching, therapy, motivation, content business or request for personal rescue.

## Strong framing

Ashraellen is a multilingual public research platform that uses literature, satire, visual formulas, video, sound and archive structure to help people recognise hidden mechanisms of self-deception, interpretation, loss of reality, attention collapse and inner autonomy.

## Risks

- Sounding too much like a standard cultural grant.
- Sounding too mystical or therapeutic.
- Overloading the application with too many project branches.
- Making the budget look like personal support instead of protected working time and production infrastructure.
- Depending on PDFs or attachments instead of making the main application strong.

## Strategic emphasis for EV

EV should see:
- a person with an unusual trajectory;
- a body of work already in motion;
- a project that can grow with relatively modest support;
- a public knowledge system outside standard academic and media channels;
- a practical 12-month acceleration path.

## Open questions

- Exact amount to request.
- Whether to request 12 months, 18 months or a staged amount.
- How much technical infrastructure to include directly.
- How to phrase the mainstream consensus view answer.
- Whether to include personal biography strongly or keep it as background.
