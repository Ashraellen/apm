## Current decisions

- Treat Kone as its own institutional workspace.
- Do not reuse generic EV language without adaptation.
- Emphasize artistic research, literature, meaning, public archive and multilingual cultural work.
- Do not frame Ashraellen as blog, YouTube project, spiritual teaching, therapy, motivation, infobusiness or request for personal rescue.
- Kone has clarified that the connection to Finland must be demonstrated in the application itself.
- Current practical eligibility routes: Finnish institutional affiliation OR working group with a Finnish citizen / Finland-based participant.
- Current preferred route: create our own working group led by Nikolai Kostyshev / Ashraellen and invite a real Finnish / Finland-based specialist into it.
- The grant-period focus should not be only `Radiance / Sampo`.
- Current preferred grant-period frame: `Radiance: Sampo and Song — Finnish / Kalevala-related artistic research and literary localization pathway`.
- Sampo and Song should be treated as the first two completed research nodes of the Radiance cycle and the minimum grant-period focus.
- The Finnish participant must not be symbolic or passport-based.
- The real role to seek: Finnish literary localization consultant and cultural-context advisor.
- The role is necessary because future Finnish-language access and Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related cultural accuracy require a Finnish-language and cultural-context specialist.
- Money should not be divided informally after a grant is awarded; all compensation must be tied to documented work and budget lines.
- Rights to Radiance / Sampo / Song and the core artistic-research architecture remain with Nikolai Kostyshev / Ashraellen unless separately agreed in writing.
