# Working Group Strategy — Kone Foundation

Status: active working strategy  
Created: 2026-06-29  
Workspace: `Projects/Ashraellen/Grants/Kone/`

---

## 1. Purpose

This document fixes the current working decisions about the possible Kone Foundation application structure for Ashraellen / Radiance.

It should prevent the strategy from being reduced either to an isolated individual application or to a nominal Finnish connection.

---

## 2. Current grant-period focus

The Kone application should not be framed as only `Radiance / Sampo`.

Current preferred frame:

```text
Radiance: Sampo and Song
Finnish / Kalevala-related artistic research and literary localization pathway
```

Meaning:

```text
Radiance is the wider long-term literary-philosophical artistic research cycle.
Sampo and Song are the first two completed research nodes of the cycle and should form the minimum grant-period focus.
```

The grant period should work with at least two research nodes:

```text
Book I — Sampo
Focus: abundance, provision, ownership, participation, the living source of the world.

Book II — Song
Focus: word, song, hearing, language, tuning, speech as relation rather than mere expression.
```

Strategic reason:

```text
Sampo proves the first completed Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related node.
Song shows that Radiance is not a single-book project but a repeatable artistic-research method across the series.
Together they demonstrate the first pair of the cycle's research architecture.
```

---

## 3. Kone eligibility route clarified

Kone Foundation clarified that there is no separate application-form route to select for this issue.
The connection to Finland must be clearly demonstrated elsewhere in the application.

For this project, the practical routes are:

```text
1. affiliation with a Finnish institution; OR
2. a working group including someone who is a Finnish citizen or based in Finland.
```

Current preferred practical route:

```text
Create our own working group led by Nikolai Kostyshev / Ashraellen and invite a real Finnish / Finland-based specialist into it.
```

This is not a nominal or symbolic Finnish connection.
The Finnish participant must have a real and necessary role.

## 4. Working group structure

Proposed structure:

```text
Working group led by Nikolai Kostyshev / Ashraellen

Lead author / principal artistic researcher:
Nikolai Kostyshev / Ashraellen

Finnish / Finland-based collaborator:
Finnish literary localization consultant and cultural-context advisor
```

Role of Nikolai Kostyshev / Ashraellen:

```text
Lead author, creator of Radiance, principal artistic researcher.
Responsible for the literary-philosophical research frame, writing, English project materials, selection of excerpts, public archive structure and overall artistic direction.
```

Role of the Finnish / Finland-based collaborator:

```text
Finnish literary localization consultant and cultural-context advisor.
Responsible for reviewing Finnish-language access strategy, advising on Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related terminology, localizing selected Finnish samples from Sampo and Song, checking cultural resonance, and helping the project avoid superficial or inaccurate handling of Finnish mythopoetic material.
```

---

## 5. Why a Finnish specialist is genuinely necessary

The Finnish collaborator is not needed for a formal passport-based reason.
The role is necessary because the project engages with Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related mythopoetic material and aims to prepare future Finnish-language access.

Core reasoning:

```text
The Finnish connection is not decorative.
A Finnish-language and cultural-context collaborator is necessary because the project engages with Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related mythopoetic material and aims to prepare responsible Finnish-language access to the work.
The collaborator's role would include reviewing Finnish translation samples, advising on cultural and linguistic resonance, and helping the project avoid inaccurate or superficial use of Finnish / Kalevala-related material.
```

Internal Russian formula:

```text
Нам нужен не номинальный финский участник.
Нам нужен человек, который поможет книге пройти в финский язык без потери смысла и без культурной небрежности.
```

## 6. Money / budget principle

Money should not be divided informally after receiving a grant.
All compensation must be built into the application budget and tied to real work.

Core principle:

```text
We do not seek a person in order to divide money.
We seek a Finnish / Finland-based participant whose work makes the project stronger, more accurate and more responsible in the Finnish cultural-linguistic context.
```

Possible budget logic:

```text
Nikolai Kostyshev / Ashraellen:
lead author, artistic research, project development, writing, archive structure, English materials, overall direction.

Finnish / Finland-based collaborator:
Finnish localization, cultural-linguistic consultation, terminology review, selected sample localization, Finnish context review.

Other possible costs:
translation, editing, website / archive support, design, public presentation, external services, if allowed and strategically justified.
```

Approximate role logic:

```text
If the Finnish participant is only a consultant: limited paid advisory / localization role.
If the Finnish participant is a full working group member: clear work package, duration, deliverables and budget line.
If the Finnish participant becomes a major co-researcher / co-curator: larger share only if the contribution is genuinely large.
```

Rights principle:

```text
Rights to Radiance / Sampo / Song and the core artistic-research architecture remain with Nikolai Kostyshev / Ashraellen unless a separate written agreement states otherwise.
The Finnish collaborator's role is advisory, editorial, localization-related and cultural-contextual unless separately agreed.
```

## 8. Search strategy for working group participants

Do not send chaotic outreach.
Prepare a small package first, then contact candidates in waves.

### First package to prepare

```text
1. Project Brief EN — Radiance: Sampo and Song.
2. Working Group Role Proposal EN.
3. Short invitation email EN.
4. Candidate tracker.
5. One selected English excerpt from Sampo.
6. One selected English excerpt or concise summary from Song.
```

### Candidate buckets

```text
A. Finnish literary localization consultant
Practical role: review / localize Finnish samples, advise on literary language.

A. Finnish / Finland-based cultural-context advisor
Practical role: advise on Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related terminology and resonance.

B. Artistic research collaborator
Practical role: strengthen the research-and-art frame and possible institutional readability.

C. Institutional contact / host / affiliation route
Practical role: possible Finnish institutional affiliation or orientation.
```

Possible search routes:

```text
1. Kalevala Society — orientation and possible direction to suitable person / network.
2. Finnish Literature Society / SKS — orientation after summer break; possible archive / research / literature context.
3. Finnish literary translators / editors / localization consultants.
4. University / folklore / oral tradition researchers.
5. Uniarts Helsinki / artistic research context.
6. Independent Finland-based artistic researchers or literary translators.
7. Cultural heritage / Kalevala-related organizations such as Juminkeko, if strategically useful.
```
