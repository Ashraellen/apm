# First_Decisions — Radiance / Сияние

Date of first fixation: 2026-06-13

This file records the first canon decisions for the new series **Сияние**.

---

## 1. Series identity

Series title:

**Сияние**

English / repository-safe project folder:

```text
Radiance
```

Series subtitle:

**Северные сказания о настоящей истории мира**

Working genre:

```text
philosophical speculative fiction
mythopoetic fantasy
artistic research through fiction
northern campfire cycle
```

The series should feel like a story told by fire, not like a dissertation wearing a fur coat.

---

## 2. Narrative frame

The main frame:

```text
A strange narrator sits by a campfire in a summer camp and tells children, or almost children, the "real" history of the world hidden inside northern myths.
```

The listeners should probably be teenagers rather than small children.

Reason:

```text
They are still open enough to hear myth, but already wounded enough to understand that adults lie, systems break, hunger exists, and God is not found through slogans.
```

The campfire is important because it is the first human screen:

```text
Before books, lectures, phones, feeds, interfaces and grant portals, people sat near fire and listened.
```

The narrator does not behave like a guru.

He does not demand belief.

He says, in essence:

```text
I do not ask you to believe. Just listen. Some stories become clear only after a person stops demanding proof from them.
```

---

## 3. Main metaphysical discovery

The early personal conclusion behind the project:

```text
All Creation is the Manna-machine.
```

Meaning:

```text
Earth already produces food, air, water, warmth, seed, fruit, rhythm and renewal.
The human being does not need to invent abundance.
The human being needs to stop breaking the living mechanism that already feeds him.
```

This is the central turn:

```text
Sampo was not lost.
The human being stopped recognizing it.
```

The true problem is not the absence of miracle.

The true problem:

```text
A person does not recognize a miracle when it grows from soil instead of being made of metal.
```

---

## 4. Sampo and abundance machines

Sampo is the first central motif.

Internal working formula:

```text
Sampo is the Manna-machine.
```

This is an authorial mythopoetic equation, not an academic claim of direct historical identity.

In the fictional system, Sampo, manna, the self-setting tablecloth, the cornucopia, Akshaya Patra and other abundance devices may be treated as different cultural interfaces of the same principle:

```text
The Principle / Protocol of Abundance.
```

Core distinction:

```text
People search for a device that will feed them.
But they already live inside a Creation that feeds them.
```

The machine does not fail because it is absent.

It fails because the user approaches it as a consumer rather than a participant.

---

## 5. Pantheons as teaching teams

The author's earlier bogoiskatelstvo included the study of many pantheons:

- Greek;
- Indian;
- Russian / Slavic;
- Finnish / Karelian;
- and other mythological systems.

The inner image from that period:

```text
Pantheons appeared as "landing teams": groups arriving from somewhere beyond ordinary human development — from the future, a parallel layer, another mode of being, or a higher order of reality — to teach and develop early human communities.
```

For external / institutional language, this must be translated carefully:

```text
Pantheons are symbolic systems of transmission: mythic ensembles through which cultures encoded models of language, law, craft, ritual, power, death, desire, cosmology and inner development.
```

In the fiction itself, ambiguity may remain alive.

The reader should not be forced to decide whether these beings are:

- real arrivals;
- future humans;
- entities from parallel space;
- archetypal programs;
- mythic names of inner functions;
- forms through which Spirit taught humanity;
- or all of this at once.

---

## 6. Finnish / northern spine

The series should partially connect different pantheons, but the main emphasis must remain on the Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related northern line.

Other pantheons are mirrors.

The northern material is the main entrance.

Current formula:

```text
Not "all myths equally at once", but one northern spine with comparative echoes.
```

The project must not pretend to "explain Finland to Finns".

Correct attitude:

```text
The author returns to Kalevala-related material as part of his own earlier spiritual-philosophical search and now works with it as an artistic researcher, carefully, respectfully, and without claiming academic ownership.
```

## 8. Main prohibitions

Do not turn the series into:

- a school retelling of Kalevala;
- a list of gods and their abilities;
- mythological Marvel;
- pseudo-historical proof of ancient aliens;
- a sermon about ecology;
- a direct spiritual teaching;
- a lecture disguised as fantasy;
- a grant application wearing reindeer antlers.

The book must remain literature.

The research must live inside image, scene, rhythm, voice, silence and recognition.

---

## 9. Future institutional direction

The first external step should not be a grant request.

Correct first contact with Finnish institutions:

```text
The author is beginning a philosophical speculative fiction / artistic research project involving Kalevala-related material and asks for orientation regarding sources, context, and possible expert direction.
```

Possible future contacts:

- Kalevala Society;
- Finnish Literature Society / SKS;
- Uniarts Helsinki / artistic research networks;
- other relevant Finnish / Karelian cultural or research bodies.

Longer-term grant logic:

```text
First establish a real Finnish research / cultural connection.
Then adapt the project for a working-time grant, possibly through Kone Foundation or another suitable body.
```

## 10. First core sentence

```text
They searched for a machine that produces bread.
They lived on a planet where bread grew from the ground.
That is why the first Teachers understood: the problem was not hunger.
The problem was that the human being did not recognize a miracle unless it was made of metal.
```
