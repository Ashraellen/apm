# Mythopoetic Instruction Canon — Radiance / Сияние

Status: ACTIVE INTERNAL CANON  
Created: 2026-06-29  
Updated: 2026-06-29

This file records the deeper internal canon behind **Radiance / Сияние**: why the series exists, what it studies, and how ancient stories are understood inside the project.

It is an internal canon file. It may be translated into external institutional language, but it should not be copied into grant letters without adaptation.

---

## 1. Core internal premise

All ancient stories, epics, fairy tales and mythopoetic narratives are not treated inside **Radiance** as empty inventions, primitive explanations, decorative folklore or dead cultural material.

They are treated as image-based instructions.

They tell, in symbolic and narrative form:

```text
how it was,
how it is,
and how it should be.
```

Russian internal formula:

```text
Древние истории, в образной форме, рассказывают нам о том, как было, как есть и как должно быть.
```

The inner premise is that after the world was created, tools and instructions were left to all peoples. These instructions were not left only as abstract laws, technical diagrams, academic explanations or philosophical systems. They were left in forms that could pass through memory, song, fear, wonder, ritual, childhood, fire, landscape and language.

They were left as:

- stories;
- epics;
- fairy tales;
- songs;
- names;
- prohibitions;
- ordeals;
- sacred objects;
- impossible tasks;
- heroic failures;
- thresholds;
- monsters;
- helpers;
- gifts;
- warnings;
- repeated motifs;
- images that do not die when literal understanding is lost.

This is why myth can survive where doctrine collapses, and why a fairy tale can carry something a system cannot hold.

## 2. Truth in image, not literal chronicle

The project does not need to claim that every ancient story is a literal historical report.

The stronger internal position is this:

```text
They are true not because they are chronicles.
They are true because they preserve law in image.
```

Russian:

```text
Они истинны не потому, что являются хроникой.
Они истинны потому, что сохраняют закон в образе.
```

A mythopoetic image can be more durable than an explanation. An explanation depends on the reader's conceptual system. An image can pass through centuries, change clothing, enter another language, be misunderstood, laughed at, simplified, moralized, turned into folklore — and still keep a working charge inside itself.

Inside **Radiance**, the ancient image is not opposed to truth. The image is one of the ways truth survives time.

Core formula:

```text
Сказки и эпосы — это не ложь о прошлом.
Это правда, записанная на языке образов.
```

English internal formula:

```text
Fairy tales and epics are not lies about the past.
They are truth written in the language of images.
```

## 3. Why instructions were given as stories

A direct instruction can be refused, forgotten, institutionalized, weaponized or reduced to a slogan.

A story works differently.

A story does not only inform the mind. It passes through the whole human being:

- memory;
- imagination;
- fear;
- hunger;
- shame;
- longing;
- rhythm;
- compassion;
- recognition;
- desire;
- bodily response;
- the ability to see oneself without being directly accused.

The most important instructions were not always given as rules because rules can be obeyed without transformation.

A story can wait until a person becomes ready to understand it.

This is why **Radiance** does not try to turn myth into a dry system. The aim is not to extract a diagram from the story and throw the story away. The aim is to restore the working power of the image itself.

Internal formula:

```text
Мы не возвращаемся в древность.
Мы возвращаем древнему образу его рабочую силу.
```

English:

```text
We are not returning to antiquity.
We are returning the ancient image to its working force.
```

## 4. One root, many cultural languages

Alongside the Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related line, the author studies, with different degrees of depth, epics, fairy tales and ancient stories of different peoples.

The internal assumption is that many traditions may point toward one deep root or one related order, but this root was not given to all peoples in one flat, identical form.

Different peoples received different languages of access.

These languages were shaped by:

- land;
- climate;
- work;
- fear;
- food;
- animals;
- memory;
- wounds;
- migrations;
- enemies;
- kinship systems;
- rituals;
- sound;
- instruments;
- local spirits and names;
- the way each culture learned to hear the world.

Therefore, **Radiance** must not flatten all traditions into one interchangeable mythological soup.

The project does not say:

```text
All myths are the same.
All cultures say the same thing.
Differences do not matter.
```

The correct internal position is:

```text
Different peoples did not necessarily receive different truths.
They received different languages of access to truth.
```

Russian:

```text
Разные народы получили не разные истины, а разные языки доступа к истине.
```

This means that comparative work is allowed and important, but it must preserve reverence for cultural specificity.

A Finnish / Karelian image should not be dissolved into a generic universal symbol. A Slavic image should not be used as decoration. A Biblical echo should not erase a Vedic echo. A Greek parallel should not replace the northern spine.

The point is not to erase differences.

The point is to observe how a related inner order becomes visible through different cultural interfaces.

## 5. The Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related line

For **Radiance**, the Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related field remains the main northern spine.

This does not mean that other traditions are irrelevant.

It means that the primary entrance of the current cycle is northern, and that the first major working images are Sampo, Song, Väinämöinen, Ilmarinen, Louhi, Pohjola, Kullervo, Lemminkäinen, Ilmatar, Tapio, forest, threshold, craft, breath, return and participation.

Correct proportion:

```text
Northern spine.
Comparative echoes.
One deep order.
Many cultural languages.
Living literature.
```

The Kalevala-related field is not used as a decorative grant element. It is not treated as a costume, aesthetic flavor or exotic setting.

It is a living source field through which **Radiance** investigates how ancient images may still contain working instructions about abundance, language, craft, maturity, trauma, death, birth, forest, measure and responsibility.

## 6. What the author studies

The object of study is now wider than a single epic, a single motif, or a single literary cycle.

The author studies how ancient stories preserve instruction.

More precisely, the author studies four connected layers.

### 6.1. World-order

How is the world structured, if ancient stories are not merely fantasies but image-based witnesses of order?

This does not mean proving ancient events as literal archaeology.

It means asking what kind of world must be true for these images to keep returning across peoples and centuries.

### 6.2. The human being inside the world

How should the human being live inside an already given world?

Not as owner only, but as participant.

Not as consumer only, but as one who serves, guards, hears, cultivates, measures, receives and answers.

The human mistake repeated through the cycle is the attempt to extract provision without participation, power without maturity, song without hearing, craft without responsibility, return without transformation, forest without service.

### 6.3. The language of transmission

Why were the most important instructions transmitted through images, tales, songs, epics, riddles, impossible tasks, sacred objects and repeated motifs?

Why did the instruction become story?

Why did truth choose image as one of its vessels?

This is central to **Radiance** because the project itself must not become a manual. It must become literature.

### 6.4. The loss of reading

What happened to the human being if he began to read instruction as fiction, symbol as ornament, epic as old material, and living law as cultural archive?

The modern problem is not only disbelief.

The modern problem is loss of literacy before image.

A person can still read the words and no longer hear the instruction.

Radiance asks whether contemporary literature can help restore this hearing without becoming primitive literalism, ideology, sermon or academic deadness.

## 7. Artistic form as test, not proof

Radiance does not prove ancient truth as a thesis.

It tests whether artistic form can make ancient truth audible again — and not only audible, but clearly and distinctly understandable through lived literary experience.

Russian core formulation:

```text
Мы не доказываем древнюю истину как тезис.
Мы проверяем, может ли художественная форма снова сделать её слышимой и явственно понимаемой.
```

English working formulation:

```text
We do not prove ancient truth as a thesis.
We test whether artistic form can make it audible again — and clearly understandable as lived experience.
```

This formula is crucial because it keeps **Radiance** within artistic research rather than doctrine, sermon or academic proof.

The project is not trying to force belief.

It is not trying to establish a metaphysical thesis from above.

It is not trying to replace scholarship with revelation.

It asks whether fiction, scene, rhythm, image, silence, character and consequence can restore the working intelligibility of an ancient image.

The test is not whether the reader agrees with a proposition.

The test is whether the reader can begin to hear the instruction inside the image again.

---

## 8. Why the series exists

Radiance exists to restore the working force of ancient images through literature.

It does not exist to prove a doctrine.

It does not exist to build a new religion.

It does not exist to modernize old myths for entertainment.

It does not exist to explain epic from above.

It exists to test whether literary form can return the ancient image to life — so that the reader does not merely understand a concept, but encounters a law through scene, rhythm, silence, character, recognition and consequence.

Core purpose:

```text
To return ancient stories to their function: not as cultural decoration, but as living instructions for participation in the world.
```

Russian:

```text
Вернуть древним историям их функцию: быть не украшением культуры, а живыми инструкциями к участию в мире.
```

## 9. What each current book-node studies as instruction

### Book I — Сампо

Sampo is not primarily a question of whether a miraculous machine existed.

Sampo is an instruction about abundance.

It teaches that provision cannot be separated from participation. The human being searches for an abundance machine while already standing inside the living world that feeds him.

Wrong question:

```text
Where is Sampo?
```

Deeper question:

```text
Why does the human being want to own the source while standing inside it?
```

### Book II — Песня

Song is not merely sound, performance, beauty or magic.

Song is an instruction about language and hearing.

It asks what happens when speech stops being noise, opinion and argument, and begins to participate in the order of reality.

Wrong question:

```text
What is the magical song?
```

Deeper question:

```text
What must change in the human being before word becomes tuning rather than noise?
```

### Book III — Кузница

The forge is not merely a place where miraculous objects are made.

It is an instruction about craft, form, technology and responsibility.

It asks whether a person can forge happiness if the maker himself does not know measure.

### Book IV — Порог

The threshold is not merely refusal, danger or guarded treasure.

It is an instruction about maturity.

It asks why some gifts are withheld from those who still call appetite readiness.

### Book V — Сбой

The broken hero is not merely tragedy.

He is an instruction about trauma and false destiny.

He shows how pain can become worldview, memory can become script, and suffering can become destructive identity.

### Book VI — Возвращение

Return is not merely resurrection or a second chance.

It is an instruction about whether passage through death can become consciousness rather than vanity restored.

### Book VII — Дыхание

Birth is not merely origin myth.

It is an instruction about creation without violence: waiting, carrying, rupture, breath and allowing emergence.

### Book VIII — Лес

Forest is not merely nature, resource or background.

It is an instruction about the living body of the world.

It asks whether the human being can return from ownership to service.

## 10. Internal vs external language

The internal canon may say:

```text
Сотворив мир, Творец оставил народам инструменты и инструкции.
```

This is true to the author's inner metaphysical language.

But for institutions, grants, archives, universities and cultural organizations, this must be translated carefully.

External version:

```text
The project treats ancient epic and mythopoetic narratives as symbolic systems of transmission: image-based forms through which cultures preserved knowledge about world-order, human responsibility, measure, participation, transformation and right relation.
```

External comparative version:

```text
Alongside the Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related line, the project studies epic and mythopoetic traditions of other cultures with varying degrees of depth. This comparative layer does not flatten cultural differences, but observes how different peoples preserved related symbolic structures through their own languages, landscapes, images, rituals and narrative forms.
```

External artistic-research version:

```text
The project does not aim to prove ancient truth as a thesis. It tests whether literary form can make ancient images audible and intelligible again as lived experience.
```

Do not write externally in a way that sounds like:

```text
I can prove that all myths come from one source.
All religions and epics are the same.
The Kalevala is only one example of my universal system.
Ancient stories are literal technical manuals from God.
```

Better external stance:

```text
Different traditions are approached not as interchangeable myths, but as culturally specific languages of access to related questions of world-order, human responsibility, measure, participation and transformation.
```

## 11. Main dangers

### 11.1. Dead academic reduction

Do not reduce the project to:

```text
a study of mythological motifs
```

The motifs matter because they carry instruction.

### 11.2. Primitive literalism

Do not reduce the project to:

```text
ancient stories are exact technical reports
```

The truth of the stories is not dependent on proving them as literal chronology.

### 11.3. Universal flattening

Do not reduce all traditions to one undifferentiated system.

The root may be one or related, but the languages of access are different and must be respected.

### 11.4. Spiritual sermon

Do not let the novel become a sermon, doctrine or spiritual instruction manual.

The instruction must become scene.

### 11.5. Decorative folklore

Do not use Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related material as atmosphere.

The northern material is not decoration. It is the main spine of the current cycle.

### 11.6. Thesis-proof trap

Do not make the project sound like an attempt to prove ancient truth as a thesis.

Radiance tests form.

It asks whether the ancient instruction can become audible and intelligible again through literature.
