## 2. Core premise

The ancient stories about abundance machines did not describe merely lost objects.

They preserved images of a deeper principle:

```text
Creation itself is the Manna-machine.
```

Earth already feeds.

The human being does not need to invent abundance.

The human being needs to learn how not to break the living mechanism that already feeds him.

---

## 3. Mythopoetic instruction canon

Inside **Radiance**, ancient stories, epics, fairy tales and mythopoetic narratives are not treated as empty inventions, primitive explanations, decorative folklore or dead cultural material.

They are treated as image-based instructions.

They tell, in symbolic and narrative form:

```text
how it was,
how it is,
and how it should be.
```

Russian internal formula:

```text
Древние истории, в образной форме, рассказывают нам о том, как было, как есть и как должно быть.
```

The internal premise is that after the world was created, tools and instructions were left to all peoples.

These instructions were not left only as abstract laws, technical diagrams, academic explanations or philosophical systems. They were left in forms that could pass through memory, song, fear, wonder, ritual, childhood, fire, landscape and language.

They were left as stories, epics, fairy tales, songs, names, prohibitions, ordeals, sacred objects, impossible tasks, heroic failures, thresholds, monsters, helpers, gifts, warnings, repeated motifs and images that do not die when literal understanding is lost.

This is why myth can survive where doctrine collapses, and why a fairy tale can carry something a system cannot hold.

The project does not need to claim that every ancient story is a literal historical report.

The stronger internal position is:

```text
They are true not because they are chronicles.
They are true because they preserve law in image.
```

Russian:

```text
Они истинны не потому, что являются хроникой.
Они истинны потому, что сохраняют закон в образе.
```

Inside **Radiance**, the ancient image is not opposed to truth. The image is one of the ways truth survives time.

Core formula:

```text
Сказки и эпосы — это не ложь о прошлом.
Это правда, записанная на языке образов.
```

English internal formula:

```text
Fairy tales and epics are not lies about the past.
They are truth written in the language of images.
```

## 4. Why instruction becomes story

A direct instruction can be refused, forgotten, institutionalized, weaponized or reduced to a slogan.

A story works differently.

A story does not only inform the mind. It passes through the whole human being: memory, imagination, fear, hunger, shame, longing, rhythm, compassion, recognition, desire, bodily response, and the ability to see oneself without being directly accused.

The most important instructions were not always given as rules because rules can be obeyed without transformation.

A story can wait until a person becomes ready to understand it.

This is why **Radiance** does not try to turn myth into a dry system. The aim is not to extract a diagram from the story and throw the story away. The aim is to restore the working power of the image itself.

Internal formula:

```text
Мы не возвращаемся в древность.
Мы возвращаем древнему образу его рабочую силу.
```

English:

```text
We are not returning to antiquity.
We are returning the ancient image to its working force.
```

## 5. One root, many cultural languages

Alongside the Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related line, the author studies, with different degrees of depth, epics, fairy tales and ancient stories of different peoples.

The internal assumption is that many traditions may point toward one deep root or one related order, but this root was not given to all peoples in one flat, identical form.

Different peoples received different languages of access.

These languages were shaped by land, climate, work, fear, food, animals, memory, wounds, migrations, enemies, kinship systems, rituals, sound, instruments, local spirits and names, and the way each culture learned to hear the world.

Therefore, **Radiance** must not flatten all traditions into one interchangeable mythological soup.

The project does not say:

```text
All myths are the same.
All cultures say the same thing.
Differences do not matter.
```

The correct internal position is:

```text
Different peoples did not necessarily receive different truths.
They received different languages of access to truth.
```

Russian:

```text
Разные народы получили не разные истины, а разные языки доступа к истине.
```

The point is not to erase differences.

The point is to observe how a related inner order becomes visible through different cultural interfaces.

## 6. Finnish / Karelian priority

The cycle may compare different mythic traditions, but the Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related line remains the main northern spine.

Other traditions are mirrors and echoes.

They do not replace the northern entrance.

Correct proportion:

```text
Northern spine.
Comparative echoes.
One deep order.
Many cultural languages.
Living literature.
```

The Kalevala-related field is not used as a decorative grant element. It is not treated as costume, aesthetic flavor or exotic setting.

It is a living source field through which **Radiance** investigates how ancient images may still contain working instructions about abundance, language, craft, maturity, trauma, death, birth, forest, measure and responsibility.

## 7. Sampo

Sampo is the first entrance into the cycle.

Sampo is treated as:

- an image of abundance;
- an ancient technological-mythic symbol;
- a northern name for the principle of provision;
- a test of maturity;
- a mirror of the human desire to receive without participation;
- a fictional doorway into the deeper mechanism of Creation.

Internal term:

```text
Протокол Сампо / The Sampo Protocol
```

Public first-book title for now:

```text
Сампо
```

Sampo is not primarily a question of whether a miraculous machine existed.

Sampo is an instruction about abundance.

It teaches that provision cannot be separated from participation. The human being searches for an abundance machine while already standing inside the living world that feeds him.

Wrong question:

```text
Where is Sampo?
```

Deeper question:

```text
Why does the human being want to own the source while standing inside it?
```

## 8. Song / Песня

Song is not merely sound, performance, beauty or magic.

Song is an instruction about language and hearing.

It asks what happens when speech stops being noise, opinion and argument, and begins to participate in the order of reality.

Wrong question:

```text
What is the magical song?
```

Deeper question:

```text
What must change in the human being before word becomes tuning rather than noise?
```

## 9. Pantheons

Pantheons are approached as functional systems, not decorative collections of gods.

Internal authorial image:

```text
Pantheons as "landing teams" — groups of higher functions arriving into human culture to teach language, craft, law, ritual, death, power, desire, order and inner transformation.
```

Institutional / cautious formulation:

```text
Pantheons as symbolic systems of transmission: mythic ensembles through which cultures encoded models of perception, craft, language, power, death, desire and inner transformation.
```

The fiction may preserve ambiguity.

It should never flatten itself into a single explanation.

## 10. Book I opening location and Ivar

The first book may open in a remote northern house / gathering place / mini-camp somewhere in Finland, in forest, by water, or in another quiet northern landscape.

This is not "Ivar's house".

Correct current rule:

```text
Ivar himself comes there once a year.
The place is a temporary northern shelter for solitude, reflection, and the gathering of people close to him.
```

Ivar is not Finnish.

He is a person of the world:

```text
not a national representative
not a local folklore authority
not an institutional teacher
not a spiritual brand
```

He loves solitude and he loves his guests.

This tension matters:

```text
He withdraws from the world, but does not hate people.
He does not gather followers; he receives those who can still hear.
```

Current Book I gathering:

```text
12 guests + Ivar
```

This is not a direct Jesus-and-apostles composition.

The echo may exist under the surface, but must never become costume symbolism.

## 11. Opening door, not permanent frame

Important correction:

```text
The campfire is not the main frame of the whole cycle.
It is the opening door / hook of Book I.
```

Book I may begin with Ivar at a fire, starting the first story about the "real" history hidden inside northern myths.

But after the hook works, the narrative must pass into active fiction:

- heroes;
- events;
- choices;
- mythic figures;
- conflicts;
- consequences;
- inner laws revealed through action.

Ivar is a threshold figure.

He opens the first door; he does not hold every book hostage by the sleeve.

The fire may return later only if the story earns it.

## 12. Series movement

Book I begins with abundance.

Then the cycle opens through functions:

```text
Сампо       — abundance / Creation as Manna-machine
Песня       — word / song / reality tuning
Кузница     — craft / form / responsibility
Порог       — access / northern power / maturity
Сбой        — trauma / damaged script / false destiny
Возвращение — death / passion / return from dissolution
Дыхание     — birth / gestation / creation without violence
Лес         — Earth / beast / living mechanism / service
```

The sequence should feel like a path of initiation, but not a manual.

Current named spine has eight active book nodes. The final number may be expanded later.

Locations are not locked.

A later book may choose another place, such as a large room with a fireplace, if that place naturally serves the book's function.

## 13. Internal vs external language

The internal canon may say:

```text
Сотворив мир, Творец оставил народам инструменты и инструкции.
```

This is true to the author's inner metaphysical language.

But for institutions, grants, archives, universities and cultural organizations, this must be translated carefully.

External version:

```text
The project treats ancient epic and mythopoetic narratives as symbolic systems of transmission: image-based forms through which cultures preserved knowledge about world-order, human responsibility, measure, participation, transformation and right relation.
```

External comparative version:

```text
Alongside the Finnish / Karelian / Kalevala-related line, the project studies epic and mythopoetic traditions of other cultures with varying degrees of depth. This comparative layer does not flatten cultural differences, but observes how different peoples preserved related symbolic structures through their own languages, landscapes, images, rituals and narrative forms.
```

Do not write externally in a way that sounds like:

```text
I can prove that all myths come from one source.
All religions and epics are the same.
The Kalevala is only one example of my universal system.
Ancient stories are literal technical manuals from God.
```

Better external stance:

```text
Different traditions are approached not as interchangeable myths, but as culturally specific languages of access to related questions of world-order, human responsibility, measure, participation and transformation.
```

## 14. Main danger

The main danger is explanatory heaviness.

Do not let the project become:

```text
a lecture with mythological illustrations
```

The meaning must arrive through:

- scene;
- dialogue;
- rhythm;
- contradiction;
- silence;
- recognition;
- human and mythic action;
- the refusal to overexplain.

Do not rely on the campfire dialogue to carry every idea.

Do not turn the place into a guru estate.

Do not turn guest resistance into constant interruption.

Do not reduce the project to:

```text
ancient stories are exact technical reports
```

Do not reduce all traditions to one undifferentiated system.

Do not let the novel become a sermon, doctrine or spiritual instruction manual.

The instruction must become scene.
