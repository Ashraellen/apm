Mandatory micro-corrections:

1. Replace:

```text
the house had already woken, though no one had announced a rising.
```

with:

```text
the house had already woken, though no one had announced that it was time to get up.
```

Reason: `a rising` is too unnatural in English and briefly sounds like translated phrasing rather than living prose.

2. Replace:

```text
where the nail passed into the finger.
```

with:

```text
where the nail met the finger.
```

Reason: the current phrase is understandable but too calqued. The replacement keeps the bodily precision without awkwardness.

3. Replace:

```text
He wore a dark sweater, his hair braided in a long plait thrown behind his shoulder.
```

with:

```text
He wore a dark sweater, his hair in a long braid thrown back over his shoulder.
```

Reason: `plait` is slightly too British-marked and less consistent with the established Chapter 01 wording. `Braid` keeps continuity with Ivar’s canonical appearance.

4. Replace:

```text
A hunk would have felt too rustic or heavy, and slice preserves the chapter’s edible ordinariness.
```

This line is not in the prose body. No correction needed. Controller note only: `slice` is accepted as the chapter-wide rendering of `ломоть`; do not change the title or recurring term.

5. Replace the final line:

```text
Like everything living that a person had taken too long for a store.
```

with:

```text
Like everything living that a person had mistaken for a reserve for too long.
```

Reason: the current English line is grammatically and semantically unstable. It risks meaning `taken too long` as duration of action rather than `слишком долго принимал за запас`. `Mistaken for a reserve for too long` preserves the pressure and the `запас` logic without collapsing into a technical phrase.
