Mandatory micro-corrections:

1. Replace:

```text
Upon it one can build everything that the living thing had interfered with yesterday.
```

with:

```text
On it one can build everything that the living thing had interfered with yesterday.
```

Reason: `Upon it` is too elevated for this dry kitchen logic. `On it` keeps the physical/mental surface clearer.

2. Replace:

```text
The notebook was open not on the map.
```

with:

```text
The notebook was open, but not to the map.
```

Reason: current English is unnatural. The correction preserves the important fact that David is not reopening the map-line.

3. Replace:

```text
“Almost teaching.”
```

with:

```text
“Almost a lesson.”
```

Reason: Lina’s dry answer should sound natural enough for Nick’s `That’s worse` to land cleanly.

4. Replace:

```text
After dinner the rain grew stronger.
```

with:

```text
After lunch the rain grew stronger.
```

Reason: source `После обеда` here means after the midday meal/afternoon. `After dinner` shifts the day too late and conflicts with the later `By evening` movement.

5. Replace:

```text
“Not only paper.”
```

with:

```text
“Not only the paper.”
```

Reason: improves the dialogue while preserving David’s shift: the recipe is a pointer, not the whole authority.

6. Replace:

```text
Only the first belief that labour can be caught into sameness.
```

with:

```text
Only the first belief that labour can be caught in sameness.
```

Reason: `caught into sameness` is too grammatically strained. `Caught in sameness` keeps the strange pressure of the original without sounding like an error.

7. Replace:

```text
“Mix” was replaced with “join.”
```

with:

```text
“Mix” was replaced with “combine.”
```

Reason: `join` is too odd in English recipe context and pulls attention toward the translator’s choice. `Combine` preserves `соединить` while remaining believable as a recipe word.
