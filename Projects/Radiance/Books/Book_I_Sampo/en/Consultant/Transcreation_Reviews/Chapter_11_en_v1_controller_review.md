Main risk:
No structural problem. The chapter preserves the movement from bread as share/quantity into bread as deadline, dough, yeast, process, waiting, first rising, knocking down, second rising, baking, method, repetition, and the first dangerous wish that heavy living work might do itself. Bread is not merely food; it becomes a process that cannot be fully protocolized. Thomas wants correctness and tempo. David wants proportions and repeatability. Lina knows the process through hands. Mandatory micro-corrections:

1. Replace:

```text
how much of the water had to be warm, how much sugar was enough, how much time was not hers.
```

with:

```text
how warm the water had to be, how much sugar was enough, how much time was not hers.
```

Reason: `how much of the water had to be warm` sounds as if only part of the water is warm. The correction keeps the knowledge/measure distinction.

2. Replace:

```text
“It will be enough for as much as it will be enough.”
```

with:

```text
“It will be enough for however much it is enough for.”
```

Reason: the current version is too grammatically strained. The replacement keeps Lina’s maddening anti-number answer while sounding like possible English.

3. Replace:

```text
“You cook the yeast — you get stone.”
```

with:

```text
“Cook the yeast and you get stone.”
```

Reason: smoother Marek-English while preserving the severe literalness.

4. Replace:

```text
That is where the dog had rummaged, Sofia thought, and immediately grinned inwardly: even here, beside a bowl, a person needs the living thing to confirm his expectation step by step.
```

with:

```text
So that was where the dog had been digging, Sofia thought, and immediately grinned inwardly: even here, beside a bowl, a person needs the living thing to confirm his expectation step by step.
```

Reason: the line preserves the authorial idiom, but `rummaged` is too odd in English. `Had been digging` is still strange enough to carry the authorial flavour without sounding like a wrong verb.

5. Replace:

```text
Lina placed the two rounded blanks into tins, covered them with the towel, and set them again closer to the stove.
```

with:

```text
Lina placed the two rounded pieces of dough into tins, covered them with the towel, and set them again closer to the stove.
```

Reason: `blanks` is not natural here and may sound technical/industrial. The replacement remains concrete.

6. Replace:

```text
The second proofing was coming to an end.
```

with:

```text
The second rise was coming to an end.
```

Reason: `proofing` is technically correct but too bakery-technical for the surrounding literary texture. `Second rise` keeps the chapter title/body resonance.

7. Replace:

```text
“Did you think once?”
```

with:

```text
“Did you think it was only once?”
```

Reason: the current line is not natural English. The correction preserves Lina’s weary practical answer.

8. Replace:

```text
The house filled with something that could not yet be eaten, but could already not be not wanted.
```

with:

```text
The house filled with something that could not yet be eaten, but could no longer not be wanted.
```

Reason: the current double negative is too awkward. The replacement keeps `уже нельзя было не хотеть` without smoothing away the pressure.

9. Replace:

```text
David suddenly laughed.

Not hard.

But alive.
```

with:

```text
David suddenly laughed.

Not much.

But alive.
```

Reason: `not hard` is the wrong collocation for the Russian `не сильно` in this emotional context.
