Mandatory micro-corrections:

1. Replace:

```text
He set it near the edge of the table as if it were not drink, but another tool for a northern evening.
```

with:

```text
He set it near the edge of the table as if it were not a drink, but another tool for a northern evening.
```

Reason: `not drink` is unidiomatic here. The article fixes English naturalness without changing the object/tool logic.

2. Replace:

```text
“I thought if... if I corrected it. Not found. Not went farther. Just corrected it. So it would be right. But it still...”
```

with:

```text
“I thought if... if I corrected it. Not to find. Not to go farther. Just to correct it. So it would be right. But it still...”
```

Reason: the current English is grammatically broken. The replacement preserves David’s fragmented denial: not finding, not continuing, just correcting.

3. Replace:

```text
“What wrong way?” Nick asked, without mockery now.
```

with:

```text
“What went the wrong way?” Nick asked, without mockery now.
```

Reason: the current line is not natural English. The replacement keeps Nick’s suddenly non-mocking concern.

4. Replace:

```text
But Ivar did not look at him as at shame.

He looked as at a person who had returned.
```

with:

```text
But Ivar did not look at him as if he were shame itself.

He looked at him as one looks at a person who has returned.
```

Reason: the current version is too calqued. The replacement preserves the crucial ethical distinction: David is not material for shame; he is a person who has returned.

5. Replace:

```text
They did not know how, and should not, sit like in a temple around someone else’s nausea.
```

with:

```text
They did not know how, and should not have known how, to sit as if in a temple around someone else’s nausea.
```

Reason: the current phrase is syntactically awkward. The correction keeps the anti-ritual, anti-beautification logic.

6. Replace:

```text
“Because I am afraid of improving something again.”
```

with:

```text
“Because I am afraid of ruining their event.”
```

Reason: the current line changes the meaning and loses Nick’s source-based joke about the ants’ `мероприятие`. The correction restores the ant-business humor and avoids over-intellectualizing the line.

7. Replace:

```text
The earth does not skip material just because a person has called it failure.
```

with:

```text
The earth does not pass over material just because a person has called it failure.
```

Reason: `skip material` sounds too technical in English. `Pass over material` preserves the sense that earth does not exclude what a human being calls failure.

8. Replace:

```text
“You will look who wants it.”
```

with:

```text
“You will look to see who wants it.”
```

Reason: the current line is ungrammatical in English. The replacement preserves Ivar’s minimal instruction: do not only look at the map/desire, look at the one who wants to close it.
