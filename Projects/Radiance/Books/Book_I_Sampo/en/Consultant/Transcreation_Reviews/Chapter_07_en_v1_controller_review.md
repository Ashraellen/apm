Mandatory micro-corrections:

1. Replace:

```text
The sleeves no longer dripped, but the fabric still held water inside, like an offence that decent people do not show before guests.
```

with:

```text
The sleeves no longer dripped, but the fabric still held water inside, like a grievance decent people do not show in front of guests.
```

Reason: `offence` risks the wrong idiomatic meaning in English. `Grievance` is closer to `обида` and keeps the dry humanization of the wet fabric.

2. Replace:

```text
“Then draw the house. A stick from it. Then mud. Then another stick. Then back. Done.”
```

with:

```text
“Then draw the house. A stick coming out of it. Then mud. Then another stick. Then back. Done.”
```

Reason: `A stick from it` is too clipped even for Marek’s dry speech. The replacement keeps the crude primitive-map joke without sounding like an English error.

3. Replace:

```text
Every thing on the paper received a place. Every place — a name. Every name — the possibility of being connected with another.
```

with:

```text
Each thing on the paper received a place. Each place — a name. Each name — the possibility of being connected with another.
```

Reason: `Every thing` is visually and rhythmically awkward in English. `Each` preserves the step-by-step reduction into map logic.

4. Replace:

```text
“The transfer of the instrument has taken place.”
```

with:

```text
“The transfer of the tool has taken place.”
```

Reason: `instrument` is a little too stiff and can sound musical/surgical. `Tool` keeps Nick’s procedural irony and the concrete pencil-as-object pressure.

5. Replace:

```text
“No. You are not so cold like this.”
```

with:

```text
“No. This way you are not so cold.”
```

Reason: the current line is understandable but not natural English. The replacement preserves the old woman’s crucial anti-map correction: the board does not reveal truth; it makes cold easier to bear.
