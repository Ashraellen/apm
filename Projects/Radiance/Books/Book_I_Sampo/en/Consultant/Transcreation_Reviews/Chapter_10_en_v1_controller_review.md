Mandatory micro-corrections:

1. Replace:

```text
if one begins weighing every work, half of what is necessary will have to stop being done.
```

with:

```text
if one begins weighing every piece of work, half of what is necessary will have to stop being done.
```

Reason: `every work` is not natural English in this context. The correction keeps the labour/accounting logic without sounding like a translation artifact.

2. Replace:

```text
It smelled of berries, a wet towel, an unwashed plate, and water, which two people had gone to fetch.
```

with:

```text
It smelled of berries, a wet towel, an unwashed plate, and water two people had gone to fetch.
```

Reason: removes the unnecessary relative-clause stiffness and keeps the sentence closer to the physical kitchen rhythm.

3. Replace:

```text
How much to suffer, so they release you from the bucket.
```

with:

```text
How much to suffer, so as to be released from the bucket.
```

Reason: the current `they release you` has an unclear subject and sounds too casual. The replacement preserves the moral-accounting pressure of the bucket line.

4. Replace:

```text
“I wanted honestly.”
```

with:

```text
“I wanted it to be honest.”
```

Reason: the current English is ungrammatical. The replacement preserves David’s defensive intention without over-explaining it.

5. Replace the following sequence:

```text
Honestly to the map.

Honestly to the work.

Honestly to the water.

Honestly to the bread.

Honestly to every piece that would fall into a hand.
```

with:

```text
Honest to the map.

Honest to the work.

Honest to the water.

Honest to the bread.

Honest to every piece that would fall into a hand.
```

Reason: `Honestly to...` is too grammatically awkward in English. The adjective form keeps the elliptical pressure and the repeated danger of `честно` becoming a path toward counting.

6. Replace:

```text
“Then I cut,” Lina said.
```

with:

```text
“Then I’ll cut,” Lina said.
```

Reason: the current phrase is too unnatural in English. The correction keeps Lina taking the cutting authority without making her line sound broken.

7. Replace:

```text
A piece knows how to be mine.
```

with:

```text
A piece knows how to be mine.
```

No textual change required. Controller note: keep this line exactly as it is. It is intentionally strange and central to the chapter.

8. Replace:

```text
She felt unpleasant from this precision.
```

with:

```text
This precision felt unpleasant to her.
```

Reason: the current phrase is not idiomatic English. The correction preserves Sofia’s discomfort with the accuracy of `my piece`.
