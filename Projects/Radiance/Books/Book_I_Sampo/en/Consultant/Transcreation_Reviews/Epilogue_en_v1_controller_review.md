Main risk:
No structural problem. The epilogue correctly refuses summary, explanation, and solemnity. Mandatory micro-corrections:

1. Replace:

```text
someone opened and closed the door to the entryway, cups, a knife, cloth, crumbs, paper moved across the table.
```

with:

```text
someone opened and closed the door to the entryway; cups, a knife, cloth, crumbs, and paper moved across the table.
```

Reason: punctuation and `and` make the list readable while preserving the house’s ordinary work.

2. Replace:

```text
Then a case with something long inside, which someone set against the wall and immediately moved so it would not be in the way.
```

with:

```text
Then a case with something long inside, which someone set against the wall and immediately moved so it would not get in the way.
```

Reason: more natural English.

3. Replace:

```text
if the earth went away, it would not change the fact that the hand had held the board.
```

with:

```text
if the earth came away, it would not change the fact that the hand had held the board.
```

Reason: more tactile English for washed-off earth while preserving the hand/board continuity.

4. Replace:

```text

```

with:

```text

```

Reason: in this physical context `mud` is stronger and more natural than repeated `dirt`.

5. Replace:

```text

```

with:

```text
Glove separately. Bread separately. Soul uncertain.
```

Reason: keeps Nick’s odd comic grammar but avoids the awkward adverb.

6. Replace:

```text

```

with:

```text

```

Reason: still childlike and simple, but clearer in English. Do not make it adult or aphoristic.

7. Replace:

```text

```

with:

```text

```

Reason: more natural and keeps the movement of the ditch water.

8. Replace:

```text

```

with:

```text

```

Reason: keep vehicle naming consistent with the existing `minibus` image. Marek’s later dialogue `Get in the van` may remain idiomatic.

9. Replace:

```text

```

with:

```text
She stood by the door with the closed case in one hand.
```

Reason: small naturalness correction; keeps the case tactile and indirect.
