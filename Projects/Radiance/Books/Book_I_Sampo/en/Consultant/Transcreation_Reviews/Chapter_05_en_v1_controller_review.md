Main risk:
No structural problem. The chapter preserves the movement from surface/water/vessel into thingness, storage, emptiness, and the dangerous relief of making Sampo into something that can be sought, placed, hidden, lost, or found. Things remain things first: hook, mug, shelf, bench, nails, pantry, wooden box, chest, bowls, keys, lamps, lids, rope, towel, rain. The pressure comes from how things hold, wait, contain, and make emptiness bearable. Sampo is not solved as an object; the chapter shows the danger of letting rumor become a thing.

Mandatory micro-corrections:

1. Replace:

```text
The lid of some jar lying separately, like a person who had lost his reason.
```

with:

```text
A jar lid lying by itself, like a person who had lost his reason for being there.
```

Reason: `lost his reason` in English risks meaning `lost his sanity`. The Russian `потерявший свою причину` is stranger and more existential; the replacement preserves that without triggering the wrong idiom.

2. Replace:

```text
No note, no key, no old ring, no hidden object that could have begun a new line of plot with indecent directness.
```

with:

```text
No note, no key, no old ring, no hidden object that could have begun some indecently direct new thread of events.
```

Reason: `line of plot` creates a direct meta-trace in English. The Russian source does use `линию сюжета`, but English `plot` inside narration is too exposed for the controller protocol. The replacement preserves the irony and the sense of an overly obvious narrative turn without leaking external book mechanics.

3. Replace:

```text
“Do you hear? After the chapter of water, we have been issued bowls and a box. The world has stopped even pretending to be subtle.”
```

with:

```text
“Do you hear? After the water business, we have been issued bowls and a box. The world has stopped even pretending to be subtle.”
```

Reason: source-based, but `chapter of water` in English creates avoidable meta-trace risk because this is literally Chapter 05. `Water business` keeps Nick’s comic irritation and avoids external chapter mechanics.

4. Replace:

```text
“That empty is a content.”
```

with:

```text
“That emptiness is content.”
```

Reason: `a content` is not natural English here. The replacement keeps Nora’s odd precision and the philosophical pressure of `пустое — это содержание` without sounding like an ESL artifact.
