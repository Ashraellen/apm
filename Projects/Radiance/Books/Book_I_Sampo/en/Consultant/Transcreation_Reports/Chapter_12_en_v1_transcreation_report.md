## Transcreation approach

The English draft was created as literary transcreation preserving the chapter’s movement from bread as cooled proof into recipe, writing, memory, living continuation, the jar of yesterday’s dough, and the first explicit danger of repeatability.

The core pressure: after Chapter 11’s rising/waiting, Chapter 12 shows what happens when a successful living process cools into evidence. Thomas wants a usable recipe; David wants a record without turning it into a map; Lina, Eva, Nora, Marek, Ivar, and Sofia keep pointing toward the part of knowledge that cannot remain on paper. The Sampo danger advances: not machine yet, not system yet, but the first belief that labour can be caught into sameness.

## Key choices

- **Title:** `Глава двенадцатая. Рецепт` rendered as **Chapter Twelve. Recipe**. Kept plain and functional; no article, no metaphorical elevation.
- `На следующий день хлеб остыл` rendered as **“The next day the bread had cooled.”** Keeps the chapter’s immediate physical change and continuation from Chapter 11.
- `хлеб перестал быть чудом ожидания и стал доказательством` rendered as **“The bread had stopped being a miracle of waiting and had become proof.”** Central transition.
- `Получилось` rendered as **“It had worked.”** Chosen over `It turned out` because this line opens the dangerous logic of repeatability.
- Empty place near the stove rendered as **“An empty place is always more convenient for thought.”** Preserves the abstract pressure becoming physical.
- Thomas’s flour interrogation preserved through **enough / about enough**, keeping Lina’s refusal of quantitative capture.
- Nick’s line rendered as **“The morning has begun with a crime against precision.”** Keeps bureaucratic-comic register.
- `допрашивать вчерашний день` rendered as **“to interrogate yesterday.”** Important comic/epistemic hinge.
- `Когда хлеб остыл, его можно было изучать` rendered as **“When bread cooled, it could be studied.”** Central danger line.
- David’s `возможно` scene preserved with Nick’s gloss: **“yes, but I’m ashamed.”** Keeps David’s awkward self-awareness and Nick’s social scalpel.
- David’s counter-line to Nick preserved: irony as a way of not participating while participating anyway. Important mutual growth from earlier chapters.
- `Пока тесто взяло` rendered as **“As much as the dough took.”** Kept intentionally bodily and strange; controller should check whether this needs `as much as the dough would take`.
- Lina’s `Вчера оно тебя слышало?` rendered as **“Did it hear you yesterday?”** Keeps dough as living participant without over-mystifying it.
- David’s failed note **“Flour — enough”** preserved, then crossed out.
- `Первый подъём был сколько? / До первого подъёма` rendered as **“How long was the first rise? / Until the first rise.”** Preserves Lina’s maddening memory-answer.
- Lina showing her hands rendered as **“This one’s.”** Slightly strange, intentionally bodily.
- `они не отделяли знание от участия` rendered as **“they did not separate knowledge from participation.”** Key line.
- Eva’s line `пока не начнёт держать себя` rendered as **“until it begins to hold itself.”** Central embodied instruction.
- `неточно, зато правда` rendered as **“imprecise, but true.”** Preserves the chapter’s central opposition.
- Nora’s `Он не запомнит руки` rendered as **“It won’t remember the hands.”** Keeps the recipe as a not-quite-living subject.
- Recipe’s function as description preserved: **“It is supposed to describe an action.” / “There.”** Nora’s minimal strike retained.
- `бумага не вмещает всё` rendered as **“paper could not contain everything.”** Important shift in David.
- Notebook compromise preserved: Thomas may see, David does not let go. `Не доверие. / Но уже не крышка.` rendered as **“Not trust. / But already not a lid.”** Keeps Chapter 10–11 notebook ownership pressure.
- `Это невозможно использовать` rendered as **“This cannot be used.”** Thomas’s practical fear.
- Lina’s **“With hands.”** kept as the simplest possible answer.
- Sofia’s line `ты против того, что они не остаются на бумаге` rendered as **“You are against the fact that they don’t stay on paper.”** Central.
- Eva’s `иначе каждый раз начинаешь с нуля` rendered as **“Otherwise every time you begin from zero.”** Keeps the real usefulness of writing; the chapter does not demonize the recipe.
- Marek’s transferability line rendered as **“You simply ask as if the answer is obliged to be transferable.”** Strong but slightly abstract; controller should review register.
- Ivar’s line `Бумага не поднимается` rendered as **“Paper does not rise.”** Must remain blunt and minimal.
- `бумага лежит и не дышит` rendered as **“paper lies there and does not breathe.”** Keeps object-body contrast.
- The jar scene preserves the small piece of yesterday’s dough as **not beautiful / grey-beige / damp / forgotten remnant** rather than turning it into charming sourdough imagery.
- `Это уже знает вчера` rendered as **“This already knows yesterday.”** Strange, but essential; controller should decide whether `remembers yesterday` would be too soft/logical.
- David’s epistemology objection preserved: **“Smell is not knowledge.” / “For you — no.”** Key Lina line.
- Nick’s line rendered as **“an argument between epistemology and sour dough.”** Keeps the comic-intellectual collision.
- `Значит, требует ухода` rendered as **“Then it requires care.”** Central living/unreliable correction.
- The notebook vs living piece contrast preserved: paper does not sour, ask for warmth, require feeding, or die of forgetfulness.
- `Не сам по себе. / Не без человека. / Не как кнопка.` rendered as **“Not by itself. / Not without a person. / Not like a button.”** Important anti-automation nuance.
- Nora’s feeding question preserved, and `не забывать` rendered as **“not forgetting.”** Kept as a slightly awkward recipe item because the source makes it become a point in the recipe.
- The recipe list preserved line-by-line, with `Печь — пока корка скажет` rendered as **“Bake — until the crust says.”** Intentionally strange; echoes Chapter 11.
- Thomas’s document-power line rendered as **“like a person who had received a document, but had not received power.”** Central bureaucratic/satirical pressure.
- David’s answer `Чтобы помнить, где спрашивать` rendered as **“So we remember where to ask.”** Important reconciliation: recipe as pointer, not possession.
- Nick’s diagnosis rendered as **“cartographic illness to bread uncertainty, with signs of recovery and complications.”** Keeps continuity from map disease.
- Sofia’s vision keeps stone, grain, notches, and the normal question: **“If we repeat the same number of times, will the same amount come out?”**
- `первaя вера в то, что труд можно поймать в одинаковость` rendered as **“the first belief that labour can be caught into sameness.”** Central Sampo-danger line; controller should review `sameness` but it preserves the pressure.
- Nick’s warning preserved: **“The most dangerous things usually begin with a normal question.”**
- Nora’s `Нормальный не значит маленький` rendered as **“Normal does not mean small.”** Simple and strong.
- Lina’s repetition line rendered as **“What is bad is not repetition. / When you think repetition belongs to you.”** Core ethical hinge.
- `фраза могла бы стать слишком большой` rendered as **“The phrase could have become too large.”** Preserves narrative self-restraint.
- David’s map confession preserved: **“I thought the same thing about the map.”** No self-accusation, stronger because of dryness.
- Rain non-repetition paragraph preserved as sensory argument, not explanation.
- Nick’s `recipe for rain` line preserved with **“wind — until it holds itself”**, echoing dough.
- Final recipe-copying scene preserves **clean page**, **margins for clarifications**, **Eva’s resistance to numbers**, **Lina correcting only words that lead too far away from the hand**.
- `Смешать` → `соединить` rendered as **“Mix” was replaced with “join.”** This is deliberately literal; controller may consider `combine`, but `join` keeps the living/relational sense.
- Nora’s line `Потому что кусок у печи — нет` rendered as **“Because the piece by the stove hasn’t.”** Preserves contrast between neat recipe and untidy living piece.
- Lina’s `Это завтра` rendered as **“That is tomorrow.”** Key line: paper cannot do tomorrow today.
- Recipe page described as an object that can be folded, kept, passed on, hidden, copied. Preserved.
- `В ней лежало не обещание. / Обязанность.` rendered as **“In it lay not a promise. / An obligation.”** Central final contrast.
- Ivar’s final answer `Забыть, что записал не всё` rendered as **“Forgetting that you did not write everything down.”** Keeps the chapter’s moral without explaining it.
- Final jar/paper contrast preserved: paper asks for nothing; jar must be opened, smelled, fed, warmed, and waited for again.
- Final line rendered as **“And that which had to rise again stood by the stove under a cloth.”** Keeps the living continuation and chapter closure.

## Controller focus

Please review especially:

1. **“As much as the dough took.”** — source-faithful but strange; possible alternative: `as much as the dough would take`.
2. **“This already knows yesterday.”** — deliberately strange; possible alternative `This already remembers yesterday`, but that may soften Lina’s bodily logic.
3. **“the answer is obliged to be transferable.”** — conceptually accurate; check whether it sounds too academic for Marek.
4. **“the first belief that labour can be caught into sameness.”** — central but unusual; check whether `caught in sameness` or `captured as sameness` would be better.
5. **“Mix” → “join.”** — chosen to preserve `соединить`; controller may prefer `combine` if `join` feels too odd in recipe context.
6. **“Not trust. / But already not a lid.”** — literal and strange, but preserves the notebook ownership arc.
7. **“That is tomorrow.”** — should remain simple and final; avoid over-explaining.
