## Transcreation approach

The English draft was created as literary transcreation preserving the chapter’s pressure: the immediate continuation from the fire-circle ending of Chapter 01, the deepening of Sofia’s perception, the ambiguity of hearing/rumor, and the first embodied vision of the seven before the taking of Sampo.

The chapter keeps the movement from rational explanation into attention, then from attention into participatory vision. The English stays close to the Russian architecture: short pressure lines, object-world density, physical transitions through fire, snow, bread smell, hands, earth under the nail, and warm moss.

---

## Key choices

- **Title:** `Глава вторая. Тёплый снег` rendered as **Chapter Two. Warm Snow**.
- **Sampo** remains `Sampo`, untranslated and unexplained.
- `Сначала услышать` rendered as **“First, hear.”** This preserves the command’s simplicity and the hearing motif.
- `Сначала был слух` rendered as **“At first there was a rumor.”** This chooses the rumor/word-of-mouth sense of `слух` while keeping proximity to the earlier hearing language.
- `Слух о Сампо?` rendered as **“A rumor about Sampo?”**.
- `О достатке` rendered as **“About enough.”** This avoids the misleading weight of “wealth” and keeps the moral pressure of sufficiency / enoughness.
- `Для сытых — да` rendered as **“For the well-fed — yes.”** Kept blunt, not softened.
- `Пустые руки` rendered as **“Empty hands.”** Repeated as a structural image.
- `человеку стало легче сложить в себе будущую беду` rendered as **“it became easier for the man to assemble future misfortune inside himself.”** Strange but faithful to the internal construction of justification.
- The old woman’s line `Место, которое кормит...` rendered as **“The place that feeds... is recognized by the fact that after you it can still feed.”** Preserves the ethical center without explanation.
- `Север не делает человеку таких подарков...` rendered as **“The north does not give a person such gifts... It gives just enough for denial to become work.”** This keeps the cold precision of the Russian line.
- Final image preserved: **“Like bread not yet broken.”**

## Places requiring controller attention

- Check whether **“At first there was a rumor”** is the best English solution for `Сначала был слух`, given that the chapter also leans on hearing/listening.
- Check whether **“About enough”** carries `О достатке` accurately, or whether **“About having enough”** would be clearer without weakening the line.
- Check whether **“the well-fed”** is acceptable for `сытых`, or whether **“those who are full”** should be used for a more bodily tone.
- Check whether **“a defence of logic”** is the right rendering of `защита физики`; it preserves the functional meaning, but the Russian word is more deliberately odd.
- Check whether **“the story would not receive its end”** is acceptable for `рассказ не получит конца`; it is slightly strange in English but keeps the inner-world phrasing.
- Check whether **“the living could be hooked”** is strong and accurate enough for `живое можно зацепить`; it preserves the iron-hook logic, but may feel sharp in English.
- Check whether repeated **“story”** uses are authorized, since this chapter explicitly concerns Ivar telling a story and not external book mechanics.

---

## Known risks

- **Volume:** preserved; English expands only where needed for natural literary rhythm.
- **Humor:** minimal in this chapter; Nick’s few lines remain dry and defensive rather than sitcom-like.
- **Sampo canon:** preserved; Sampo remains a pressure and recognition problem, not an artifact with explanation.
- **Ivar rule:** preserved; Ivar does not become POV, teacher, or guru. He speaks sparingly, allows seeing, and stops the session by moving people back toward the house.
- **Meta-trace risk:** controlled; `story`, `reader`, and related terms are used where the Russian itself uses `рассказ` / narrative language inside the fire-circle context. Controller should still audit these.
- **English smoothness:** some strange lines are intentionally kept strange: `About enough`, `the story would be poorer`, `denial to become work`, `the living could be hooked`.
