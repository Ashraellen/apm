## 1. Chapter function preserved

Chapter 20 follows directly from the loss in Chapter 19. It does not erase the loss; it introduces a gift that makes the soup better without making the fish return.

The chapter movement is preserved:

1. Soup begins not because it is time, but because there is nothing more to wait for.
2. Three mushrooms and a few berries are not enough to justify the water.
3. The characters confront fairness before the ladle.
4. David refuses to count ahead again.
5. Matti arrives with potatoes, bread, and a jar from Laura.
6. The gift brings joy too quickly, making Sofia uncomfortable.
7. Thomas tries to turn the gift into a debt or rule.
8. Matti refuses that frame: there was extra; eat it warm.
9. The soup changes materially, but not magically.
10. The distribution remains unequal, practical, and relational.
11. The final insight: one cannot count on the gift, but one must not refuse it.
12. The soup remains thin, but something floats in every bowl.

No structural changes recommended.

## 2. Title decision

Russian:

```text
Глава двадцатая. Дар
```

Chosen English:

```text
Chapter Twenty. Gift
```

Rationale:

`Gift` is plain and conceptually open. `The Gift` would sound more narrative/event-like and might over-mark Matti’s arrival as a singular miracle. The chapter is not about one object, but about gift as a difficult mode of relation: it arrives, cannot be counted on, cannot be paid back immediately, and still must be accepted.

Recommendation: keep `Gift`, unless controller prefers idiomatic title rhythm with `The Gift`.

## 3. Central chapter pressure

Central pressure:

```text
A gift does not cancel loss; it changes what can be done with what remains.
```

The draft preserves Sofia’s discomfort: joy arrives too quickly after grief over the lost fish, and this feels almost like betrayal.

## 5. Character voice notes

### Sofia

Sofia experiences uncomfortable joy. The potato does not have the right to correct the evening, and still it becomes easier. This is the chapter’s emotional core.

### Thomas

Thomas tries to pre-calculate fairness, payment, distribution, tomorrow, and debt. By the end he can almost accept that one cannot count on gifts, but must still eat when given.

### Lina

Lina remains action. She does not spiritualize the gift. She washes, cuts, puts in the pot, distributes by eyes, saves potatoes for tomorrow, covers bread for morning.

### Matti

Matti must remain almost brutally practical. He does not give charity; he delivers what Laura had extra and says `Eat it warm.`

### Nick

Nick restrains himself several times because words could spoil the gift. His jokes come back gradually through work and food.

### David

David writes only `gift —`, then closes the notebook. Later he refuses to write because he is eating. This is a strong reversal of the notebook habit.

### Nora

Nora asks the future-facing question: what if someone comes tomorrow? She has learned food can move unpredictably.

### Eva

Eva keeps everyone grounded through bread, rag, floor, eating, and one loud true laugh at the cucumber line.
