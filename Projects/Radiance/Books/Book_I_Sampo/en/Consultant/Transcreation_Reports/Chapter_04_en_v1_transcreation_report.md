## Transcreation approach

The English draft was created as literary transcreation preserving the chapter’s shift from bread/slice/reserve into water/surface/vessel. The chapter stays grounded in ordinary actions: cleaning after breakfast, fastening a jacket, carrying a chipped mug, walking the damp path, scooping water, holding it in the palm, watching a drop cling to a damaged place, seeing a nail on a board.

The main task was to keep water from becoming an explained symbol. Water remains water: lake, surface, cold, depth, reflection, a mugful, a palmful, a vanishing drop. The ethical pressure emerges through form, containment, holding, losing, and the difference between scooping and taking away.

## Key choices

- **Title:** `Глава четвёртая. Поверхность` rendered as **Chapter Four. Surface**.
- `К воде вышли не сразу` rendered as **“They did not go to the water at once.”** Kept simple and bodily.
- `дом не любил резких переходов` rendered as **“the house did not like abrupt transitions.”** Preserves the living-house feel without over-mystifying.
- `возвращала дереву его собственную поверхность` rendered as **“returning its own surface to the wood.”** Maintains the surface motif directly.
- `изображал связь` rendered as **“at least imitate signal”** to keep the humor around David’s phone.
- Nora’s mug: `кружка` rendered as **mug** throughout, not “cup,” to preserve the practical object quality and enamel specificity.
- `Не для запаса... Для воды` rendered as **“Not for reserve... For water.”** Keeps the reserve/store logic from Chapter 03.
- `тропа... не столько ведёт, сколько соглашается` rendered as **“does not so much lead as agree to be walked on.”** Strange but faithful to the path’s agency.
- `Поверхность была ровной. Почти.` rendered as **“The surface was even. Almost.”** Keeps the title pressure.
- `Зачерпнуть можно. Забрать — нет.` rendered as **“You may scoop. You may not take away.”** Chosen to preserve the distinction between taking water temporarily and possessing/removing it.
- `Кружку?` rendered as **“The mug?”** to keep Ivar’s corrective minimalism.
- `вода... приняла форму кружки` rendered as **“water that had accepted the shape of the mug.”** Preserves the form-not-possession logic.
- `Ладонь берёт ненадолго` rendered as **“The palm takes for a short while.”** Direct, simple, and central.
- `Она просто была водой` rendered as **“It was simply water.”** Preserves Sofia’s recognition that the lake does not perform signs for humans.
- `сосуд` rendered as **vessel** in Sofia’s question to preserve broader resonance beyond mug/container.
- `Через потерю` rendered as **“Through loss.”** Kept stark and exact.
- `В нём озеро стало местом. А здесь оно было присутствием.` rendered as **“In it the lake had become a place. Here it was a presence.”** Preserves the image/photograph distinction.
- `Иногда начало...` rendered as **“Sometimes the beginning is not when a person takes. It is when he checks whether he can throw.”** Kept clear and restrained.
- Final line `Не унесённое` rendered as **“Not carried away.”** Chosen over “not taken away” for rhythm and finality.

## Places requiring controller attention

- Check whether **Chapter Four. Surface** is the right title, or whether **The Surface** would be better. Current choice mirrors the clean Russian title and keeps abstraction open.
- Check whether **“at least imitate signal”** is natural enough for `изображал связь`, or whether **“pretend to have signal”** would be more idiomatic.
- Check whether **“the house did not like abrupt transitions”** preserves the living-house quality without sounding too whimsical.
- Check whether **“returning its own surface to the wood”** is acceptable; it is strange but important for the chapter motif.
- Check whether **“does not so much lead as agree to be walked on”** keeps the path’s agency without becoming too mannered.
- Check whether **“You may scoop. You may not take away.”** is the best solution for `Зачерпнуть можно. Забрать — нет.` Alternatives: **“You can scoop. You cannot take away.”** / **“You may draw water. You may not take it away.”**
- Check whether **“accepted the shape of the mug”** is correct for `приняла форму кружки`; it preserves the living water logic but may sound slightly elevated.
- Check whether **“The palm takes for a short while”** should become **“The palm only takes for a short while.”** Current version is more Ivar-like and less explanatory.
- Check whether **“the surface gathered itself again”** preserves `Поверхность собралась снова` without over-personifying.
- Check whether **“In it the lake had become a place. Here it was a presence.”** is acceptable or too abstract in English.
- Check whether **“For the source not to leave between the fingers”** is natural enough for `Чтобы источник не уходил между пальцами`; it is deliberately strange.
- Check final line **“Not carried away.”** against the title/logic of `забрать / унести`; it may be preferable to render as **“Not taken away.”** if the controller wants stronger lexical continuity.

## Known risks

- **Volume:** preserved; English expands slightly where needed for natural literary flow.
- **Humor:** preserved through Nick, Marek, Nora, Eva, and David, but kept quieter after the pressure of Chapters 02–03.
- **Sampo canon:** preserved; no reduction of lake/water into a symbol or doctrine. The logic remains surface, vessel, taking, holding, losing, and the wish for something capable of holding.
- **Ivar rule:** preserved; Ivar remains action-based and concise. He touches water, throws a stone, shows the palm, and answers briefly without becoming a lecturer.
- **Objectness:** preserved; mug, chip, drop, nail, boat, phone photo, roots, moss, and water remain physical objects first.
- **Meta-trace risk:** controlled; no external book/chapter/reader language added. The only abstract phrasing is source-driven and internal to Sofia’s perception.
- **English smoothness:** several constructions are intentionally a little strange to preserve the authorial pressure: `returning its own surface to the wood`, `the path... agree to be walked on`, `the surface gathered itself again`, `For the source not to leave between the fingers`.
