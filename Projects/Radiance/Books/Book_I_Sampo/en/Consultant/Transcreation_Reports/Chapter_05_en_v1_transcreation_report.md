## Transcreation approach

The English draft was created as literary transcreation preserving the chapter’s movement from surface/water/vessel into thingness, storage, emptiness, and the dangerous relief of making Sampo into something that can be sought, placed, hidden, lost, or found.

The chapter keeps objects as objects first: hooks, mugs, shelves, benches, nails, a pantry, a wooden box, a chest, bowls, keys, lamps, lids, rope, rain, towel. The pressure comes from how things hold, wait, contain, and make emptiness bearable.

---

## Key choices

- **Title:** `Глава пятая. Вещь` rendered as **Chapter Five. The Thing**.
- `вещь` rendered mostly as **thing**, not consistently as “object.” This preserves the bluntness and danger of the Russian word.
- `предмет` rendered as **object** where the Russian shifts from `вещь` to `предмет`, especially in `Так слух становился предметом` → **“That is how a rumor became an object.”**
- `She felt the number of things` preserves `она почувствовала количество вещей` without smoothing it into “she noticed many things.”
- `not as a symbol / worse / as a thing` preserves the core ontological pressure of the opening.
- Nora’s mug remains **enamel mug**, with `blue rim` and `chip` carried through for continuity with Chapter 04.
- `держал / держала / держали` rendered as **held** in the early sequence: **The hook held. / The mug held. / The shelf held. / The bench held.**
- `не отпускали сразу` rendered as **“they did not let go at once.”** Preserves contrast with palm/water.
- `коробка с гвоздями... мелкие злые мысли` rendered as **“a box of nails... small evil thoughts.”** Keeps the dry animate-object menace.
- Nick’s line `После главы воды...` rendered as **“After the chapter of water...”** This is source-based character speech but may require controller attention for meta-trace risk.
- `панtry / кладовая`: rendered as **pantry**, not storeroom, to keep domestic specificity.
- `вещи... перестали удивляться людям` rendered as **“things... had stopped being surprised by people.”** Preserves source-based personification.
- The small box is kept ordinary but charged; `suspected of an answer` preserves the Russian `подозревают в ответе`.
- `Пустота лежала на дне спокойно` rendered as **“Emptiness lay calmly at the bottom.”** Keeps emptiness physical.
- `пустое — это содержание` rendered as **“empty is a content.”** Deliberately strange; controller should check.
- `Она хорошо хранится` rendered as **“It keeps well.”** Keeps Ivar’s dry line and the storage motif.
- `Слух редко остаётся слухом` rendered as **“A rumor rarely remains a rumor.”** Preserves Chapter 02 continuity.
- `Сначала слышат. Потом ищут, что держать.` rendered as **“First they hear. Then they look for something to hold.”** Core chapter logic.
- `It is there` for `Там есть оно` keeps the shift from place into an `it` that can become a thing.
- `Find it first. Then you can be kind.` preserves the old woman’s severe moral compression.
- `the form of a thing he had not yet seen` preserves `форма вещи, которой он ещё не видел`.
- `The rumor began to grow heavy` preserves `слух стал тяжелеть`.
- `That is how a rumor became an object` uses **object** because Russian uses `предмет`, not `вещь`, and this marks the conceptual shift.
- `If Sampo is a thing, it is simpler` preserves Thomas’s dangerous relief around `проще`.
- `Not the same` for `Не то же самое` preserves Ivar’s minimal correction.
- `the third word` remains unsaid but heard; this preserves the sequence `искать / найти / [взять]`.
- `wood with emptiness inside` preserves the bowl as made form rather than symbol.
- Final: **“It had received a place where it could wait.”** preserves the waiting-place logic of the empty bowl under the towel.

## Places requiring controller attention

- Check title **Chapter Five. The Thing**. It is direct and faithful, but controller may prefer **A Thing** if the chapter’s focus is less definite.
- Check whether **thing** versus **object** is distributed correctly. Current approach: `вещь` = thing, `предмет` = object.
- Check the line **“She felt the number of things.”** It is faithful but strange.
- Check **“the lid of some jar lying separately, like a person who had lost his reason.”** `reason` is used for `причина`; “purpose” may be more natural but weaker.
- Check **“The mug is still continuing the event.”** It preserves Nick’s joke but may need naturalization.
- Check source-based meta-risk: **“After the chapter of water...”** This comes from Nick’s `После главы воды`, but it could trigger meta-trace concerns. Possible alternative: **“After the water episode...”** but that may weaken source.
- Check **pantry** for `кладовая`; possible alternative: **storeroom**, but pantry keeps the domestic house texture.
- Check **“a thing already suspected of an answer”** for `вещь, которую уже подозревают в ответе`.
- Check **“empty is a content”** for Nora’s `пустое — это содержание`; intentionally strange.
- Check **“It keeps well”** for `Она хорошо хранится`; Ivar-like, but maybe too idiomatic in English.
- Check **“A rumor had no throat, so it did not go hoarse.”** Preserves `не садился`; could be changed to “could not go hoarse,” but current phrasing is clear.
- Check **“they have it”** for `у них` in the list of words; it adds a pronoun for English clarity.
- Check **“the world knows how to be not an enemy, but a condition.”** Faithful and important, but slightly formal.
- Check **“the form of a thing he had not yet seen”**; awkward but source-faithful.
- Check **“That is how a rumor became an object.”** It preserves the conceptual turn, but might need **“became a thing”** for title continuity. Current choice follows Russian `предмет`.
- Check **“an empty form begins to promise more than a full one”**; important and likely correct.
- Check **“the word itself had already sounded here before”** for `само слово уже звучало здесь раньше`; natural enough but maybe slightly abstract.
- Check **“There it was”** for `Вот оно`; it points to relief appearing between Nick and Thomas, not the thing itself.
- Check **“The things had taken their places.”** Good and source-faithful, but it bears strong chapter pressure.
- Check final line **“It had received a place where it could wait.”** It preserves the closing but may be slightly smoother than Russian.

## Known risks

- **Volume:** preserved; no intentional compression.
- **Humor:** preserved through Nick, Nora, Marek, Lina, and the object-world jokes, without turning scenes into comedy.
- **Sampo canon:** preserved; Sampo is not made into a solved object, but the chapter shows the danger of making it into a thing.
- **Ivar rule:** preserved; Ivar remains concise and action-based. He does not lecture; he rearranges, touches, places, answers minimally.
- **Objectness:** preserved; the chapter depends on things being things rather than symbols.
- **Meta-trace risk:** one source-based phrase in Nick’s speech, **“After the chapter of water,”** should be audited. It is in the Russian final and should probably remain, but controller should decide.
- **English smoothness:** several phrases remain intentionally strange to preserve authorial pressure and the chapter’s thingness.
