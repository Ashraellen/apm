## Transcreation approach

The English draft preserves Chapter 13 as the immediate consequence of Chapter 12: the living continuation of yesterday’s bread is not lost through malice, violence, or carelessness alone, but through order, cleanliness, usefulness, and not knowing. The chapter’s core pressure is the sour smell that can belong either to life or to spoilage, depending on the system reading it.

The chapter does not make Matti a comic foreigner or a villain. His short English is kept functional, tool-like, and sufficient for work. He is useful, practical, careful, and wrong. That is why the loss is heavier: he does not destroy the starter; he cleans it away.

## Key choices

- **Title:** `Глава тринадцатая. Кислое` rendered as **Chapter Thirteen. Sour**. Kept blunt and nominal rather than explanatory. Controller should check whether `The Sour` would better preserve the substantive force of `Кислое`, but `Sour` is cleaner as an English chapter title.
- `Дождь не закончился. / Он только устал.` rendered as **“The rain did not end. / It only got tired.”** Preserves the tired weather-continuity from Chapter 12.
- `серой, мелкой, ровной тяжестью` rendered as **“a grey, fine, even weight.”** Keeps pressure, not lyricism.
- `Лес стоял неподвижно и мокро...` rendered with **“as if someone had put it there before the beginning of all their words and had now forgotten to remove it.”** Preserves the dry absurdity and the chapter’s later removal/cleaning logic.
- `Кислое стояло у печи.` rendered as **“The sour thing stood by the stove.”** Chosen to preserve the odd materialization of `кислое`; controller may review.
- Lina’s morning feeding line rendered as **“So it wakes up.”** Keeps the living process without mystifying it.
- David’s note **“morning — feed”** kept without quantity, preserving the shift from recipe to incomplete instruction.
- `дышал не грудью, а терпением` rendered as **“breathing not with a chest, but with patience.”** Central image; should remain strange.
- `Если не знать... Если знать — тоже...` rendered as **“If one did not know... If one did know...”** Keeps the epistemic trap.
- Thomas’s `слишком кисло / посмотрим` exchange preserved around **too / we’ll see**.
- Nick’s blanket lines preserved: **“officially withdrawn from human activity”**, **“ancient form of refusing civilization.”** Keeps humor under wet domestic pressure.
- Ivar’s absence line preserved: when he is inside, even silence seems to know it is being heard.
- Recipe sheet no longer in the notebook: preserved as important continuation of David’s arc.
- `На листе было тихо. / В банке было не тихо.` rendered as **“On the sheet it was quiet. / In the jar it was not quiet.”** Keeps the paper/life contrast.
- Thomas’s city-program line kept as **ingredients, program, time. You put it in, choose, wait.** Avoided over-explaining bread machine logic.
- Lina’s correction preserved: **“You’re against the fact that it doesn’t behave like a program.”**
- `слово “программа” уже вошло в комнату...` rendered as **“the word ‘program’ had already entered the room and laid itself on the table too evenly, too dryly.”** Key Sampo-danger trace.
- Matti’s English retained short and functional: **“I check house,” “I bring one,” “Bad? Spoiled?” “I clean,” “I throw.”** It must not be mocked or over-standardized.
- Narration explicitly preserves: Matti’s words are tools like keys; needed word taken, unnecessary word not taken.
- `Дом сразу стал другим. / Не чужим — проверяемым.` rendered as **“The house immediately became different. / Not foreign — inspected.”** Key shift.
- Matti checking the house is kept detailed and respectful: sink, trap, pipe, stove, damper, ash, firewood.
- Marek/Matti dialogue preserved as short practical English.
- Nick’s “natural smell” line preserved.
- Ivar/Matti handshake preserved: no hidden meaning. Their relation is practical and ordinary.
- `Для Матти дом был домом...` rendered as **house was a house / people — people / stove — stove / water — water / smell — smell.** Important worldview summary.
- Movement after Matti arrives preserved as almost cheerful, simple domestic maintenance.
- `Именно поэтому никто не следил за банкой.` rendered as **“That was exactly why no one was watching the jar.”** Key turn.
- `Old? / Bad? Spoiled? / I clean` preserved as short English. Matti’s mistake is in the category, not in intention.
- `Он действительно не знал... но не так, чтобы взять ответственность за запах` rendered as **“He sort of knew. But not enough to take responsibility for the smell.”** Preserves Nick’s uncomfortable partial knowledge.
- `Для человека, который пришёл проверять дом...` preserved: sour jar is not mystery, not living continuation, not tomorrow’s bread; it is something forgotten to clear away.
- `Никакой драмы. / Никакого злодейства. / Просто порядок.` rendered exactly in function: **“No drama. / No villainy. / Just order.”** Central line.
- Lina holding the salt box as weight preserved.
- `Тишина не сразу поняла...` rendered as **“Silence did not understand at once that it needed to become silence.”** Important tonal line.
- Lina’s English **“It was not spoiled.”** preserved.
- `starter` kept; Matti does not understand until Lina says **“For bread.”**
- Lina’s repeated **“I know.”** preserved as the painful line: she knows he meant to clean.
- `Он не сломал. / Он убрал.` rendered as **“He had not broken it. / He had cleared it away.”** Central distinction.
- Thomas’s defense **“He didn’t know”** preserved as self-saving, not merely moral defense.
- `It wasn’t written on it` preserved because Thomas hears himself and the whole chapter turns.
- David turning over the recipe and reading missing lines preserved: `do not throw away if it smells sour`, `this is alive`, `not every bad smell means death`.
- Nora’s line rendered as **“He didn’t know that living things sometimes smell like that.”** Central.
- Matti offering new yeast preserved as wrong replacement in a replaceable system.
- `выброшенное было не количеством. / И не ингредиентом. / Оно было продолжением вчерашнего.` rendered as **“what had been thrown away was not a quantity. / And not an ingredient. / It was the continuation of yesterday.”** Must remain exact.
- Thomas/Lina impossibility exchange preserved: even if written, how would Matti know this was `it`?
- David’s labelling idea preserved: **Starter. Do not throw.** Then the logic of endless labels and inevitable unlabeled life.
- `Мусор — это не свойство вещи.` rendered as **“Rubbish is not a property of a thing.”** Key philosophical line.
- `иногда это имя, которое ей дали слишком рано` rendered as **“Sometimes it is a name given to it too early.”**
- Ivar’s one-word **“There.”** preserved as answer to `He didn’t know.`
- `ни оправданием, ни приговором` rendered as **“neither excuse nor sentence.”**
- Nora approaching the rubbish bag and Nick stopping her preserved without joke.
- Trash bag paragraph preserved as concrete and helpless, not symbolic abstraction.
- Lina sitting down as first real gesture of loss preserved.
- Flour on Lina’s nail preserved as visual anchor.
- `рецепт не запомнит руки / руки не вернут то, что не узнали` rendered as **“a recipe would not remember the hands / the hands would not bring back what had not been recognized.”**
- Thomas’s **“We can start again”** handled as careful, not stupid, even kind.
- Lina’s answer preserved: **“Everything is never lost. / But that does not mean nothing is lost.”**
- David’s central line rendered as **“Between starting again and continuing.”** This should remain simple and painful.
- Matti after the mistake still doing his work preserved as both unbearable and right.
- `Жизнь часто так и делала: не останавливалась достаточно уважительно.` rendered as **“Life often did exactly that: it did not stop respectfully enough.”**
- Final order list preserved: towels, gas cylinder, coffee, checked stove, washed jar, recipe sheet, absence by stove.
- `Пустота / отсутствие` rendered as **emptiness / absence**. Important distinction.
- Nora’s `Кислое не всегда плохое` rendered as **“Sour is not always bad.”** Nick’s response preserves danger of moralizing.
- Shelf labelling scene preserved: it helps **sometimes**, and Thomas does not add **but not always**.
- Outdoor sour smell of wet rot preserved; no caretaker in the forest obliged to return everything to a clean appearance.
- Sofia’s glint parallels Matti’s action without turning into an explanatory historical flashback.
- Ivar’s answer **“Sometimes it can’t.”** preserved as non-closing.
- Final lines preserved: words not false, just too little; nothing stood by the stove under a cloth; recipe remained; continuation had been thrown away.

## Controller focus

Please review especially:

1. **Title:** `Chapter Thirteen. Sour` — possible alternative: `Chapter Thirteen. The Sour`, but current version is cleaner in English.
2. **“The sour thing stood by the stove.”** — deliberately strange to preserve `Кислое стояло у печи`; possible alternative: `The sour stood by the stove`, but that may be too opaque.
3. **Matti’s English.** It is intentionally short and non-native but not comic. Please check if it stays respectful.
4. **“Rubbish” vs “trash.”** Draft uses `rubbish` for the concept and `trash/rubbish bag` depending on line. Controller may standardize, but the philosophical line should probably remain `Rubbish is not a property of a thing.`
5. **“cleared it away”** for `убрал` — chosen to preserve order/cleaning logic. `removed it` is possible but less active/domestic.
6. **“Between starting again and continuing.”** Must remain simple; do not beautify.
7. **“Life often did exactly that: it did not stop respectfully enough.”** Check naturalness but keep the dry pain.
8. **“Sometimes it can’t.”** Ivar’s line should remain minimal and non-explanatory.
