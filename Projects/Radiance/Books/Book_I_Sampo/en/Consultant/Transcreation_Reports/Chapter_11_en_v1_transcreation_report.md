## Transcreation approach

The English draft was created as literary transcreation preserving the chapter’s movement from bread as share/quantity into bread as deadline, dough, yeast, process, waiting, first rising, knocking down, second rising, baking, method, repetition, and the first dangerous wish that the heavy living work might do itself.

The core pressure: bread is not merely food; it becomes a process that cannot be fully protocolized. The dough rises, is knocked down, rises again, bakes, rests, and only then can be eaten. This frustrates Thomas’s need for correctness and David’s desire for repeatable proportions. The Sampo danger advances: they have not found Sampo, but the wish for a repeatable method and eventually self-operating labour has appeared.

## Key choices

- **Title:** `Глава одиннадцатая. Подъём` rendered as **Chapter Eleven. Rising**. Chosen over `The Rise` or `Ascent` because the chapter is about dough rising, not elevation/heroic ascent.
- `хлеб уже не был едой. / Он стал сроком` rendered as **“bread was no longer food. / It had become a deadline.”** Preserves table pressure.
- `завтра... молча покажет пустую доску` rendered as **“tomorrow would sit down at the table and silently show an empty board.”** Keeps animate tomorrow.
- Yeast jar described as **“a thing it is better not to forget”**, preserving anti-symbolic objectness.
- `дрожжи проходят проверку на жизнеспособность` rendered as **“the yeast undergoes a viability test.”** Preserves Nick’s bureaucratic humour.
- `Дай дрожжам сначала проснуться` rendered as **“Let the yeast wake up first.”**
- `не все способны просыпаться под протокол` rendered as **“Not everyone can wake up under protocol.”** Strong Nick line.
- Lina’s movements: **precise, but not measuring**. Important distinction between knowledge and measurement.
- `не прижимала процесс к цифре` rendered as **“She did not press the process down into a number.”** Central.
- Marek: too hot yeast becomes stone — preserved as **“It won’t be bread, it’ll be stone.”**
- `хлебный кодекс... статье о камне` rendered as **“the northern bread code opens with an article on stone.”** Nick’s legal/bureaucratic register.
- Bread returning to flour/water/yeast/heat, but **“it is impossible to return to flour. One can only begin a new process.”** Key line.
- `Хватит на сколько хватит` rendered as **“It will be enough for as much as it will be enough.”** Strange but preserves Lina’s anti-number answer.
- `Это не ответ. / Это тесто.` rendered as **“That is not an answer. / It is dough.”** Central.
- `работает / шевелится` rendered as **working / moving**, preserving Nora’s living-process perception.
- Dough resistance/gathering preserved physically: sticks, stretches, gathers, becomes something third.
- Lina’s hands: **“They did not command the dough. They knew...”** Key object-body line.
- `Не всё, что быстрее, потом можно есть` rendered as **“Not everything that is faster can be eaten later.”** Important line.
- David’s proportions question preserved, but softened by his own caveat.
- `примерно` rendered as **roughly**, with David’s line: **“‘Roughly’ is a word that pretends it is more honest than it is.”**
- Bowl under towel: **nothing happening / impossible to look away** preserved.
- `Столько` rendered as **“That long.”** Keeps Lina’s maddening non-answer.
- Nick’s bread committee: **“dead end due to the resistance of facts.”**
- Marek’s placement logic: **“Here. / Because not there.”** Intentionally severe and strange.
- Ivar’s one-word line: **“Wait.”** Must remain minimal.
- `слово осталось на столе... Как неудобный предмет` rendered as **“the word had remained on the table... Like an inconvenient object.”** Central.
- Nora: **“It isn’t hurrying” / “You’d hear it.”** Preserved.
- The bowl becomes the day’s centre; it **does nothing / and that is not true**.
- Sofia’s anxiety sequence preserved with repeated **What if...** questions.
- Thomas lifting the towel as prosecutor over evidence preserved.
- `тесто чуть поднялось... надежда стала нетерпеливее` rendered as **“the dough had risen a little... hope became more impatient.”**
- `Когда что-то начало происходить... требует темпа` rendered as **“When something has begun happening, a person already demands tempo from it.”** Key conceptual line.
- User’s phrase `где собака порылась` rendered as **“where the dog had rummaged”** to preserve his preferred idiom literally/authorially; controller should check whether this is too strange in English or should be adapted.
- First rising / knocking down sequence preserved: the living does not go in a straight line.
- Lina’s line: **“Do you need bread, or victory over waiting?”** Central.
- David/Sofia porch conversation: bread vs method. **“A method so there will be bread.”**
- `повторить` rendered as **repeated** / **Made so that it happens again**. Central Sampo-danger hinge.
- `новый зубец` rendered as **“a new tooth of the gear.”** Strong mechanical image; controller should review.
- Stone/grain glint preserved as not full vision, but **“a short blow of stone against stone.”**
- `А если бы оно само?` rendered as **“And what if it did it by itself?”** This is the chapter’s deep Sampo seed.
- `само` rendered as **by itself**, not automatically, to keep pre-machine innocence and danger.
- David’s **“A mill?”** preserved as early/just-in-time word.
- Sofia: **“Not yet.”** Preserved.
- Second proofing: towel rising over two soft hills, invisible work inside.
- Oven phase: smell before bread, bread smell as political pressure, smell before permission.
- Ivar: **“Because you are asking the wrong one.” / “The crust.”** Preserved.
- Crust speaking not with words but colour/smell/sound. Important object authority.
- `Чудо взросления` rendered as **“A miracle of maturation.”** Nick-line; controller should check.
- Finished bread still requiring waiting preserved: even ready things need to be left alone.
- `Готовое тоже иногда нужно оставить в покое` rendered as **“Even what is ready sometimes needs to be left alone.”** Key line.
- Bread as event that might not have happened, not quantity.
- Thomas immediately asks how to make it better next time; accepted as beginning, not error/shame.
- `рука уже хотела узнать, что именно повторить` rendered as **“the hand already wanted to know what exactly to repeat.”** Central.
- Final: house as place where people ate bread and already searched for a way not to depend on bread requiring time.
- Final two lines: **“The dough had risen. / But the person already wanted to know how to make it rise again.”** Preserves closing blow.

## Places requiring controller attention

- Check title **Chapter Eleven. Rising**. It correctly avoids heroic `Ascent`, but `The Rising` might also work if a more noun-like title is desired.
- Check **“deadline”** for `срок`. Strong, but perhaps `term` or `due date` would be weaker. `Deadline` fits bread-as-time-pressure.
- Check **“an empty board”** for `пустую доску`; likely correct and object-level.
- Check **“undergoes a viability test”**; good Nick register.
- Check **“She did not press the process down into a number.”** Strong, central.
- Check **“how much time was not hers.”** This line preserves `сколько времени — не её`; intentionally strange.
- Check **“It will be enough for as much as it will be enough.”** Very literal and strange; may need smoother alternative, but preserves Lina’s function.
- Check **“It is dough.”** for `Это тесто`; must be minimal.
- Check **“something third”** for `чем-то третьим`; likely acceptable.
- Check **“They did not command the dough.”** Good and important.
- Check **“Roughly”** for `примерно`; likely best.
- Check **“That long.”** for `Столько`; deliberately annoying answer.
- Check **“dead end due to the resistance of facts.”** Nick line; good but formal.
- Check **“Here. / Because not there.”** Very Ivar/Marek-like; intentionally strange.
- Check **“Wait.”** Must stay one word.
- Check **“Like an inconvenient object.”** Strong, good.
- Check **“bread that hurries out loud.”** Nick line; likely works.
- Check **“where the dog had rummaged.”** This is the user’s preferred idiom rendered literally. It may read odd in English; possible alternative would be **“that was where the dog had been digging”**, but current preserves authorial phrase more directly.
- Check **“a new tooth of the gear.”** for `новый зубец`; strong mechanical turn. Possible alternative: **“a new notch.”**
- Check **“a short blow of stone against stone.”** Strong, source-faithful.
- Check **“And what if it did it by itself?”** Must retain dangerous innocence. Do not over-modernize to `automatically`.
- Check **“A mill?”** It may sound too mundane, but source says exactly `Мельница?` and the word being early/on time is important.
- Check **“The second proofing”** for `Вторая расстойка`; technically correct, but perhaps too bakery-technical. Alternative: **“The second rise.”** Controller should decide.
- Check **“a miracle of maturation.”** for Nick’s `Чудо взросления`; maybe **“a miracle of growing up”** is more natural, but current keeps bread/maturity double sense.
- Check **“Even what is ready sometimes needs to be left alone.”** Key line; likely must remain.
- Check **“waiting is sometimes edible.”** Nick-line, accepted-sounding.
- Check final **“The dough had risen. / But the person already wanted to know how to make it rise again.”** Must preserve the final blow.

## Meta-trace audit watchpoints

- No unauthorized external `chapter`, `scene`, `page`, `author`, `reader`, `novel`, `plot`, `character`, `ending`, `prologue`, or `epilogue` language was added.
- Words like `protocol`, `test`, `code`, `committee`, `record`, `methodology`, `voting rights`, `political pressure`, and `method` are internal to Nick’s bureaucratic humour and Thomas/David’s control-method logic.
- The stone/mill glimpse is kept as Sofia’s brief inner perception, not an explanatory historical flashback.
- Ivar remains minimal and object/action based: he repairs a strap, says `Wait`, later points to the crust, and does not lecture.
- Sampo is not explained or found. The chapter advances the Sampo danger by showing the birth of repeatability: how to make bread happen again, and then how to make labour do itself.

---

## Known risks

- **Title risk:** `Rising` may sound abstract, but in context it is concrete and central.
- **Dough terminology:** `proofing` is accurate but slightly technical; controller should decide if `second rise` is preferable.
- **Idiom risk:** `where the dog had rummaged` deliberately preserves the author’s preferred Russian idiom. It may be too alien in English.
- **Style risk:** Several lines remain intentionally strange: `time was not hers`, `That long`, `It is yeast`, `Because not there`, `the crust has been granted voting rights`, `the hand already wanted to know what exactly to repeat`.
- **Canon risk:** The “by itself” line is a major seed and should not be flattened.

## Russian summary for author

Создана английская literary transcreation одиннадцатой главы. Сохранён главный переход: после доли/хлеба как количества хлеб становится сроком, процессом, тестом, подъёмом, ожиданием и первым опасным желанием повторить живое. Томас хочет правильности и темпа; Давид хочет пропорций и способа; Лина знает процесс руками; Марек возвращает к камню/корке/печи; Ник удерживает бюрократическую иронию; Ивар остаётся минимальным и говорит только там, где нужно. Центральный образ: тесто поднимается, его обминают, оно поднимается снова, хлеб печётся, готовое всё равно требует покоя. Главный скрытый сдвиг: не просто получить хлеб, а найти способ, чтобы хлеб был снова; затем мелькает камень, женщина, плечо, мука и мальчик: `А если бы оно само?` Это ещё не Сампо, но уже опасная дверь к нему. Финал сохранён: тесто поднялось, но человек уже хочет знать, как заставить его подняться снова.
