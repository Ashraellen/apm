## 1. Chapter function preserved

Chapter 21 follows the thin soup/gift chapter by moving into collective work. The chapter is not about a lesson being explained; it shows participation appearing through practical necessity: the water goes the wrong way, and everyone becomes involved.

The chapter movement is preserved:

1. Morning porch after rain; traces from the lost fish and boars blur but do not disappear.
2. Sofia first perceives the problem bodily, by slipping.
3. Water is going toward the threshold and under the porch.
4. Marek begins work without making a speech.
5. Others first stand because Marek seems “already engaged instead of them.”
6. Sofia’s first participation is not conceptual help, but holding a wet board.
7. Thomas begins by wanting the line/rule first, then shifts into carrying stones and digging.
8. David closes the notebook because the work is hard to write down, and carries water instead.
9. Eva holds the door; Nora hands gloves and later arranges small stones.
10. Lina corrects placement and keeps the work practical.
11. Ivar appears with boards, gives only minimal functional words, and does not become a teacher.
12. The water finally goes past the porch.
13. The solution is provisional, crooked, ugly, and enough.

No structural changes recommended.

## 2. Title decision

Russian:

```text
Глава двадцать первая. Участие
```

Chosen English:

```text
Chapter Twenty-One. Participation
```

Rationale:

`Participation` is the strongest direct equivalent. It keeps the chapter from becoming “Help,” which would sound voluntary and moral. Here participation is not a noble decision; it is entering the condition because the board will slide, the water will go under the house, the door must be held, the stones must be carried.

Recommendation: keep `Participation`.

## 3. Central chapter pressure

Central pressure:

```text
One stops being a spectator not when one understands the whole task, but when one holds the part that would slide without one’s hand.
```

This is preserved through Sofia’s hand on the wet board. The chapter’s knowledge is tactile, tiring, dirty, and provisional.

## 5. Character voice notes

### Sofia

Sofia’s arc is tactile and bodily: slipping, holding, ache, dirty hand, inability to let go before the board stops sliding. No abstract lesson should be added.

### Marek

Marek remains exact, work-first, not explanatory. `Divert it`, `Not here`, `Stones`, `More`, `For now`.

### Thomas

Thomas initially wants a line, a measure, a rule. During the work he shifts to `More?`, `This one?`, `Small one.` This is concrete progress.

### Nick

Nick jokes while carrying stones, but several times silence or exhaustion cuts the joke. Keep this balance.

### David

David begins with the notebook, then closes it and carries water. Later he refuses to write because he may lie. This is one of the strongest anti-explanatory moves.

### Eva

Eva holds the door — a small but essential function. She also manages cold entering the house.

### Nora

Nora wants to help; she is assigned gloves, then creates usefulness by stacking small stones. Important quiet participation.

### Lina

Lina sees placement and consequences. She says very little, but her corrections make the water go.

### Ivar

Ivar must remain minimal. He contributes boards and pressure, not wisdom. Do not expand him.
