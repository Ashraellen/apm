## 1. Chapter function preserved

Chapter 15 continues the exact line from Chapters 13–14:

- Chapter 13: the living continuation is thrown away.
- Chapter 14: the house learns that a label can protect what has already been named, but cannot replace recognition.
- Chapter 15: the house learns that even a correct label does not preserve life if the living thing is not kept in the right condition.

The chapter’s central pressure is preserved:

```text
The label remains.
The place has to change.
```

This chapter is not anti-label. It moves beyond label into condition: heat, damp, place, time, attention, and the labor of watching longer.

## 2. Title decision

Russian:

```text
Глава пятнадцатая. Условие
```

Chosen English:

```text
Chapter Fifteen. Condition
```

Rationale:

`Condition` keeps the dry, almost formulaic title and links directly to the chapter’s key problem: the starter is labelled but not well placed. `Conditions` would be more idiomatic for environment, but it would lose the singular conceptual pressure of `Условие`. `The Condition` is possible, but would sound more abstract/philosophical and less clean beside `Sour` and `Label`.

Recommendation: keep `Condition`, but controller should check whether singular without article feels acceptable in this title sequence.

## 3. Key lexical choices

### 3.1. Условие / condition

The central line was rendered as:

```text
Everything living was not only “do not throw away.”

It was “hold it in condition.”
```

This is intentionally slightly strange. A smoother version would be:

```text
It was “keep it under the right conditions.”
```

But that smooths away the title-pressure and makes the idea too explanatory. The Russian has a hard conceptual compression: `держать в условии`. Current English preserves that compression, but controller should check if it feels too unnatural.

Possible alternatives:

```text
It was “keeping it in condition.”
It was “holding it in the right condition.”
It was “keeping it within the condition.”
It was “keeping it under the right conditions.”
```

My recommendation: keep current line if controller accepts the deliberate pressure; otherwise use `It was “holding it in the right condition.”`

### 3.2. Метка / label

`Label` continues from Chapter 14. The label is useful but insufficient. The final contrast has been preserved:

```text
The label remained on the glass.

The jar already stood in another place.
```

### 3.3. След / trace / mark

The old circle on wood is not translated as `mark` because Chapter 14 already uses mark/label logic. Here it is a `trace`, not an intentional sign.

Key line:

```text
The old circle on the shelf was not a sign. Not a label. No one had written on it.
```

This preserves the source distinction: trace is not label, but still speaks.

### 3.4. Отблеск / glint

`Glint` continues the established translation for София’s short perception flashes. It remains sensory, not explanatory.

## 4. Important preserved lines / pressure points

### Label peeling off

```text
The words held. The glue did not.
```

Important because the chapter begins by showing that even the label itself depends on conditions.

### Paper and damp

```text
Paper had simply remembered that the air was wet.
```

Keeps the house-world texture and avoids overexplaining.

### Wrong order of inspection

```text
The order of inspection was precise and wrong at the same time.
```

This line shows David’s methodological impulse: correct procedure, wrong center.

### Place, not label

```text
Not at the labels. At the place.
```

Important for Lina’s role and the chapter’s title.

### Yesterday was yesterday

```text
Yesterday was yesterday.
```

Should remain dry and non-aphoristic.

### Protected from hand, not heat

```text
“From a hand,” Lina said. “Not from heat.”
```

Key conceptual line. Do not soften.

### Two speeches not coinciding

```text
The sign kept saying: do not throw away.

The living thing said: it is not right for me here.

And those two speeches did not coincide.
```

This should remain central.

### List putting on uniform

```text
I hear the list putting on a uniform.
```

Nick’s line preserves the bureaucratic danger from Chapters 14–15.

### Not losing and forcing to live

```text
Not losing it and forcing it to live.
```

This is one of Lina’s most important lines in the chapter. It should remain sharp and simple.

### Trace, not label

```text
The trace did not explain what had stood on it. It only said: something was already held here.
```

This is one of Sofia’s central recognitions.

### Ivar holding the place

```text
Sometimes it seems we arrived at ourselves. And Ivar is simply holding the place so we don’t scatter too early.
```

Nora says this and then retreats. It should not become doctrinal. It is a living guess, not an explanation of Ivar.

### Fire does not read

```text
Fire does not read.
```

The glint’s key line. It should remain blunt.

### Coincidence not labelled

```text
Because the moment she said it, the coincidence would become an object. They would start considering it, checking it, labelling it.
```

This preserves the chapter’s self-protective refusal to turn a mystical overlap into a system.

### Look longer

```text
“Then look.”

“I am looking.”

“Longer.”
```

This connects to Chapter 14’s `Then you look`, but is more pedagogical and domestic in the present moment.

### Ivar sees now

```text
“You knew?”

“No.”

“But you said ‘yes.’”

“Now I see.”
```

Ivar remains minimal and does not claim prior knowledge.

### Final movement

```text
It has to stand in the right world.

At least a small one.

At least on a shelf.

At least for today.

The label remained on the glass.

The jar already stood in another place.
```

This ending keeps the domestic-metaphysical pressure without explaining Sampo.

## 5. Character voice notes

### Sofia

Sofia notices traces and conditions, but does not turn them into doctrine. Her interiority remains exact, quiet, and suspicious of systems.

### Thomas

Thomas intensifies the practical/procedural response: temperature, humidity, exact place, number of checks. He is not stupid; he is afraid and wants not to lose the living thing again.

Important line:

```text
“I want to learn,” he said.
```

### David

David now resists over-listing more than before. In this chapter he still writes, but also learns not to write everything down.

Important note: `David stood nearby and did not write anything down. That was noticeable.`

### Nick

Nick’s humor remains protective, but less flippant. He is capable of stopping, not just mocking.

Important lines:

```text
“I hear the list putting on a uniform.”
```

```text
“Right now, his court of appeal is collapsing inside.”
```

### Lina

Lina is precise, practical, not teacherly. She does not explain more than needed. She moves the jar because the jar’s place matters more than the discussion.

### Nora

Nora gives the Ivar line and the fire line echo without trying to explain the coincidence. She remains sharp and unsure.

### Ivar

Ivar remains minimal. He did not know in advance; he sees now. This is important: he is not turned into a guru or omniscient figure.
