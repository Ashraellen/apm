## Transcreation approach

The English draft was created as a literary transcreation preserving the chapter’s movement from airport realism into northern strangeness, without turning the material into generic fantasy, spiritual retreat language, or polished commercial prose.

The main goal was to preserve Sofia’s living lens, Nick’s defensive humor, Marek’s dry timing, Eva’s calm understatement, and Ivar’s ordinary force through presence and action rather than explanation. The chapter keeps the object-world active: suitcases, wet wool, coffee, buses, forest, bilberries, firewood, soup, bread, fish bones, moss, fire, smoke, lake, cold.

---

## Key choices

- **Title:** `Глава первая. Маленькое событие` rendered as **Chapter One. A Small Event**.
- **Sampo** remains `Sampo`, untranslated and unexplained.
- **Ивар** rendered as **Ivar**, with no added guru language or mystical framing.
- **Ник** rendered as **Nick**, preserving his skeptical, contract-minded, defensive humor.
- **Ева, Марек, Лина, Давид, Томас, Нора** rendered as **Eva, Marek, Lina, David, Thomas, Nora**.
- `земля не молола / просто терпела` rendered as **“the earth was not grinding / It was simply enduring”** to echo the Prologue while grounding the airport scene.
- `Север уже начал воспитательную работу` rendered as **“the north had already begun its educational work”** to preserve humor without making it sitcom-like.
- `люди в микроавтобусе просто ещё не доехали...` preserved as a long reflective sentence, because the pressure belongs to Sofia’s perception and the chapter rhythm.
- `Дом не выглядел старым. Он выглядел продолжительным.` rendered as **“The house did not look old. It looked prolonged.”** This is intentionally strange but functional.
- `Печь грела потому... суп пах потому... пол скрипел потому...` kept as parallel structure to preserve working warmth and object causality.
- `Север любит оптимистов. Они хрустят громче.` rendered as **“The north loves optimists. They crunch louder.”** Kept dry and slightly brutal.
- Final bridge into the Prologue kept: **“That night, the earth was grinding”** and **“something slowly turned.”**

## Places requiring controller attention

- Check whether **“It looked prolonged”** is acceptable for `Он выглядел продолжительным`, or whether it needs a less strange but still accurate alternative.
- Check whether **“wet wool”** sufficiently carries `мокрой шерстью`; it is natural English but may sound slightly material-specific.
- Check whether **“the north had already begun its educational work”** preserves the humor without becoming too abstract.
- Check whether **“like traces of how the earth had once tried to explain itself to a person and changed its mind”** carries the map sentence accurately.
- Check whether **“The fire did not catch at once. First it thought. Then it made a decision.”** preserves the living-object tone without over-personifying.
- Check whether Nick’s voice is consistent: dry, defensive, not too sitcom-like.
- Check whether Ivar’s lines remain ordinary enough and do not turn him into a teacher figure.

---

## Known risks

- **Volume:** preserved; English expands where required for natural literary movement.
- **Humor:** preserved by function, especially Nick/Marek/Lina exchanges; no added punchlines intended.
- **Sampo canon:** protected; Sampo is introduced as word and pressure, not solved object or doctrine.
- **Ivar rule:** protected; Ivar acts, carries firewood, speaks sparingly, and remains seen from outside.
- **Meta-trace risk:** controlled; no external book/story/reader language added in the prose body. The only potentially sensitive word `book` appears inside the world in phrases like “book covers” / “in a book,” where it belongs literally.
- **English smoothness:** kept readable but not over-smoothed; several strange constructions are deliberately preserved.
