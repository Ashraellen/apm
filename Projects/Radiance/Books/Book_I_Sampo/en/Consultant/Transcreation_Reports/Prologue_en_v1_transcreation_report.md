## Transcreation approach

The English version was created as literary transcreation rather than literal translation. The draft keeps the prologue’s mythic pressure, northern stillness, bodily horror, feeding-world logic, and slow movement from Sampo as extractable object toward Sampo as the living world that cannot be possessed.

The English was allowed to breathe slightly longer where needed for rhythm and natural literary pressure, but without adding explanation or smoothing the strangeness into generic fantasy.

---

## Key choices

- **Sampo** remains `Sampo`, untranslated and unexplained.
- `земля молола` was rendered as **“the earth was grinding”** to preserve the mill/body/creation pressure without making Sampo a machine.
- `северное сияние` was rendered as **“the northern lights”**, not as a poetic substitute, to keep the northern physical world clear.
- `старший` was rendered as **“the eldest”**, preserving age/authority without turning him into a teacher, shaman, or wise guide.
- `то, что кормит` became **“what feeds”**, kept deliberately simple and recurring in function.
- The warning line became **“Hunger does not end for the one who wants to take what he is standing inside.”** This keeps the philosophical strike direct, not sermon-like.
- `лес ... стал одним большим слухом` became **“the whole forest stopped being forest and became one vast act of hearing.”** This keeps the strangeness without over-explaining it.
- `Рот` was preserved as **“A mouth.”** Short, plain, bodily, and not symbolic decoration.
- The final answer **“Sampo.”** is kept as the smallest possible word, not as an explanation.

---

## Places requiring controller attention

- Check whether **“the earth was grinding”** carries enough force, or whether a more primal form such as **“the earth ground”** would better fit the opening.
- Check whether **“one vast act of hearing”** is acceptable for `одним большим слухом`, or whether it should be rougher/stranger.
- Check whether **“the eldest”** should remain, or whether **“the oldest”** / **“the leader”** would better serve the prologue’s voice.
- Check whether **“open in a plea”** correctly carries `открытый в просьбе` without becoming too polished.

---

## Known risks

- **Volume:** preserved; English is slightly expanded where required for natural literary rhythm.
- **Humor:** minimal in the prologue; no added cleverness.
- **Sampo canon:** protected; Sampo is not explained, solved, or turned into an artifact.
- **Ivar rule:** not applicable in this unit.
- **Meta-trace risk:** no external book/chapter/story language introduced inside the prose body.
- **English smoothness:** kept literary and readable, but some strange constructions are intentionally preserved.
