## 1. Epilogue function preserved

The epilogue is not a summary speech and not an explanatory close. It is a departure scene in which the house continues working, the unfinished `Сампо —` sheet travels with David, bread is wrapped, things are packed, the threshold is crossed, and Aila hears what almost everyone mistakes for ordinary noise.

The movement is preserved:

1. Departure begins without solemnity.
2. The house does not become quiet; it returns to ordinary work.
3. The weather is not rainy, but after rain.
4. The little ditch still works; water continues past the house.
5. Bags appear one by one, with Nick’s sock humor keeping the air grounded.
6. Sofia folds badly; her hand still remembers the board.
7. The sheet `Сампо —` remains unfinished.
8. David places the unfinished sheet into the folder between map and recipe, while the pencil goes elsewhere.
9. Lina wraps the bread as something requiring form, not as mere leftovers.
10. Thomas receives bread, not an answer.
11. Nora’s line about the soul not going with wet things is left unmade into a slogan.
12. Ivar remains nearby but not central, useful rather than wise.
13. Sofia remembers a small sequence of `not here` / `not under the house` / `not with wet things` / `not in the center` / `not after the dash`, then stops it before understanding becomes a list.
14. Aila’s case enters as the hidden musical object and first sound.
15. They leave not as procession, but as people with bags, wet boots, bread, papers, gloves, unsaid words, and road ahead.
16. The house stands without holding or releasing.
17. Aila does not mistake the water’s sound for ordinary noise.
18. She says nothing and steps out.

No structural changes recommended.

## 5. Character voice notes

### Sofia

Sofia does not summarize. She feels the hand, the threshold, the house, and the impossibility of packing the house as a thing.

### David

David carries the unfinished paper. He does not complete it and does not abandon it. The folder placement matters.

### Thomas

Thomas receives bread, not clarity. He has learned to separate bread from wet jacket, and this is shown as a kind of real understanding.

### Nick

Nick remains funny but softened. He does not turn Nora’s line into a phrase, and that restraint is important.

### Lina

Lina provides form: wrapping bread, separating wet things, covering the knife. Practical order, not explanation.

### Nora

Nora’s line about the soul is one of the epilogue’s strongest small phrases. It must remain simple and serious.

### Marek

Marek’s last practical instructions remain exact and unsentimental: time, road after rain, careful on step, get in the van.

### Ivar

Ivar stays useful and minimal: damper, firebox, scarf, jars, door. No final wisdom speech.

### Aila

Aila is the first sound. She listens rather than explains. The epilogue must not turn her into an oracle.

## 6. Meta-trace audit

Potential watchpoints:

- `Soul uncertainly` is grammatically odd; likely comic and acceptable, but controller should check.
- Nora’s `The soul also not with the wet` is deliberately childlike; do not overcorrect unless needed for readability.
- `Aila did not mistake it` repeats `mistaken/mistake`; probably source-faithful and strong.
- `minibus` vs `van`: draft uses both (`minibus` for object, `van` in dialogue/narration). Controller should decide consistency. In prior chapters, if vehicle has been called `minibus`, perhaps use `minibus` throughout. If English idiom wants `van`, use `van` throughout. Watchpoint.
- `case with something long inside` should remain indirect; do not name instrument unless source does.

No unauthorized explanation of Sampo. No doctrine. No forced epiphany. No final motivational closure.
