## 1. Chapter function preserved

Chapter 16 continues the movement from Chapters 13–15:

- Chapter 13: the living continuation is lost.
- Chapter 14: a label helps, but cannot teach recognition.
- Chapter 15: the right name is not enough; the living thing must stand in the right condition.
- Chapter 16: attention itself becomes scheduled, useful, dangerous, and insufficient.

The chapter is not against duty/rotation. It shows that a watch can help, but it can also turn care into permission, fear into procedure, and looking into interference.

The central movement is preserved:

```text
A schedule is useful.
But the house does not read the schedule.
```

## 2. Title decision

Russian:

```text
Глава шестнадцатая. Дежурство
```

Chosen English:

```text
Chapter Sixteen. Watch
```

Rationale:

`Duty` is possible, but it leans toward obligation and institutional tone. `Watch` keeps both meanings active:

- assigned watching / watch duty;
- the act of looking;
- the danger of watching too much;
- the fire/starter line: guard not fear, but the condition.

This is especially important because the glint turns on the boy watching the embers all night and the older woman saying that fire is kept with wood, not eyes.

Controller should verify whether `Watch` is clear enough as a chapter title. My recommendation is to keep it.

## 3. Core lexical choices

### 3.1. Дежурство / watch / rotation / shift

The chapter uses several related English terms:

- `Watch` for the title and Ivar’s naming of the phenomenon.
- `rotation` for David’s practical arrangement.
- `turn` for the human, domestic sense: Eva’s turn, Sofia’s turn.
- `shift` once in Nick’s line: `Your shift is over.`

This avoids making the chapter too bureaucratic while keeping the scheduled-care pressure.

### 3.2. Условие / condition

Chapter 15’s term continues, especially in:

```text
All of that was condition.
```

The chapter widens condition from jar/place to the whole house: stove, door, wet wood, water, cold, people, breath, fear, and time.

### 3.3. Смотреть / look / watch

The chapter deliberately uses `look`, `watch`, and `see` with care:

- `look` — ordinary act of looking;
- `watch` — active guarding / duty;
- `see` — recognition, Ivar’s minimal knowing after the fact.

Important accepted line candidate:

```text
“Now I see.”
```

This continues Ivar’s rule: he is not omniscient; he sees now.

## 4. Important preserved lines / pressure points

### Opening false non-approach

```text
In the morning they did not go to the jar at once.

At least that was how it seemed.
```

The house is already orbiting the jar before admitting it.

### Body betraying thought

```text
That is how one stops not a thought, but a body that has already given the thought away before you.
```

Preserves Sofia’s awkward self-exposure.

### Fruit testimony

```text
Here even fruit gives testimony.
```

Nick’s line keeps the legal-bureaucratic comic tone without overplaying it.

### David’s change

```text
Without the old pleasure with which he had once laid a map out on the table.
```

Important continuity: David is no longer purely in love with system-making.

### No night watch

```text
Because at night it also needs not only to live, but to be left in peace.
```

Key line: care includes not touching.

### Crooked list

```text
It hung slightly uneven.

And for some reason everyone looked at it as at a small concession to the world.
```

This line is important for the house learning not to straighten everything.

### Watching as interference

```text
That now even simply looking feels like interference.
```

Preserved directly.

### Look, do not wake

```text
Look. Don’t wake.
```

This is Lina’s core teaching without turning teacherly.

### The house does not read the schedule

```text
The house, obviously, did not read the schedule.
```

Central chapter line. It extends Chapter 15’s `Fire does not read.`

### Water is not in general

```text
Water is not in general.
```

Nora’s line should remain awkwardly exact and immediately embarrassing to her.

### David not writing

```text
And saw how he did not write.
```

This is one of the chapter’s quiet victories.

### Legal approach to the jar

```text
She was waiting for the moment when she could approach the jar lawfully.

As if the living thing had become something for which one needed a pass.
```

Preserves the danger of scheduled care becoming permission.

### Too many hands

```text
Too many hands.
```

Important, must stay sparse.

### Glint with fire and eyes

```text
Fire is kept with wood, she says. Not with eyes.
```

This mirrors the starter line: watching is not the same as maintaining the condition.

### Beauty not putting on clean clothes

```text
Beauty did not have time to put on clean clothes.
```

Preserves the anti-ornamental interruption after Lina’s `Live beside it.`

### Distant looking

```text
Sometimes looking from afar was exactly the distance the living thing could bear.
```

This is a key corrective to both neglect and anxious interference.

### Ivar’s minimal line

```text
Give it time.
```

Ivar remains minimal and does not explain.

### Final ending

```text
By evening the schedule hung straight.

The house was cooling anyway.
```

This ending preserves the chapter’s hard movement: the paper may be straight, but condition remains living and unstable.

## 5. Character voice notes

### Sofia

Sofia moves from wanting her lawful turn to lowering her hand after hearing the whole house. Her growth is bodily, not explanatory.

### Thomas

Thomas wants to be responsible, but his fear keeps turning into procedure. He learns a little faster than before, but does not become suddenly wise.

### David

David’s arc is important: he writes the schedule, but later does not write more points. He straightens the list, but also accepts that `straight` is not the same as `good`.

### Nick

Nick’s humor is still active but less evasive. Several times he does not joke, or jokes without undermining the truth.

### Lina

Lina carries the practical center:

- no night watch;
- look, don’t wake;
- too many hands;
- live beside it;
- when needed / I won’t always know.

She must remain dry, not therapeutic.

### Nora

Nora’s line `Water is not in general` and her echo of the glint line must feel unplanned, not mystical performance.

### Ivar

Ivar arrives late and minimal. He names the watch, accepts the motivation, and gives only:

```text
Give it time.
```

He does not explain Sampo, the starter, the house, or the lesson.
