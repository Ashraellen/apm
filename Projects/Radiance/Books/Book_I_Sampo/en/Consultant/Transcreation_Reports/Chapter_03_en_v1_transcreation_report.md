## Transcreation approach

The English draft was created as literary transcreation preserving the chapter’s function: after the fire-circle pressure of Chapters 01–02, the morning domestic world returns and becomes more unsettling precisely because it is ordinary. Bread, the knife, the hand, the slice, crumbs, firewood, berries, moss, and the fire pit all remain functional objects first; their pressure emerges through action rather than explanation.

The main task was to preserve the chapter’s quiet comic rhythm without breaking the post-night tension: Nick’s procedural humor weakens and returns in smaller doses, Marek stays dry, Nora remains exact and strange without becoming mystical, Thomas becomes exposed through the slice, and Sofia’s perception continues to move from explanation toward embodied attention.

---

## Key choices

- **Title:** `Глава третья. Ломоть` rendered as **Chapter Three. The Slice**.
- `ломоть` rendered as **slice** throughout. This is simpler than “hunk” and keeps bread ordinary while allowing the thick/medium/thin distinctions to work.
- `утром хлеб лежал на столе так, будто ночью ничего не произошло` rendered as **“In the morning the bread lay on the table as if nothing had happened in the night.”** Preserves the insolent ordinariness.
- `почти дерзко` rendered as **“almost insolent”** rather than “bold” or “impudent,” to keep the dry comic charge.
- `хлеб как подозреваемого` rendered as **“bread as if it were a suspect.”** Preserves Eva’s light, precise humor.
- `партнёр средней тяжести` rendered as **“a conversational partner of medium difficulty.”** Slightly odd by design; keeps Nick’s procedural absurdity.
- `утро подаёт признаки жизни` rendered as **“Morning is showing signs of life.”** Keeps Nick’s dry register.
- Ivar’s entrance remains task-based: firewood, kettle, stove. No added mysticism.
- `он устанавливает границу` rendered as **“He was establishing a boundary.”** Preserves the non-literal reading of Nick cutting bread.
- `юридической аккуратностью` rendered as **“almost legal precision.”** Keeps Nick’s inner profession/temperament without over-explaining.
- `я уважаю ресурс / ты его допрашиваешь` rendered as **“I respect the resource / You are interrogating it.”** Preserves humor and bread-as-object pressure.
- `кто взял, чтобы съесть, а кто — чтобы спрятать` rendered as **“who took in order to eat, and who took in order to hide.”** Preserves central moral distinction without smoothing.
- `рука часто знает раньше головы` rendered as **“It often knows before the head does.”** Simple, bodily, and non-lecturing.
- `компромиссный ломоть` rendered as **“A compromise slice.”** Preserves Nick’s quiet observation.
- `телефон уважает усталость` rendered as **“the phone respects fatigue.”** Keeps comic lightness without turning sitcom-like.
- `запас` rendered contextually: **store**, **extra**, **for later**, or **reserve** depending on the object/function.
- `не верь первому виду` rendered as **“don’t trust the first appearance.”** Keeps Nora’s phrase strange but usable.
- Final line `как всё живое, которое человек слишком долго принимал за запас` rendered as **“Like everything living that a person had taken too long for a store.”** This is intentionally strange and may need controller attention.

## Places requiring controller attention

- Check whether **Chapter Three. The Slice** is the best title, or whether **The Hunk** / **A Slice** would better carry `ломоть`.
- Check whether **“a conversational partner of medium difficulty”** preserves Nick’s humor or sounds too technical/awkward in English.
- Check whether **“almost legal precision”** is acceptable for `почти юридической аккуратностью`, or whether **“almost juridical precision”** would be closer but less natural.
- Check whether **“It landed on the plate with a sound as if it had taken a position”** preserves the slice’s weight without over-personifying.
- Check whether **“I respect the resource / You are interrogating it”** lands naturally and keeps the comic edge.
- Check whether **“The earth is not greedy”** and **“who took in order to hide”** preserve the central distinction without making Ivar too aphoristic.
- Check whether **“for later”** is enough for `впрок`, especially in the Thomas slice sequence.
- Check whether **“It was said to me that way”** is the best rendering of Nora’s `Мне так сказалось`; alternatives may include **“It came to me that way”**, but that risks sounding more mystical.
- Check whether **“the word hand no longer fully belonged to them”** preserves `Слово "рука" теперь принадлежало им не полностью`.
- Check final line: **“Like everything living that a person had taken too long for a store.”** It is faithful in pressure but deliberately odd. Controller may prefer **“mistaken for a store”** or **“treated as a reserve.”**

## Known risks

- **Volume:** preserved, with natural English expansion where needed.
- **Humor:** preserved as low, dry, situational, especially through Nick, Marek, Eva, and Nora.
- **Sampo canon:** preserved; Sampo is not explained in this chapter and remains active through bread, taking, hand, store, and living matter.
- **Ivar rule:** preserved; Ivar does not become POV, guru, or explainer. He enters through work and gives only short action-guiding lines.
- **Objectness:** preserved; bread remains bread, the slice remains edible, crumbs remain crumbs, firewood remains firewood. The meaning emerges from use.
- **Meta-trace risk:** controlled. Source itself contains `story` and `tale` language across the project; in this chapter the key risk is the final `tale under a familiar name`, but it corresponds to `сказка под привычным именем` and should be considered source-based rather than leakage.
- **English smoothness:** several strange constructions are intentionally retained to preserve authorial pressure rather than flatten the text into ordinary literary realism.
