## 1. Chapter function preserved

Chapter 18 continues directly from the final image of Chapter 17: the tracks of the forest remain on the floor. The central movement is not “cleaning” in a simple household sense, but redistribution / exchange / relocation:

- dirt changes address;
- clean water becomes grey water;
- floor water goes to the bushes;
- ash is not simply waste, but something still needing correct handling;
- peels are not thrown away but taken out;
- wet chips and wet logs need time near warmth;
- porridge becomes food through water, grain, apples, salt, spice, heat, waiting;
- stuck porridge is not torn off, but released with water;
- words become too clean if they do not carry residue.

The chapter deepens the house logic from conditions into circulation.

## 2. Title decision

Russian:

```text
Глава восемнадцатая. Обмен
```

Chosen English:

```text
Chapter Eighteen. Exchange
```

Rationale:

`Exchange` preserves the broad transfer logic: inside/outside, clean/dirty, wet/dry, heat/ash, food/leftovers, place/no place. Alternatives like `Trade`, `Swap`, or `Transfer` would narrow the meaning too much.

Recommendation: keep `Exchange` unless controller strongly prefers `Exchange` vs `The Exchange`.

## 5. Character voice notes

Sofia observes movement of matter and place. She repeatedly resists making the thought beautiful. This is preserved:


Marek remains functional and spare. He knows place, timing, ash, fox, damp, and heat. He explains only as much as needed.

Lina remains practical. Her strongest chapter lines:

- `It is needed. Just not here.`
- `For everyone.`
- `Don’t tear it off.`
- `It’s soaking.`
- `Not by itself. With water.`

Thomas continues to ask for measure but begins to stop before asking. He cuts apples, leaves the pot to soak, moves the wet log near warmth, and accepts `we’ll see` with a tired smile.

### Nick

Nick keeps humor tied to household labor and bureaucracy. He jokes while actually doing the work.

### David

David’s notebook continues to fail productively. Categories remain insufficient. He writes English categories, then crosses out `BOWL` and writes `миска`, because the English word is too clean.

### Nora

Nora’s perception remains small and exact: seeds, place, washing hands, `It’s easier that way.` She continues to be embarrassed by correctness.

### Eva

Eva is domestic realism. She keeps everyone from drifting into metaphysics, often by assigning wiping, washing, or spoons.
