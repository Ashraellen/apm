## Transcreation approach

The English draft was created as literary transcreation preserving the chapter’s movement from object/place/waiting into traces, tracks, indications, evidence, and the infection of the gaze. The chapter shows how, once rumor has received a place where it can wait, the world begins to look readable: tracks in mud, moss in a crack, an old notch, a peeled patch of bark, stones in a half-circle, crumbs on the table.

The key was to avoid turning the forest into a mystical code. The forest shows nothing. The danger is that people have already decided that it could show. Therefore the English keeps the physical world concrete and damp: wet clothing, mud, rope, boards, moose tracks, moss, notches, roots, stones, rain, crumbs.

## Key choices

- **Title:** `Глава шестая. След` rendered as **Chapter Six. The Trace**.
- `след` rendered contextually as **trace**, **track**, or **tracks** depending on the concrete situation.
- `стала записывающей` rendered as **“had become recording.”** This is intentionally strange and preserves the path as surface that records pressure.
- `Всё могло быть просто следствием дождя` rendered as **“All of it could simply be a consequence of rain.”** Keeps `следствие` without overplaying evidence language too early.
- `искала форму направления` rendered as **“searching for the form of direction.”** Preserves the bodily-intellectual unease.
- `Если искать приключение — далеко` rendered as **“If you look for an adventure — far.”** Keeps Marek’s dry practicality.
- `фраза повышенной влажности` rendered as **“a phrase of increased dampness.”** Deliberately odd; preserves Nick’s comic wet-world logic.
- `ветвь оставила... след движения руки` rendered as **“there was a new trace on it — not of a foot, not of a hoof, but of the movement of a hand.”** Preserves the spreading trace logic.
- Ivar’s line `След говорит, что кто-то прошёл. Не что он нашёл.` rendered as **“A track says someone passed. Not what he found.”** Core rule of the chapter.
- `picked up from the air` for `подобрать с воздуха` preserves the quietness of Ivar’s phrase.
- `улика` rendered as **evidence**, marking the dangerous escalation from sign/trace into investigation/case.
- `Мир начал превращаться не просто в карту, а в дело` rendered as **“The world had begun turning not simply into a map, but into a case.”** This is an important conceptual line.
- `точность без пользы` rendered as **“precision without use.”** Preserves Nick’s rhythm.
- `Чтобы было видно, как легко ошибиться` rendered as **“So it would be visible how easy it is to be mistaken.”** Keeps Nora’s unsettling practical demonstration.
- `болезнь взгляда` rendered as **“the sickness of the gaze.”** Important and source-faithful.
- `если долго лежать... похоже на намерение` rendered as **“If you lie long enough, everything around you will begin to look like intention too.”** Preserves Nora’s stone line.
- `не потому что изменился лес` rendered as **“Not because the forest had changed.”** Keeps the clean correction of perception.
- `лес ничего не показывал` rendered as **“The forest showed nothing.”** Preserves the anti-sign logic.
- Final: **“But they had already had time to decide that it could.”** Faithful to the closing pressure.

## Places requiring controller attention

- Check title **Chapter Six. The Trace**. Alternatives: **The Track** / **The Sign**, but `Trace` best holds track, residue, evidence, and interpretive danger.
- Check whether **“had become recording”** is acceptable for `стала записывающей`; it is grammatically unusual but motif-accurate.
- Check **“a phrase of increased dampness”** for Nick’s `фраза повышенной влажности`; it is odd but intentionally comic.
- Check whether **“adventure”** should remain singular in `wet adventure`; it reads slightly idiomatic but may soften the dry Russian.
- Check **“The path had become thinner”** for `тропа стала тоньше`; possible alternative: **“more tenuous.”** Current phrasing is simpler and more physical.
- Check **“The world had begun turning not simply into a map, but into a case.”** This is semantically exact but may require meta-trace audit because `case` can imply investigation; that is intended by `улика/дело`.
- Check **“the sickness of the gaze.”** It is strong and faithful but a little abstract.
- Check **“they had been allowed to think he had not been wrong to begin searching.”** This preserves the psychological shift in Thomas; controller may prefer more direct English.
- Check **“emptiness could have a route.”** It preserves `у пустоты мог быть маршрут`, but it is intentionally strange.
- Check **“so that the crumb could become a crumb again.”** This is central and likely right, but should be checked for tone.
- Check final line **“But they had already had time to decide that it could.”** It is faithful but slightly cumbersome; possible alternative: **“But they had already managed to decide that it could.”**

## Meta-trace audit watchpoints

- No unauthorized external `chapter`, `scene`, `page`, `author`, `reader`, `novel`, `plot`, `character`, `ending`, `prologue`, or `epilogue` language was added.
- `case` in **“the world... into a case”** is source-based from `дело` and belongs to the evidence/ulika logic, not external narrative mechanics.
- `evidence` is source-based from `улика` and should remain if controller accepts.
- The chapter’s abstraction remains internal to Sofia’s perception and the group’s shared interpretive drift.

---

## Known risks

- **Volume:** preserved; no intentional compression.
- **Humor:** preserved through Nick and Marek, but quieter than previous chapters as the “search” infection deepens.
- **Sampo canon:** preserved. Sampo is not found, shown, explained, mapped, or turned into a forest code. The chapter demonstrates how the desire for Sampo makes the world look readable.
- **Ivar rule:** preserved. Ivar remains behind, literal and brief. He does not teach; he says only enough to mark the difference between passing and finding.
- **Objectness/trace logic:** preserved. Things remain physical, and traces remain weathered, unstable, and easy to misread.
- **English smoothness:** some constructions remain intentionally strange to keep the authorial pressure: `had become recording`, `form of direction`, `phrase of increased dampness`, `sickness of the gaze`, `emptiness could have a route`.
