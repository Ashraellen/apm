## 1. Chapter function preserved

Chapter 14 continues directly after the loss of the starter in Chapter 13. Its function is not to explain the previous chapter, but to show the next defensive movement of the house: after the living continuation was thrown away, people try to protect the new beginning by marking, labelling, listing, translating, dating, warning, and making the world more readable.

The chapter’s central tension has been preserved:

```text
A label can save what has already been named.
But a label cannot replace recognition.
```

The chapter holds the balance between:

- practical necessity;
- fear after loss;
- the danger of administrative expansion;
- living attention as something more fragile and less complete than certainty.

---

## 2. Key title decision

Russian:

```text
Глава четырнадцатая. Метка
```

Chosen English:

```text
Chapter Fourteen. Label
```

Rationale:

`Mark` would sound more mythic, ritual, or prophetic in English. The chapter is deliberately domestic and administrative: white tape, jar, shelf, “ASK BEFORE THROWING,” Finnish wording, short list by the kitchen door. `Label` keeps the practical-bureaucratic surface and lets the deeper meaning emerge through the scene.

Controller may verify whether `Label` is preferable to `Mark`, but my recommendation is to keep `Label`.

## 3. Core lexical choices

### 3.1. Метка / label

The word `label` is kept consistently where the chapter deals with tape, naming, practical protection, and the limits of such protection.

Key line:

```text
A label saves what has already been named.
```

This preserves:

```text
Метка спасает то, что уже назвали.
```

### 3.2. Узнавать / recognize

The final movement uses `recognized` / `recognition`, not `identified`, because the chapter is not about technical classification. It is about seeing the living thing in front of one.

Final line:

```text
Everything else still had to be recognized.
```

### 3.3. Мусор / rubbish

`Rubbish` is retained from Chapter 13 for continuity with the accepted line:

```text
Rubbish is not a property of a thing.
```

This also keeps British/neutral literary texture and avoids switching to `trash` mid-line.

### 3.4. Wall newspaper

Russian:

```text
Матти не будет читать стенгазету на трёх языках.
```

Chosen English:

```text
Matti won’t read a wall newspaper in three languages.
```

This is intentionally a little odd in English, because the Russian joke carries a Soviet/post-Soviet administrative smell. It may sound unfamiliar but understandable from context. Controller should decide whether to keep the oddness or soften to `notice board` / `wall poster`.

My recommendation: keep `wall newspaper` unless it feels too foreign for the English reader.

## 4. Important preserved lines / pressure points

### Opening absence

```text
In the morning the shelf by the stove was empty.
Not simply free.
An empty shelf is when something can be put on it.
This one was empty differently.
```

The distinction between empty space and absence follows directly from Chapter 13.

### Words outliving the referent

```text
The words had preserved themselves better than the thing they referred to.
```

This keeps the painful administrative-literary irony of the recipe remaining while continuation is gone.

### Thomas’s late label

```text
He wrote slowly. Too evenly. Then he looked at the inscription like a person who had come on time — and was still late.
```

This line is important for Thomas’s guilt and his desire to repair through correct formulation.

### New beginning worse than hope

```text
A new beginning looked worse than hope.
```

This should remain dry and exact, not beautified.

### International protection system

```text
Splendid. We now have an international starter-protection system with probabilistic spelling.
```

This preserves Nick’s humor without making it merely comic; the joke carries fear and fatigue.

### State border

```text
Forgive me, but now this is no longer a jar, it’s a small state border.
```

This keeps the escalation from jar to system.

### Incomplete is better than nothing

```text
“Incomplete is better than nothing.”

“Yes.”
```

Important: the chapter does not reject labels. It rejects replacing seeing with labels.

### Long things stop being read

```text
Long things stop being read.
```

This was kept plain, not aphoristically inflated.

### World readable in advance

```text
But inside that desire something else was already hiding: let the world be readable in advance.

Let it explain itself.

Let a person not risk making a mistake.
```

This is one of the chapter’s conceptual centers. It must not become a lecture; the English keeps it as Sofia’s internal pressure after a scene.

### Forest without signs

```text
Nothing explained itself.

The forest was not secretive.

It simply did not put up signs.
```

This is the chapter’s counterweight to the kitchen list.

### Guidebook line

```text
A guidebook is not to blame if people read it instead of the forest.
```

Preserves Nora’s sharpness without making her a guru.

### Glint line

```text
Then look.
```

The old woman points not to the mark, but to the pot. This must remain simple.

### Recognition line

```text
Recognition was not certainty.

More like attention that knows it can be mistaken.
```

This is a key Chapter 14 formulation and should be checked carefully.

### Ending

```text
The jar had been labelled.

Everything else still had to be recognized.
```

The ending preserves the chapter’s exact movement: partial practical protection, no metaphysical closure.

## 5. Character voice notes

### Sofia

Sofia is not explaining the moral. She notices pressure, air thickening, the difference between label and recognition, and the dangerous tenderness of trying not to lose anything again.

### Thomas

Thomas is anxious, practical, guilty, and still inclined to repair through wording, lists, dates, translations, and procedure. His English should remain clear and slightly tense.

### Nick

Nick’s humor is sharper again, but should never become standalone comic relief. His lines work because they interrupt bureaucratic expansion without denying the real loss.

Important examples:

```text
The expansion of the safety system to the size of the universe.
```

```text
Because I can hear an office opening somewhere in the distance.
```

```text
Amazingly. The list is short and almost does not make me want to seize the world.
```

### Nora

Nora remains precise and quietly cutting, not teacherly. She says simple things that hurt because they are true.

Examples:

```text
People throw away many living things anyway.
```

```text
A guidebook is not to blame if people read it instead of the forest.
```

### Lina

Lina accepts practical measures, but does not let them become false salvation. Her lines should stay spare.

Examples:

```text
Sometimes useful.
```

```text
Long things stop being read.
```

```text
That a label needs care too.
```

### Ivar

Ivar appears late and minimally. He confirms the label is good, but refuses to explain what everyone already knows.

Important line:

```text
You already know.
```

## 8. Controller watchpoints

Please check especially:

1. Whether the title `Label` should remain, or whether `Mark` would better preserve `Метка`.
2. Whether `wall newspaper in three languages` should remain as a slightly foreign administrative joke, or be softened.
3. Whether `starter-protection system with probabilistic spelling` keeps Nick’s humor without sounding too modern/technical.
4. Whether `this is no longer a jar, it’s a small state border` works naturally in English.
5. Whether `A new beginning looked worse than hope` is exact enough and not too poetic.
6. Whether `The words had preserved themselves better than the thing they referred to` keeps the pain without overexplaining.
7. Whether `the human desire not to lose anything anymore` should be `not to lose anything again`; current choice is more literal/strange, intentionally.
8. Whether `read it instead of the forest` preserves Nora’s meaning and naturalness.
9. Whether `Then look` is strong enough for `Тогда смотри`.
10. Whether the final line `Everything else still had to be recognized` is the best rendering of `Остальное по-прежнему нужно было узнавать.`
