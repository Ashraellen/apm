## 1. Chapter function preserved

Chapter 23 continues directly from Chapter 22’s recognition that `Sampo` stands alone. Here the isolated word is tested: David puts it in the center, the arrangement becomes too neat, and Sofia moves it back among the other papers. The chapter does not define Sampo. It shows why definition, center, place, and arrow are no longer accurate enough.

The chapter movement is preserved:

1. Papers lie on the table after the previous recognition.
2. David holds the pencil but does not write.
3. Thomas asks what they are looking for and where it is.
4. Sofia feels the word `where` as too small/inaccurate.
5. Map, trace, question, and place are re-examined without being discarded.
6. David makes a neat scheme with `Sampo?` in the center.
7. Everyone briefly feels relief because form has returned.
8. Sofia sees the form is untrue precisely because it is neat.
9. She moves `Sampo?` out of the center and among the other sheets.
10. The scheme breaks, but becomes more honest.
11. Thomas begins to doubt arrows.
12. David names the movement as `through`.
13. Ivar refuses the wrong question: for now, one should not ask that way.
14. David writes `Сампо —` and stops.
15. The unfinished dash remains not as loss, but as caution.

No structural changes recommended.

## 2. Title decision

Russian:

```text
Глава двадцать третья. Не вещь
```

Chosen English:

```text
Chapter Twenty-Three. Not a Thing
```

Rationale:

`Not a Thing` preserves the directness of the Russian title and resists turning the chapter into abstract philosophy. `No Thing` would sound more mystical/Zen-like and should be avoided. `Not an Object` would be too conceptual. `Not a Thing` is plain, awkward enough, and right.

Recommendation: keep `Not a Thing`.

## 5. Character voice notes

### Sofia

Sofia’s function is not to explain but to move the sheet from center to among. Her hand still aches; the body rejects the false center before language can.

### David

David almost writes, almost defines, almost draws. His restraint is the chapter’s main action. The pencil remains dangerous.

### Thomas

Thomas is still the one who needs a correct question, but he evolves: `Maybe not arrows` is a major step.

### Nick

Nick’s humor guards the chapter from becoming solemn. He accidentally says the true thing: `It was lonely there.`

### Marek

Marek anchors the provisional measure: `For today` and `Sometimes that is a measure.` Sparse, practical.

### Lina

Lina does not join the metaphysical conversation. Her rag being dry becomes the only thing she names as ready.

### Nora

Nora gives permission to leave the dash as it is. Her sentence explains nothing and remains useful.

### Ivar

Ivar appears and refuses to give the right question. He contributes absence of answer, not wisdom-speech. Keep minimal.
