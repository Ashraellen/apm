## Artistic-research framing

Book I is not an academic treatise on Kalevala and not a direct retelling of the epic.

It is a completed work of literary artistic research.

Correct framing:

```text
Sampo uses the Sampo motif to investigate abundance, ownership, participation and the human failure to recognize the living systems that sustain life.
```

Short formula:

```text
Not research about a novel.
Not a novel based on research.
Research becoming a novel.
```

Russian formula:

```text
Не исследование о романе.
Не роман по мотивам исследования.
А исследование, ставшее романом.
```

## Chapter summaries / active memory

Prologue opens with the mythic image of the Earth as a living feeding mechanism and the first human attempt to extract or own what already surrounds and feeds them.

Chapter 1 moves from the modern airport and minibus entrance toward the northern house, Ivar's first appearance, the first fire, and the opening sentence that pulls the group back toward the prologue.

Chapter 2 lets Sampo stop being only a word and become rumor, pressure, hunger, measure, bread, warm snow, soil under the nail, and the first disturbance around what feeds.

Chapter 3 moves from listening into participation through morning life: bread, hand, measure, wood, moss, and the warm piece of bread left on Thomas's plate.

Chapter 4 moves from bread / hand / measure to water / surface / holding, preparing the desire for vessel, form, device and the human wish to hold a living source.

Chapter 5 opens the “Sampo as thing / object” movement: the living source begins to be imagined as something that can be found, held, hidden, guarded or taken.

Chapter 6 continues through the illness of searching: the living world begins to be read as a system of signs and traces.

Chapter 7 moves from trace to scheme: the map gives relief, not truth.

Chapter 8 brings the human-made point on the map into collision with a living place that is full, wet, inconvenient, and alive, but not the answer expected.

Chapter 9 breaks the too-clean conceptual ladder through body, shame and material return.

Chapter 10 closes the first “Sampo as thing / object” block and prepares the transition toward “Sampo as machine” through the first social crack around mine, ours, enough and share.

Chapter 11 opens the “Sampo as machine” movement through dough, yeast, warmth, waiting and the impossibility of ordering a living process.

Chapter 12 continues through recipe versus hands, written memory versus living continuation.

Chapter 13 breaks the intelligent line “recipe -> repetition -> method” with an ordinary external event: the living continuation is thrown away because it is not recognized.

Chapter 14 moves through marking what was not recognized. A mark can help, but it does not replace recognition.

Chapter 15 shows that a mark can stop a hand, but cannot create life. Living things need conditions: place, warmth, air, timing, feeding and attention.

Chapter 16 closes the starter micro-model: care for the living begins to turn into maintaining conditions, and the scale widens from jar to house.

Chapter 17 moves from the house toward the larger living world. Hidden formula: they wanted to keep the living inside the house, but the house turned out to be inside the living.

Chapter 18 shows that the house lives not by sealed reserves but by exchange.

Chapter 19 shifts outward: the living world feeds, but does not have to match human appetite.

Chapter 20 shows that a gift does not arrive as repayment or correction, but as something that could not be demanded.

Chapter 21 moves into participation: participation is not an idea but a hand where without it something becomes worse.

Chapter 22 begins the first quiet movement of recognition after participation. The earlier notes are not false, only too separate.

Chapter 23 breaks the old “where is it?” mode without defining Sampo. The question “where” no longer works.

Chapter 24 closes the main body of Book I with quiet recognition: Sampo is not defined as a thesis, and Ivar does not explain. Final blow:

```text
Сампо не стояло в доме.
Дом стоял в нём.
И никто не сказал этого вслух.
```

Epilogue_v1 is not Chapter 25. It shows the consequences of recognition during the morning of departure. The characters do not explain Sampo or state conclusions. Ayla takes a closed instrument case and listens to water outside, quietly opening Book II without announcement, music, song, or exposition.

Current Book I point-of-view rule:

```text
Book I is told primarily through Sofia as the newcomer's living lens, with controlled shifts to other guests when resistance, silence, memory or recognition require it; Ivar is not a POV character in Book I, and the true center of the book is the gradual recognition of Sampo as Creation.
```

```text
Книга I рассказывается преимущественно через Софию как живую линзу нового гостя; иногда фокус может переходить к другим участникам круга, если сцене нужны сопротивление, молчание, память или узнавание. Ивар в первой книге не является точкой зрения. Его видят другие. Настоящий центр книги — не человек, а постепенное узнавание Сампо как Творения.
```

## Opening location

Book I may begin in the modern frame at a remote northern house / gathering place / mini-camp somewhere in Finland, in forest, near water, or in another quiet northern landscape.

Important correction:

```text
This is not Ivar's house.
Ivar also comes there once a year.
```

The place must not feel like a guru estate, official retreat center, school, sect, monastery, or spiritual tourist lodge.

Current seasonal decision:

```text
late August / early September
northern Finland or another northern forest-water region
not white nights
not deep winter
not postcard Christmas North
```

The land should still be visibly feeding: berries, mushrooms, fish, water, bread, damp moss, grass, cold air, rot and growth at the same time.

## Ivar in Book I

Ivar is not Finnish. He is a person of the world. He loves solitude and his guests. He is not the owner of the place, not a guru, not a formal teacher, and not a POV character. He is the one who starts the first door in the modern frame.
