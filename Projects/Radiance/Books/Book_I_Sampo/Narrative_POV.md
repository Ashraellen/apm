# Narrative POV — Book I: Сампо

This file fixes the current narrative point-of-view decision for **Сияние. Книга I. Сампо**.

---

## Core decision

Book I should not be written as a book "about Ivar".

Book I also should not be written as a completely centerless chorus.

Correct current structure:

```text
The real center of Book I is Sampo / Creation / the gradual recognition of living provision.
The primary human lens is Sofia.
Ivar is not the protagonist and not a POV character in Book I.
```

Russian working formula:

```text
Книга идёт за Софией, но принадлежит не Софии.
```

Sofia carries the reader into the northern place because she stands in the correct position:

- she is new enough to notice;
- not knowing enough to ask silently;
- sensitive enough to register pressure;
- not already converted into a believer;
- close enough to Nik to keep the modern, ironic resistance alive.

She is not the savior of the book.

She is the living lens through which the reader first learns how to see.

---

## Primary modern-layer POV

The main modern-layer point of view in Book I:

```text
third person, close to Sofia
```

This means:

- the narration may follow Sofia's perception closely;
- her uncertainty, attention, bodily unease and recognition may guide the reader;
- her observations can introduce Ivar, the house, the guests, the road and the first manifestations;
- she does not need to understand what she sees immediately.

Do not make her an omniscient interpreter.

She should often notice more than she can explain.

---

## Controlled shifts

The book may use controlled shifts to other guests when needed.

Possible secondary lenses:

- **Nik** — skepticism, irony, resistance, defensive intelligence;
- **Nora** — silence, hidden knowledge, late speech, threshold perception;
- **Thomas** — rational pressure, direct questions, need for explanation;
- **Eva / Lina** — traces of earlier visits, returning knowledge, partial memory;
- **Marek** — logistics, dry threshold, transition, external pressure.

These shifts must be intentional.

They should serve scene pressure, not scatter the book into equal viewpoints.

Correct rule:

```text
Sofia is the primary lens.
Other guests are controlled apertures.
Ivar is seen from outside.
```

---

## Ivar and POV

Ivar should not receive internal POV in Book I.

Do not enter his mind in the first book.

He should be shown through:

- Sofia's first impression;
- practical action;
- body and movement;
- voice;
- silence;
- the way other guests react to him;
- fragments returning people say about him;
- what happens in the room after he speaks or refuses to speak.

Reason:

```text
If the narration enters Ivar's mind too early, he becomes explained.
If he becomes explained, he loses threshold force.
```

Ivar's appearance should be introduced primarily through a new guest's eyes, most likely Sofia.

Preferred method:

```text
not a passport description;
not a fantasy portrait;
not a guru entrance;
not objective character dossier.
```

Better:

```text
Sofia notices a practical action first: firewood, cup, door, chair, fire.
Then the body becomes visible: height, weight, shaved temples, long braid, silver in dark hair, attentive living face.
```

Possible internal model:

```text
Sofia first sees how he occupies space, not what he symbolizes.
```

---

## Mythic layer

The mythic layer may have its own dense narrative voice.

It should not feel like Sofia dreaming everything.

It should also not become a detached textbook retelling.

Working rule:

```text
modern layer = primarily Sofia;
mythic layer = independent living voice;
manifestation = collision between the two.
```

When myth enters the modern layer, Sofia may register its pressure, but the myth should not be reduced to her psychology.

---

## Structural warning

Avoid both wrong extremes:

### Wrong extreme 1

```text
The book becomes Ivar's book.
```

This would turn him into a guru, author-avatar, or hidden main character.

### Wrong extreme 2

```text
The book has no human lens.
```

This would scatter the reader across twelve guests, Ivar, myth, house, forest and Sampo with no breathing path.

### Correct balance

```text
Human entrance through Sofia.
Meaning center in Sampo / Creation.
Threshold opening through Ivar.
Choral pressure through the guests.
Mythic depth through manifestations.
```

## One-sentence canon

```text
Book I is told primarily through Sofia as the newcomer's living lens, with controlled shifts to other guests when resistance, silence, memory or recognition require it; Ivar is not a POV character in Book I, and the true center of the book is the gradual recognition of Sampo as Creation.
```

Russian canon:

```text
Книга I рассказывается преимущественно через Софию как живую линзу нового гостя; иногда фокус может переходить к другим участникам круга, если сцене нужны сопротивление, молчание, память или узнавание. Ивар в первой книге не является точкой зрения. Его видят другие. Настоящий центр книги — не человек, а постепенное узнавание Сампо как Творения.
```
