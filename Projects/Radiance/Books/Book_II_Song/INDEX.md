## 5. Core Book II movement

Book II moves from sound as performance / technique / effect toward song as relation.

Working formula:

```text
Book I: Sampo cannot be taken as a thing.
Book II: Song cannot be taken as sound.
```

Another internal formula:

```text
Sofia learned to recognize.
Ayla must learn to hear.
```

Do not state these formulas directly inside the novel unless they become scene, object, bodily reaction, silence, or failure.

## 6. Primary lens of Book II

Ayla is the primary human lens of Book II.

Canon:

```text
Ayla is a Polish musician living in Warsaw.
Professionally, she is primarily a cymbalist.
She plays many instruments, especially unusual, old, and rare ones.
Most instruments belong to work: performance, recording, technique, commissions, reliability.
Kantele is different.
Kantele is her personal instrument of inner hearing.
She knows the northern epic and came to Ivar because she was searching for a new relation with sound.
She is not Finnish, not a folklore ornament, not a priestess of northern music.
She is a modern Polish musician whose professional command of sound has become insufficient.
```

Current kantele decision:

```text
small 15-string kantele
not a concert instrument
not a toy
portable, personal, sufficient, resistant
```

## 7. Ivar and Ayla

Ivar also lives in Warsaw or is regularly present there.

Ayla and Ivar sometimes meet. Ayla asks him questions about sound, song, epic, instrument, silence, and the difference between performing and hearing. Ivar answers briefly. His answers should not close her questions; they should make the questions more exact and more difficult.

This must not become a teacher/student or guru/disciple model.

Correct dynamic:

```text
Ayla asks about song.
Ivar does not teach her song.
He prevents her from turning song into an answer too early.
```

---

## 8. What Book II must avoid

Do not turn Book II into:

- a retelling of Kalevala;
- an academic lecture on epic and music;
- a fantasy music school;
- a guru / ученица relationship;
- a mystical workshop;
- a folklore postcard;
- a concert novel;
- a direct announcement of Väinämöinen;
- a story where kantele is only a Finnish symbol;
- a book where Ayla simply “plays beautifully” and everyone understands;
- an explanation of Book I conclusions.
