Do not turn the project into a retelling of the Kalevala.

```text
a philosophical-mythopoetic novel / a northern tale about the true history of the world,

not inventions to be retold.
```

```text
Fairy tales contain the true history of the world.
People called it myth because they forgot the language in which it had been written.
```

## 3. Book I “Sampo”: compressed context

Book I title:

```text
Radiance
Northern Tales of the True History of the World
Book I. Sampo
```

This is not a Kalevala retelling.

The Kalevala and the northern epic are treated as traces of a true history, distorted by human memory.

Main question of Book I:

```text
Why does the human being search for a machine of abundance while standing inside Creation, which already feeds him?
```

Book I movement:

```text
from Sampo as object
through Sampo as abundance machine
into Sampo as Creation itself
```

But the text must not state too early: “Sampo = Creation.”

The reader must first desire Sampo as an object, then see the sickness of ownership, then feel the impossibility of separating Sampo from the living world.

Book I formula:

```text
They searched for Sampo as a machine.
They lived inside Sampo as Creation.
They wanted provision without participation.
```

Book I ended with recognition, not explanation.

Final movement of Chapter 24:

```text
Sampo did not stand inside the house.
The house stood inside it.
And no one said this aloud.
```

The Book I epilogue takes place on the morning of departure. The characters prepare to leave the forest house. They do not make conclusions. They carry the consequences of recognition.

The epilogue’s final gesture belongs to Ayla:

```text
Outside, the minibus was waiting.
Water moved past the house so quietly that it could be taken for ordinary noise.
Ayla did not take it that way.
She said nothing.
And stepped out.
```

This is the bridge into Book II.

---

## 4. Important note about the ending of Book I

The characters have not understood everything.

They must not become enlightened.

They must not say:

```text
We understood that Sampo is Creation.
Now it is clear that one must not search for a thing.
The main thing is participation.
Everything will be different now.
```

Correct state:

```text
They can no longer see in the old way,
but they do not yet know how to live in the new way.
```

This is crucial for Book II.

Book II begins after Sampo. It is not a prequel.

Ayla’s past and her meetings with Ivar may be revealed through memory, questions, brief returns, and echoes, but the main timeline continues after the events of Book I.

## 5. Core formula of Book II “Song”

Book II title:

```text
Book II. Song
```

Its main internal transition:

```text
Book I: Sampo cannot be taken as a thing.
Book II: Song cannot be taken as sound.
```

Or:

```text
Book I taught recognition.
Book II must teach hearing.
```

Do not place these formulas directly in the novel.

They must become scene, body, instrument, error, silence, failed performance, memory, Warsaw, water, strings, work, fatigue, or a meeting with Ivar.

Main question of Book II:

```text
Can a song be heard if the human being still wants to own sound?
```

Another internal question:

```text
What happens to a musician who knows how to produce sound but no longer trusts the act of producing it?
```

## 6. Main character of Book II: Ayla

Ayla is the primary human lens of Book II.

She appeared in Book I and the epilogue, but remained mostly silent.

In Chapter 24 she said:

```text
“It sounds different.”
```

In the epilogue she left with a closed instrument case and did not accept water as ordinary noise.

Ayla canon:

```text
Ayla is a Polish musician.
She lives in Warsaw.
Professionally, she is primarily a cymbalist.
She plays many instruments.
She is drawn to unusual, old, ancient, and rare instruments.
Most instruments belong to work.
Kantele is different.
Kantele is not her work instrument; it is her personal instrument of inner hearing.
She knows the northern epic.
She came to Ivar because she is searching for a new relation with sound.
```

Ayla is not Finnish.

Ayla is not a “northern priestess.”

Ayla is not a decorative folklore figure.

Ayla is not Ivar’s mystical disciple.

She is a modern Polish musician whose professional command of sound has become insufficient.

---

## 7. Ayla and instruments

Ayla plays many instruments, but professionally she is primarily a cymbalist.

Cymbals are her professional body.

They are connected with:

- technique;
- stage;
- commissions;
- recording;
- precision;
- craft;
- income;
- the need to produce the required sound at the required moment.

Kantele is different.

Kantele must not become an ethnic signboard.

Kantele is an instrument before which Ayla does not feel like an owner.

Working formula:

```text
On the cymbals, Ayla worked.
The kantele, she listened to.
```

Or:

```text
The cymbals answered if she struck correctly.
The kantele remained silent even when she did everything correctly.
```

Selected kantele type:

```text
small 15-string kantele
```

Reason:

```text
not a toy,
not a concert machine,
sufficient for a real musician,
small enough that mastery cannot hide behind abundance,
personal enough to be an instrument of the soul rather than of the stage.
```

Formula:

```text
Fifteen strings: enough for sound to argue with the hand.
```

## 8. Ayla and the epic

Ayla knows the northern epic.

She knows about Sampo, Väinämöinen, kantele, song, the force of the word, and the ancient relation with sound.

But before the events of Book I, this knowledge was still external.

It may have been:

- musical;
- cultural;
- artistic;
- research-based;
- professional;
- personal, but not fully lived.

After Sampo, this knowledge begins to become dangerously personal.

She came to Ivar not for mysticism, not for folklore exoticism, and not for a northern retreat.

She came because she suspected:

```text
in the old songs, music was not merely art,
but a way of entering into relation with the world through sound.
```

Her search:

```text
She is not searching for new music.
She is searching for a new relation with sound.
```

## 9. Ayla and Ivar in Warsaw

Ivar is also connected with Warsaw: he lives there or is regularly present there.

Ayla and Ivar sometimes meet.

Ayla asks him questions:

- about sound;
- about song;
- about epic;
- about kantele;
- about silence;
- about why an instrument does not sound when she does everything correctly;
- about whether one can play without owning sound;
- about why in the epic song does more than a human being.

Ivar answers briefly.

But his answers must not become lessons.

He is not a teacher in the direct sense.

He is not a guru.

He does not teach her to sing.

He makes the question more exact and more difficult.

Correct dynamic:

```text
Ayla asks about song.
Ivar does not give her song.
He does not let her turn song into an answer too early.
```

Possible Ivar-like replies:

```text
You want sound too quickly.
First listen to what does not play.
The instrument does not owe you an answer just because you know how.
You are asking about music. But it is not about music.
If you are looking for song, do not ask it to sound first.
```

In the novel, such lines must be used carefully, not as a collection of aphorisms.

## 10. Why Ayla went to the forest house

Ayla may have gone to Ivar’s forest house because she had long been asking him about sound.

But he did not take her there “for training.”

Best internal explanation:

```text
Ayla came to Ivar with a question about sound.
He brought her to a place where first one had to see what cannot be taken.
```

Book I showed:

```text
Sampo cannot be taken as a thing.
```

Book II must continue:

```text
Song cannot be taken as sound.
```

Therefore her question about song begins not with song, but with Sampo.

## 11. How Book II should begin

Book II should begin after Book I.

Best direction:

```text
Warsaw.
Ayla has returned from the forest house.
Work instruments are nearby.
The cymbals as professional reality.
The closed or opened kantele case.
She cannot simply continue working as before.
```

Do not begin with a biographical explanation.

Do not begin with “Ayla was a Polish musician...”.

Begin with a scene.

Possible first movement:

```text
Ayla returns to her apartment or studio.
There is a commission / rehearsal / recording / need to work with sound.
She knows how to work.
But now sound has stopped being merely material.
The kantele stands apart.
She does not open the case for a long time.
```

Backstory should appear through flashes of memory:

- first meeting with Ivar in Warsaw;
- an old question about the epic;
- a conversation about song;
- why she chose the kantele;
- why the cymbals became work;
- why she went to the forest house.

The past should appear only when it begins to work in the present.

## 13. What Book II must not do

Do not:

- begin as a prequel;
- give Ayla’s biography as a long exposition;
- turn Ayla into a Finnish priestess of kantele;
- make Ivar a music teacher;
- turn Ayla and Ivar’s meetings into lessons;
- use the Kalevala as a plot cheat-sheet;
- immediately introduce Väinämöinen as an explicit character or announced theme;
- begin with beautiful kantele playing;
- make music a magic button;
- make song into power without critique;
- explain directly that “Song is relation”;
- repeat Sofia’s path one-to-one;
- turn sound into a new version of the Sampo-machine.

Main risk of Book II:

```text
after people stopped taking Sampo as a thing,
they may try to take the living through sound.
```

## 14. Possible nerve of the first scene

Ayla returns to Warsaw.

The city sounds different.

Not mystically.

Ordinarily:

- tram;
- stairwell;
- water in pipes;
- neighbor’s door;
- phone;
- work message;
- instrument cases;
- cymbals;
- commissioned recording;
- kantele case by the wall.

She has to work.

But after the forest house it is difficult for her to treat sound as material.

She may open a work instrument first, not the kantele.

And understand: the hand knows what to do, but hearing no longer agrees.

The kantele stands apart.

Fifteen strings are silent.

The silence is not empty.
