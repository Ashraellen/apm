# Book2_Na_Vse_Volya_Ego — Error404

This file fixes the current canon for **Book II** of the Error404 cycle.

Working title:

**На всё воля Его**

English working sense:

**All Is His Will** / **It Is All His Will**

Final English title is not locked yet.

---

## 1. Status

Book II is now planned as a separate novel in the Error404 cycle, not as a direct Vlad-centered continuation.

Main protagonist:

**a priest / clergyman of undefined confession**

The confession should remain deliberately undefined enough for the book to work across languages and cultures.

The priest is not a caricature.
He is not a stupid fanatic.
He is not an anti-religious target.
He is sincere, disciplined, frightened, intelligent, and trapped inside the form through which he learned to understand God.

---

## 2. Core premise

The priest has repeated and used the formula:

```text
На всё воля Его.
```

At first this phrase functions as consolation, discipline, and spiritual restraint.

But one day the phrase turns absolute.

Main question:

```text
If absolutely everything is His will, where does sin come from?
```

The priest enters Window №7 not through Vlad's legal complaint, but through a theological fracture.

His question is not simply:

```text
Why me?
```

His question is:

```text
If all is His will, what exactly have we been calling sin?
```

---

## 3. The dangerous theological fracture

The priest's reasoning:

```text
If sin is outside His will, then there is a place outside His will.
If there is a place outside His will, His will is not absolute.
If His will is absolute, sin cannot exist as an independent force against Him in the simple church-pedagogical sense.
If sin exists only as a system of restraint, then what was I teaching, confessing, warning against, and naming for all these years?
```

This must not become an anti-religious pamphlet.

But the book must go along the edge of rupture.

Rule:

```text
Do not attack God.
Do not flatten faith.
Do not mock living belief.
But do not soften the crack to protect the form.
```

The truth of the book comes from the fact that the question is born inside faith, not outside it.

---

## 4. What the book is really about

The book is not about proving that religion is false.

It is about the moment when a working religious system of restraint collides with the absolute idea of divine will.

Key distinction:

```text
A system may restrain people and still not be the Absolute Truth.
A form may be useful and still not be God.
A doctrine may function as a fence and still fail when brought before infinity.
```

The priest sees that the concept of sin, as commonly used in church instruction, may operate as a system of checks and balances.

It works because people hold themselves back by fear, shame, duty, guilt, hope, obedience, and the desire to remain acceptable.

But the priest begins to see that this useful system does not fully survive the phrase:

```text
На всё воля Его.
```

---

## 5. The priest's internal rupture

The priest does not immediately leave, reject, or mock the system.

He tries to stay inside it and save the formula.

He tries to classify the question as:

- temptation;
- demonic thought;
- spiritual delusion;
- pride of mind;
- dangerous reasoning;
- attack on faith;
- lack of humility;
- forbidden curiosity.

But the question does not disappear.

Formula:

```text
He does not become possessed by a demon.
He becomes possessed by the defense against the demon.
```

Or:

```text
He fights not the devil, but the question he cannot exorcise.
```

---

## 6. Chancellery manifestation in Book II

There should be no screen as the main interface.

The screen belongs more naturally to Vlad's legal-digital world.

For the priest, the Chancellery manifests as a **second layer of thought**.

Not a voice.
Not a vision.
Not text on a screen.
Not a hallucinated external speech.

It appears as a thought inside thought — already complete, fuller than his own thought, clearly felt as arriving from outside the ordinary thinking process.

Working formula:

```text
A thought appears in him, but not from him.
It does not sound.
It is simply already there.
```

The priest experiences these thoughts as potentially supernatural, dangerous, or demonic because they are too calm, too precise, and too impossible to dismiss.

---

## 7. The squared notebook

The priest is a pedant.

He begins recording these "other" thoughts in a squared notebook.

The notebook may be labelled cautiously, for example:

```text
Notes on thoughts
For discernment
Private observations
Do not open without necessity
```

The notebook is important because he tries to make the impossible manageable.

Vlad wanted screenshots and attachable evidence.

The priest wants a disciplined notebook.

Function:

```text
He tries to put infinity into a grid.
```

The squared notebook is funny, human, and spiritually exact.

---

## 8. Tone of the Chancellery-thoughts

Book II must move away from Vlad's dry digital-bureaucratic tone.

For the priest, the answers should be more inward, paternal, almost ecclesial, but still sharp.

Not sentimental.
Not syrupy.
Not sermon-like.
Not theatrical.

Correct tone:

```text
soft, but unsafe;
paternal, but cutting;
clear, but not explanatory;
gentle, but impossible to evade.
```

The thought should not flatter him.
It should not comfort him too quickly.
It should ask the exact question he is trying to avoid.

Example:

```text
Сын мой, уточни: грех находится вне воли Его или внутри неё?
```

## 9. Canonical forms of address

The following address forms are approved for Book II.

They should not be varied randomly.
Each form should correspond to the layer of the priest being addressed.

```text
Сын мой          — базовое отеческое обращение.
Ищущий Меня      — когда речь о самом поиске Бога.
Служитель мой    — когда вопрос касается его служения.
Пастырь          — когда речь об ответственности за других.
Человек божий    — когда с него снимается сан и роль.
Чадо моё         — редкий церковно-отеческий оттенок.
Возлюбленный сын — очень редко, в самых глубоких местах.
```

Do not use:

```text
Раб мой
```

Reason:

It is too risky for a multilingual cycle and creates the wrong social/historical resonance. The book needs theological danger, not unnecessary wording damage.

## 10. Multilingual awareness

The Error404 cycle is multilingual.

Book II address forms must be translatable, or at least adaptable, into other languages without losing tone.

Possible translation logic:

```text

UA: Сину мій
```

## 12. The edge of rupture

Book II must walk along the edge of rupture.

It must not become a safe respectful theological essay.
It must not become an anti-religious pamphlet.

The correct place is between them:

```text
The book should be dangerous because the question is honest.
```

The priest's breakdown must feel real.

He begins to "rage" not because he is possessed, but because he cannot understand how a thought that feels more alive than his defense can be dangerous, and how a question that threatens doctrine can also feel closer to God.

The deeper horror:

```text
He prays not for God to answer,
but for the question to stop.
```

Future books:

```text
As the Chancellery gives.
```

Vlad may reappear in the cycle, but Book II is not Vlad-centered.

---

## 14. Practical warning

When writing Book II:

- do not make the priest stupid;
- do not turn him into a religious clown;
- do not attack religion from outside;
- do not protect the form from the living question;
- do not use screen notifications as the main device;
- use the squared notebook as his way of trying to control the impossible;
- keep the inner second-layer thoughts distinct from ordinary thoughts;
- keep humor on the edge of pain;
- let the question become impossible to exorcise.

Final formula:

```text
He served the formula "На всё воля Его" until the word "всё" opened and looked back at him.
```
