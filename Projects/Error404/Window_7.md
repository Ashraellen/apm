# Window_7 — Error404

Source document: `ERROR_404_GOD_NOT_FOUND_project_sources_v12_FINAL_NO_CUTS.md`

This file contains the canon and mechanism of **Window №7 / Окно №7**, including the waiting hall, non-conversations, personal forms of the Chancellery, and answers through coincidence between external text and inner question.

---

## 1. Core principle

Window №7 is not a physical office.

It opens in a **state**.

The Chancellery is not “somewhere.” It manifests where a person can no longer avoid asking the question.

For Vlad, this appears as:
- waiting hall;
- corridor;
- queue;
- ticket number;
- window;
- application;
- operator;
- category of complaint.

Because Vlad’s form of appeal is legalistic: complaint, registration, responsible office, evidence, and procedure.

Other people may experience the same state differently or not consciously experience a “hall” at all.

---

## 2. The category

Window №7 handles claims under the category:

**Почему я? / Why me?**

This is not a noble philosophical question at first.

It is the core human complaint:
- why me?
- why again me?
- why not someone else?
- why did this happen to me specifically?
- why does my suffering not generate official recognition?

---

## 3. The waiting hall

The waiting hall of Window №7 should feel wrong.

It should not feel like an ordinary queue of living people who can chat, introduce themselves, and respond normally.

It should feel like a place where **lonelinesses sit next to each other**.

Formula:

```text
Влад говорит.
Голос выходит.
Проходит совсем немного.
И исчезает.

Не эхом.
Не ответом.
Не паузой перед нормальным разговором.

А именно проваливается в мягкий, глухой слой зала.
```

---

## 4. The cotton hall / ватный зал

Voices in the hall do not behave like voices in ordinary space.

When Vlad asks a question aloud, his words do not fly toward another person and create a normal dialogue.

They sink into a thick grey cotton layer.

People should not react to Vlad as ordinary interlocutors:
- no immediate turning toward him;
- no normal eye contact;
- no ordinary conversational rhythm;
- no living social exchange.

The hall must not be a place where people simply talk.

---

## 5. Non-conversations

All “conversations” in Window №7 should be built as **non-conversations**.

Visitors do not see and hear each other in the normal sense.

Each is inside:
- their question;
- their pain;
- their claim;
- their inner room.

They may say phrases that look like answers to Vlad’s questions, but those phrases actually belong to their own inner monologue.

Sometimes they match Vlad’s question exactly.
Sometimes crookedly.
Sometimes almost beside the point.
Sometimes they sound like an answer while coming from another context entirely.

Key formula:

```text
Люди не отвечают на вопросы.
Их внутренние монологи случайно пересекают вопросы Влада.

Иногда точно.
Иногда криво.
Иногда страшно точно именно потому, что криво.
```

---

## 6. How to build their lines

Ordinary dialogue is wrong.

Wrong:

```text
— Вы здесь давно?
— Давно.
```

Correct:

```text
— Вы здесь давно? — спрашивает Влад.

Человек не смотрит на него.
Вопрос тонет.
Пауза.
Человек произносит в своё пространство:

— Я сначала думал, что это закончится само.
```

This does not directly answer “for how long,” but it answers the deeper state: the person has been inside waiting for a long time.

Another model:

```text
— Вы здесь уже были?

Женщина не смотрит на него, откручивает крышку термоса и говорит:

— Первый раз не взяла термос. Потом поняла.
```

Again, not a normal answer, but it reveals she has been there before.

Another model:

```text
— Вы что-то написали?

Девушка смотрит в смятый листок и говорит:

— Если написать "я не виновата", они спросят, кто виноват.
```

## 7. People come from different places in life

On the earthly level, people in the waiting hall may be anywhere:
- lying in a bath and staring at the ceiling;
- stopped at a traffic light, not hearing the honking;
- sitting in front of a turned-on TV but seeing nothing;
- washing dishes and rubbing one plate too long;
- sitting in a car in a parking lot, unable to get out;
- smoking on a balcony and looking at other windows;
- standing in a church but no longer hearing the service;
- lying beside a person they no longer feel close to.

At that moment, all of them are in one state:
They are asking “Why me?”

Not always in words.
Sometimes by body.
Sometimes by silence.
Sometimes by being unable to move.

---

## 8. Do not explain this in Chapter 2

Very important:
In Chapter 2, do **not** explain the mechanism directly.

Do not write:
- they are all in different places;
- this happens in consciousness;
- the hall is a form of state;
- everyone sees their own version.

In Chapter 2, leave the strangeness.

The reader should notice:
- people do not look at Vlad;
- they do not react like living interlocutors;
- his voice sinks;
- answers sound like answers, but not quite;
- phrases sometimes come from elsewhere;
- everyone seems to speak to themselves;
- dialogue exists, but true dialogue does not.

The reader must feel the mechanism before understanding it.

---

## 9. Later revelation

Later in the novel, the principle can be revealed:

- the Chancellery is not a place, but a form of appeal;
- Window №7 opens in a state;
- people come into it from different points of life;
- everyone sees their own version;
- some see nothing at all and simply get stuck in the question.

This creates delayed recognition:
first unease, then understanding, then impact.

---

## 10. Window №7 and the omnipresent Chancellery

Window №7 connects to the broader principle:
The Chancellery does not belong to any device or place.

It can manifest through any surface of perception:
- phone;
- television;
- kettle;
- elevator;
- receipt;
- traffic light;
- lamp;
- advertising board;
- random phrase;
- pause;
- glitch;
- silence.

But it appears precisely when inner pressure reaches the threshold.

---

## 11. Answer through coincidence

The Chancellery does not always alter reality.
Sometimes it highlights what already exists.

Formula:

```text
Все слышат одни слова.
Но ответ слышит только тот, у кого внутри уже был вопрос.
```

External phrase + internal question = answer.

Example:
Vlad writes in a legal text:
> Заявитель не требует компенсации, однако просит признать...

At the same time TV says in a normal news context:
> ...сторона заявляет, что не требует возмещения, однако настаивает на официальном признании нарушения...

For someone else this is just a news fragment.
For Vlad it is an answer.

Not because the TV spoke to him personally.
Not because the words changed.
But because the external phrase aligned exactly with his internal question.

---

## 12. Boundaries

Do not turn Window №7 or the Chancellery into cheap mysticism.

Avoid:
- direct personal TV messages like “Vlad, specify your question”;
- constant talking objects;
- paper magically writing itself;
- every item becoming a mystical artifact;
- perfect telepathic chat between visitors.

Keep the boundary of doubt.

The answer should feel like meaning becoming visible through reality, not like a circus of supernatural props.

---

## 13. Working formula for Chapter 2

Chapter 2 should work like this:

Vlad sees the hall.
Vlad believes he is in the hall.
Vlad tries to speak.
But conversations do not become living communication.

People nearby say phrases, but not to him.
Each speaks from inside their own question.
Sometimes another person’s phrase becomes an answer to Vlad.
Sometimes an answer comes from a phrase from another story.

Vlad begins to suspect:
**no one here is really talking.**

But he does not yet understand why.

That is correct.
