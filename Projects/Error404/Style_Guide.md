# Style_Guide — Error404

Source document: `ERROR_404_GOD_NOT_FOUND_project_sources_v12_FINAL_NO_CUTS.md`

This file contains tone, rhythm, style rules, prohibitions, and practical writing principles for **Error 404: God Not Found / Ошибка 404: Бог не найден**.

---

## 1. Core tone

The main tone:

**First laugh, then think. Or think, then laugh. Ideally both at once.**

The reader should not immediately understand where exactly they were caught.

The book must be:
- funny on the surface;
- serious in depth;
- bureaucratic in texture;
- digital in interface;
- apocryphal in atmosphere;
- painfully human underneath the joke.

---

## 2. What the satire is aimed at

This is not anti-religious satire.

The book does **not** mock God.

It mocks the human habit of turning God into:
- service;
- guarantee;
- certificate;
- contract;
- compensation;
- ideology;
- emergency button;
- supreme complaints department for life.

Do not flatten the book into “religion is business” or “there is no God in churches.”

Correct position:
**The seed is in everything. The problem begins when a person worships the packaging.**

---

## 3. Main writing principle

Do not turn the novel into a collection of sketches about heavenly bureaucracy.

Every joke must lead deeper.

Model:

1. First funny.
2. Then painful.
3. Then serious.

Example logic:

Funny:
> The Department of Miracles is closed for inventory.

Painful:
> Most miracles were returned to sender because they did not match recipient expectations.

Serious:
> A person asked for salvation, but did not accept it because it came in the form of loss.

---

## 4. Scene rule

Do not explain when the scene can show.

The novel must not become a lecture about God, ego, religion, bureaucracy, or awakening.

Correct method:
- show Vlad trying to formalize pain;
- show the system answering in dry bureaucratic language;
- show earthly religious forms with both seed and packaging;
- show human contradiction through small exact actions;
- let the philosophical meaning arrive through recognition.

Wrong method:
- explaining the mechanism directly too early;
- turning characters into mouthpieces;
- making Vlad too wise too soon;
- making religious people flat frauds;
- making mystical events too obvious or theatrical.

---

## 5. Vlad’s starting tone

At the beginning Vlad is not a philosopher who is almost enlightened.

He is:
- irritated;
- tired;
- язвительный;
- wounded;
- a бытовой истец;
- a man who thinks suffering gives him the right to demand an explanation.

He argues with Heavenly support like with a stupid internet-provider bot, not like a disciple ready for revelation.

Key inner formula:
> Я страдал, значит у меня есть право требовать объяснений.

The stronger his ordinary claimant-energy is at the beginning, the deeper his later break will be.

---

## 6. Humor

Humor must be dry, precise, bureaucratic, and human.

It should often work through mismatch:
- sacred content in support-service form;
- mystical answer in administrative language;
- existential despair treated as a registered complaint;
- divine silence translated into interface behavior;
- human ego exposed through a technically polite message.

The humor must not become circus.

The system should not be “wacky.” It should be polite, dry, impossible, and terrifyingly accurate.

---

## 7. Bureaucratic system language

System language should be:
- polite;
- dry;
- exact;
- slightly absurd;
- spiritually unpleasant;
- not verbose unless used as an intertitle/protocol.

Examples:

> Пожалуйста, не создавайте повторные обращения.  
> Это не ускорит проверку алгоритмов вашей души.

> Зафиксировано: пользователь снова перепутал источник с официальным пунктом выдачи.

> Форма не является ошибкой. Ошибка начинается, когда форма объявляет себя источником.

> Пользователь предъявляет претензию к подарку, предварительно объявив его своей собственностью.

---

## 8. Intertitles and system logs

Materials from the “director’s cut” can be used inside the novel as:
- extracts from registers;
- service notes;
- acts of Heavenly support;
- protocols checking soul algorithms;
- cases from the Department of Rejected Theophanies;
- fragments from the Catalogue of Unrecognized Theophanies;
- prayer archive entries;
- system notifications;
- dry official forms.

Function:
- strengthen the previous chapter;
- prepare the next chapter;
- give the novel the texture of a mystical digital document;
- connect prose with heavenly bureaucracy;
- create the feeling that the reader is holding a fragment of a closed archive.

Do not overuse them. The main text must keep moving.

---

## 9. Law of the Seed in style

Never write the religious/spiritual world as simply false.

The living seed may be inside:
- church;
- mosque;
- synagogue;
- ashram;
- kitchen;
- taxi;
- prison;
- hospital;
- YouTube channel;
- joke;
- kebab shop.

The problem is not form.

The problem begins when a person takes form for source, package for content, institution for God, ritual for presence, and tradition for exclusive franchise.

---

## 10. Dead form and living spark

Use the idea of religious/spiritual systems as working prototypes.

A form can have:
- participants;
- roles;
- texts;
- rituals;
- symbols;
- language;
- rules;
- artifacts;
- memory;
- ways of including new participants.

But the construction itself is not life.

It needs the Spark of the Living.

Key formula:
> Форма собрана корректно. Искра Живого не обнаружена. Рекомендуется прекратить имитацию горения.

Do not repeat the Frankenstein metaphor too often. It is strong, but must remain a precise strike, not a costume.

---

## 11. Chancellery manifestations

The Chancellery does not live in the phone.

The phone is only one surface through which it can manifest because Vlad is used to perceiving the phone as an interface of reality.

The Chancellery can manifest through:
- phone;
- television;
- kettle;
- elevator;
- receipt;
- traffic light;
- radio;
- random phrase;
- pause;
- glitch;
- reflected text;
- any event that becomes a surface of answer.

But do not turn this into household poltergeist.

Principle:
**The Chancellery appears precisely when Vlad’s inner pressure reaches a threshold.**

---

## 12. Disappearing notifications

Messages of the Chancellery:
- do not come from a normal app;
- have no stable sender;
- are not saved in notification history;
- cannot be reopened;
- leave no normal technical trace;
- may appear over the screen;
- exist only in the moment of perception;
- disappear after reading, swiping, or looking away.

Formula:
**The Chancellery answers in a way that cannot be attached to the case file.**

This is especially important because Vlad is a lawyer. He needs trace, document, screenshot, proof. The Chancellery gives recognition, not evidence.

---

## 13. Answer through coincidence

The Chancellery does not always create a message.

Sometimes it highlights an already existing phrase.

Formula:
**Everyone hears the same words. Only the one who already has the question hears the answer.**

Do not make television, radio, or random people speak personally to Vlad too often.

Better:
- the phrase has a normal earthly context;
- everyone hears the same thing;
- for someone else it is background;
- for Vlad it lands exactly on his inner question.

This preserves ambiguity and avoids cheap mysticism.

---

## 14. Main prohibitions

Do not:
- make Vlad enlightened too early;
- make Poshpshekhon a guru;
- make the silent second man explain anything;
- make the Chancellery a physical app;
- make Window №7 an ordinary queue of people talking normally;
- make religious people flat villains or frauds;
- turn scenes into lectures;
- explain all metaphysics at first appearance;
- overuse direct miracles;
- overuse talking objects;
- smooth out satire, pressure, ambiguity, or discomfort.

---

## 15. Main practical rule

When writing or editing a scene, check:

1. Is it funny on the surface?
2. Is there pain under the joke?
3. Does it reveal Vlad’s mechanism rather than explain it?
4. Does the sacred stay alive under the satire?
5. Is the form shown as form, not as the source?
6. Is the system dry enough?
7. Is the scene still human?

If yes — the scene belongs to Error404.
