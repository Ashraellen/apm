### Flexible source-language rule

Accepted decision:

```text
RU = semantic base and final reference for meaning / tone / authorial intention.
EN v3 = international working support version, especially for bureaucratic-tech layer.
Target language = independent literary transcreation.
```

Not every translation must be produced directly from Russian.

Working logic:

- `UK`, `BE`, and often `PL` are usually easier and more accurate from `RU`, with optional checking against `EN v3` for interface/system language.
- `DE`, `FR`, `ES`, and `PT` may often be easier from `EN v3`, with mandatory checking against `RU` for meaning, pain, irony, religious imagery, and character logic.
- English must not replace Russian as the true authorial source; it is a support version, not a new original.

## 4. Characters

### Vlad

Accepted working character principle:

- Vlad is not a sage at the beginning.
- He is a lawyer-like, irritated claimant against reality.
- He tries to formalize suffering as a document.
- He wants an answer, but often really wants confirmation of his own version.
- His sarcasm must come from fatigue and pain, not from polished sitcom cleverness.
- He must not become too heroic, too enlightened, too noble, too slick, or too Netflix-like.

Translation note:

- In English and other target languages, Vlad’s speech should remain human, tired, dry, sarcastic, and sometimes awkward.
- Do not make him a stand-up comedian.
- Do not make him spiritually mature too early.

## 5. Canon / universe

### Heavenly Chancellery / Небесная Канцелярия

Accepted principle:

- It is not simply a place and not a fantasy office.
- It is a state / mode of reality.
- It manifests through phone, screen, notifications, windows, queues, corridors, signs, status messages, and bureaucratic interfaces.
- The miracle should feel almost like a technical glitch.
- Mysticism must remain dry and domestic, not spectacular.

Correct tone:

```text
Чудо происходит почти как технический сбой.
```

### System language

The system does not speak like a person.

It:

- registers;
- receives;
- sorts;
- redirects;
- notifies;
- asks to specify;
- reports status;
- does not comfort;
- does not over-explain;
- does not recognize absurdity as absurdity.

System phrases must be dry, correct, and therefore especially cruel.

### Window No. 7 / Окно №7

Accepted mechanism from this chat:

- Window No. 7 handles the category “Why me?”
- It belongs to claims / grievances / complaints under the existential category of personal suffering and perceived injustice.
- It registers, sorts, and redirects; it does not provide final answers.
- It asks for context and category clarification.
- It can distinguish between answer vs confirmation of applicant’s version.
- It should not be too magical. It is an interface of metaphysical bureaucracy.

Accepted English formula for sign in Chapter 1 v3:

```text
WINDOW NO. 7
CLAIMS & GRIEVANCES: SECTION "WHY ME?"
```

Accepted related request terms from Chapter 2 old version:

```text
Why exactly like this
Why exactly now
Why exactly this much
Why at all
```

Status: Chapter 2 content itself is old / archived, but the Window No. 7 mechanism and language may still be useful for canon. Needs verification when new Chapter 2 is written.

### Queue logic

Accepted / useful idea:

- Vlad’s personal tragedy being placed into a common queue is humiliating and central.
- The pain is not denied, but it is categorized.
- Categorization wounds Vlad’s ego more subtly than direct refusal.

Potential canonical line / idea:

```text
The queue is not an answer.
Other people’s pain is not an answer.
```

Status: from old Chapter 2; preserve as useful but verify after rewrite.
