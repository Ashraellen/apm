# Vlad — Character Memory

Status: active local character memory
Created: 2026-06-22
Updated: 2026-06-22

## Core

Vlad is the main character of Book I:

`Почему я?` / `Why Me?`

He is a lawyer / jurist.

At the beginning he is not a sage, not a spiritual teacher and not an enlightened observer.

He is an irritated claimant against the universe.

He tries to turn suffering into a document.

## Function

Vlad carries the question:

`Почему я?` / `Why me?`

His path should not become quick enlightenment.

He remains resistant, ironic, wounded and bureaucratically precise for a long time.

The Book I ending does not make him a teacher or a clean carrier of correct spirituality.

By the end of Book I, the old applicant / old user begins to fail:

`Пользователь не найден.`

## Safe final Book I state

Vlad does not find God as an object, address, institution, respondent or guarantee department.

He also does not become enlightened in the simplified sense.

The important shift is that the old way of asking no longer works.

Final movement:

`Почему я?` → `Мне больно` → `Кто спрашивает?` → `Пользователь не найден`

## Tone

Vlad should be shown through scenes, reactions, irritation, legal habits and bodily confusion.

Avoid making him explain the philosophy too directly.

Avoid making him too heroic, too noble, too wise, too slick or too spiritually mature.

His sarcasm should come from fatigue and pain, not from polished performance.

## Important notes

- He works as a lawyer.
- Earthly scenes are important.
- His legal thinking reflects his inner claim against God / reality / the universe.
- He notices old scripts gradually, not heroically.
- He remains a human being, a jurist and a damaged applicant.
- His development is shown by what he no longer automatically does: not always sending, not always proving, not always documenting, not always grabbing the phone.
