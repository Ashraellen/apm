# Anna — Character Memory

Status: active local character memory
Created: 2026-06-22
Updated: 2026-06-22

## Core

Anna is Vlad's close domestic counterpart / wife figure.

She is not an ideal therapist and not a perfect wise helper.

She should be alive: practical, tired, sometimes irritated, sometimes sharp.

She can see Vlad too clearly and tease him without turning into a lecturer.

## Function

Anna keeps the earthly and domestic pressure around Vlad.

She asks simple questions.

She often cuts through Vlad's dramatic legal-metaphysical constructions with ordinary practical remarks.

She anchors the book in shared life:

- bread;
- coffee;
- stove;
- shelf;
- trash;
- dishes;
- laundry;
- parcels;
- body;
- fatigue;
- the fact that someone still has to live next to Vlad while he is being processed by his own metaphysical bureaucracy.

## Tone

Her speech should be natural, direct and sometimes dryly funny.

She should not become a guru, saint, authorial mouthpiece or endlessly patient spiritual mother.

She may be loving without being soft, and sharp without becoming cruel.

## Known accepted direction

Late chapters should bring Anna back sharper and more alive.

She should not explain the metaphysics for Vlad.

Her function is to keep him from disappearing into inner institutions and sending home a report instead of himself.

Example approved tone:

`Ты сейчас сказал 'я сейчас' тем голосом, которым люди обещают начать новую жизнь с понедельника.`

Other safe tone examples:

`Я сегодня не терапевт. Я женщина, которая хочет освободить полку.`

`Влад, я мокрая, усталая и не готова к мистике. Кратко.`
