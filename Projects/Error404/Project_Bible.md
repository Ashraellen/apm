## 2. Current format

Originally, the project was conceived as a trilogy.

Current format:

```text
A cycle / series of philosophical-satirical novels.
Each novel may reveal a separate “case” of the Heavenly Chancellery.
```

Each book may have a new central protagonist.
All books are connected by:

- the shared universe;
- the Heavenly Chancellery;
- the metaphysical mechanism of misdirected search;
- recurring or intersecting characters;
- the central question of how a person fails to find God through the form convenient to their own fear.

Book I opens the door through Vlad.

But Vlad is not the only applicant.

Behind the door there is a queue.

---

## 3. Genre

Humorous mystical-philosophical novel cycle / satirical apocrypha of the digital age.

Main intonation:

First laugh, then think. Or think, then laugh. Ideally both at once, so the reader does not immediately understand where exactly the trap closed.

This is not anti-religious satire. It is not satire against God, but against the human habit of turning God into a service, guarantee, certificate, contract, compensation system, ideology, emergency button, and supreme complaints department for life.

The cycle must be:

- blasphemous in form;
- philosophical in content;
- funny on the surface;
- serious in depth;
- slightly bureaucratic;
- slightly digital;
- slightly apocryphal;
- very human.

---

## 4. Main formula

A human being files a complaint against God — and receives an impossible response through the form of their own request.

In Book I, this appears as:

```text
A man files a complaint against God — and receives an automated response from Heaven’s support system.
```

Russian meaning:

```text
Человек подаёт жалобу на Бога — и получает автоматический ответ из Небесной службы поддержки.
```

For the whole cycle, the broader formula is:

```text
God is not “not found” because He does not exist.
God is not found because the human being searches for Him as an object, address, instance, proof, justification, guarantee, or support service.

The error is not in God.
The error is in the mode of search.
```

## 6. Main principle

The project must not become a set of funny sketches about heavenly bureaucracy.

Each joke must lead deeper:

First funny:
> The Department of Miracles is closed for inventory.

Then painful:
> Most miracles were returned to sender. They did not match recipient expectations.

Then serious:
> A person asked for salvation, but did not accept it because it came in the form of loss.

Main rule of the whole cycle:

```text
Do not explain Truth through lectures.
Show how a person protects themselves from it.
```

---

## 7. The Heavenly Chancellery

The Heavenly Chancellery is not a place, institution, office, application, or fantasy bureaucracy.

It is a state through which a person encounters their own request to God, life, fate, or Truth.

It appears to each protagonist in a form understandable to their inner system:

- for Vlad — legal-bureaucratic interface: claims, windows, numbers, notifications, categories of appeals;
- for the priest — a second layer of thought, which he may mistake for temptation;
- for future protagonists — their own systems of control, explanation, defence, knowledge, service, waiting, or denial.

The Chancellery does not give simple answers.

It registers, sorts, redirects, and forces the applicant to clarify what exactly is being asked and who exactly is asking.

## 8. Protagonist of Book I — Vlad

The protagonist of Book I is **Vlad**.

He is not a saint, prophet, chosen one, or early philosopher.

At the beginning he is:

- irritated;
- exhausted;
- ironic;
- wounded;
- somewhat unpleasant;
- not evil;
- offended by the structure of the world;
- a claimant against Creation;
- a lawyerly mind trying to formalize suffering as a document.

He does not want to save the world. He wants to understand why he tried, endured, behaved correctly, and life still kept hitting him with a stool made of meaninglessness.

In the beginning he does not seek God as God. He seeks:

- explanation;
- justice;
- compensation;
- confirmation that he was right;
- proof that his pain was not meaningless;
- evidence that someone “up there” is responsible for the mess.

Key formula:

**Vlad is not a beautiful pilgrim. He is a plaintiff in a case against the Universe.**

---

## 9. Vlad’s false God-seeking

Vlad is in a process of God-seeking, but it is wrong God-seeking.

He seeks through:

- complaint;
- intellect;
- argument;
- evidence;
- contract;
- “I tried”;
- “why can others?”;
- “why did You stay silent?”;
- “why did You not stop it?”;
- “why did You not explain earlier?”.

He seeks God as an angry customer seeking clarification about the quality of delivered reality.

That is why the form of Heaven’s support system fits perfectly.

Book I leads Vlad from:

```text
Why me?
```

toward the threshold of:

```text
Who is asking?
```

But Book I does not end with enlightenment.

It ends with the old applicant identity breaking.

Final formula:

```text
The old user is no longer found in the previous form.
```

## 10. Protagonist of Book II — priest / minister

Book II is not Vlad-centred.

Working title:

**На всё воля Его**

Possible English working title:

**On All Things His Will**

Main protagonist:

A priest / minister of an undefined confession.

Main conflict:

The priest has used the formula:

```text
На всё воля Его.
```

At first this phrase served as comfort, spiritual discipline, and a way to keep people from collapse and chaos.

But one day the word “всё” becomes absolute.

Main question:

```text
If absolutely everything is His will, then where did sin come from?
```

The fracture:

```text
If sin is outside His will, then there is a place outside His will.
If there is a place outside His will, His will is not absolute.
If His will is absolute, sin cannot exist as an independent force against God in the way many religious systems often speak about it.
```

This book must not become an anti-religious pamphlet.

It investigates the fact that a religious system may be useful, restraining, educational, and even salvific on a certain level — while still not being the Absolute Truth itself.

Main pain of the priest:

```text
If the system held people back from chaos but cannot withstand Absolute Truth, what am I supposed to do with this system now?
```

## 11. Chancellery manifestation in Book II

For the priest, the Chancellery must not appear through screens, notifications, or digital interface.

That belongs to Vlad.

For the priest, the Chancellery appears as a second layer of thought.

Not a voice.
Not a vision.
Not text on a screen.
Not a hallucination.

A thought inside thought — already complete, clear, filled, and felt as having come from beyond the ordinary process of thinking.

Formula:

```text
The thought appears in him, but not from him.
```

The priest begins to interpret these thoughts as temptation, demonic suggestion, spiritual delusion, pride of mind, spiritual attack, dangerous reasoning, or false light.

But the question does not disappear.

Main formula:

```text
He does not become possessed by a demon.
He becomes possessed by protection against the demon.
```

Alternative formula:

```text
He fights not with the devil, but with the question he cannot exorcise.
```

## 12. Approved Chancellery addresses to the priest

In Book II, the Chancellery speaks in a more internal, paternal, almost ecclesial tone.

Tone:

- soft, but unsafe;
- paternal, but cutting;
- clear, but not explanatory;
- meek, but not allowing him to turn away.

Approved forms of address:

- **Сын мой** — basic paternal address;
- **Ищущий Меня** — when the search for God itself is at stake;
- **Служитель мой** — when the question concerns his service;
- **Пастырь** — when the question concerns responsibility for others;
- **Человек божий** — when rank and role are removed;
- **Чадо моё** — rare ecclesial-paternal shade;
- **Возлюбленный сын** — very rare, for the deepest moments.

Forbidden address:

```text
Раб мой
```

Do not use it.

Reason:

In a multilingual cycle it creates the wrong social-historical shade. The project needs theological danger, not an unnecessary terminological mine.

## 13. Key phrase of Vlad

> Я пытался жить правильно.  
> Я терпел.  
> Я старался.  
> Я делал вид, что понимаю.  
> Я даже крестился.  
>  
> А теперь объясните мне, пожалуйста:  
> почему всё это не работает?

Decision:

Keep the phrase **“Я даже крестился.”** without explanation.

Reason:

It is strong precisely because of its naked awkwardness. It already contains faith, superstition, confusion, an attempt to do “as one should,” slight shame, helplessness, and a complaint to Creation.

Do not add “just in case” or similar explanations: that would make it funnier, but weaker.

## 14. Key system message

> Пожалуйста, не создавайте повторные обращения.  
> Это не ускорит проверку алгоритмов вашей души.

Use exactly:

**проверку алгоритмов вашей души**

Reason:

It connects two layers:

- soul — religious, mystical, human;
- algorithms — digital, cold, diagnostic, nonhuman.

This is the correct tone: blasphemous, funny, technological, and philosophically unpleasant.

## 16. Principle of future books

Each future book should have a new central protagonist or a new central form of error.

Possible future books:

1. Woman with a thermos — error of help, waiting, burnout, and substituting one’s own pain with other people’s requests.
2. Scientist with three pens — error of knowledge, control, calculation, and the impossibility of accounting for everything.
3. Spiritual success coach — error of selling a path the protagonist is afraid to walk.
4. Atheist who does not believe in God but is still offended that God remains silent — error of denial that still waits for an answer.
5. Mother who confuses love and control — error of care turned into ownership.
6. Person who received a miracle but returned it because of improper packaging — error of expecting an acceptable miracle.

Main principle:

```text
Each protagonist has their own way of not finding God.
```

## 17. Multilingual principle

The cycle is conceived as multilingual.

Key images, forms of address, titles, and formulas should remain translatable into English, Polish, and other project languages.

Special attention:

- Chancellery addresses;
- religious shades;
- satire;
- cultural transferability;
- avoiding too narrow a confessional or national attachment unless artistically required.

Russian text may be denser, more ecclesial, and more ironic, but the core must remain transferable.

---

## 18. What must not happen

Do not turn the cycle into:

- an anti-religious pamphlet;
- an esoteric textbook;
- self-help;
- a comic about heavenly bureaucracy;
- a set of funny scenes without pain;
- a sermon;
- a philosophical treatise disguised as a novel;
- a series where every character is visibly pre-appointed as a future protagonist.

Characters must appear naturally.

Future protagonists must look like living people, not teasers.

## 19. Short brief for new chats

**Error 404: God Not Found** is a multilingual cycle of philosophical-satirical novels about the Heavenly Chancellery and people who search for God through distorted forms of their own fear, pain, knowledge, faith, or control.

Book I, **Why Me? / Почему я?**, follows Vlad, a lawyer who tries to formalize his pain as a claim against God.

Book II, **На всё воля Его**, will follow a priest of an undefined confession who carries the phrase “all things are His will” to its absolute end and encounters the question: if everything is His will, where does sin come from?

The Chancellery appears to each protagonist in their own form: to Vlad through bureaucracy and notifications, to the priest through a second layer of thought, to the scientist through the limit of calculation, to the woman with a thermos through waiting and warmth that may not be enough.

The cycle may continue as a series of separate “cases” of the Chancellery, where each protagonist reveals a new way of not finding God.
