Source document: `ERROR_404_GOD_NOT_FOUND_project_sources_v12_FINAL_NO_CUTS.md`

This file contains character functions, voices, secondary human types, and the current canon for Vlad, Poshpshekhon, the silent second man, and recurring human mechanisms.

---

## 1. Vlad

Vlad is the protagonist of Book I: **Why Me? / Почему я?**

He replaces the earlier name Nikolai.

If older drafts contain Nikolai, replace with Vlad during literary editing.

Reason:
“Vlad” is shorter, sharper, more modern, and better suits a protagonist who begins not as a philosopher, but as a claimant against Creation.

“Nikolai” feels heavier and more confessional. “Vlad” is faster, drier, more everyday.

---

## 2. Vlad at the beginning

At the beginning Vlad must not sound too philosophically perceptive.

He is not someone already almost ready for enlightenment.

He is:
- irritated;
- exhausted;
- sarcastic;
- offended;
- somewhat arrogant;
- everyday and practical;
- convinced he was shortchanged;
- sure suffering gives him the right to demand an explanation;
- a man arguing with Heavenly support like with a stupid internet-provider bot.

His starting inner formulas:

```text
Я плачу за эту жизнь своим страданием.
```

```text
Я страдал, значит у меня есть право требовать объяснений.
```

```text
Я всё это терпел не для того, чтобы теперь мне отвечали автоответчиком.
```

The stronger his everyday arrogance and hidden resentment at the beginning, the deeper his break in the middle.

---

## 3. Vlad’s false search

Vlad does not begin by seeking God as God.

He seeks:
- explanation;
- justice;
- compensation;
- confirmation that he was right;
- guarantee that his pain was not meaningless;
- evidence that “up there” someone is responsible.

He comes to God not with a question, but with a complaint.

Key formula:

**Vlad came not for God, but for a guarantee that his version of pain would be officially recognized.**

---

## 4. Vlad’s professional weakness

Vlad is a lawyer.

He wants:
- trace;
- document;
- fact;
- screenshot;
- source;
- proof;
- something attachable to a case file.

The Chancellery answers in a way that cannot be attached to a case file.

This especially irritates him.

His professional weakness:
**If it cannot be fixed as evidence, it cannot be used.**

The Chancellery’s strength:
**Evidence is not needed by the one who has already read the answer.**

## 5. Operator of Temporary Access

Polite, dry, impossible.

Speaks like support service, but the meaning cuts deeper than any sermon.

Example:

```text
Ваше обращение принято.
Однако его содержание частично совпадает с ранее отклонённой претензией:
"Сделайте мир удобным для моего эго".
```

Function:
- interface of the Chancellery;
- dry mirror;
- never sentimental;
- does not flatter Vlad’s suffering;
- answers in terms Vlad understands: process, category, status, verification.

---

## 6. Demon of Correct Formulations

A master of turning sins into respectable language.

Example:

```text
Ненависть лучше не писать.
Поставим: глубокое несогласие с существованием другого человека.
```

Function:
- expose how people beautify ego, fear, envy, hatred, and control;
- connect legal/official language with spiritual self-deception;
- show how a person can use correct words to hide an incorrect inner state.

---

## 7. Angel of the Archive

Quiet, sad, precise.

Does not accuse. Only shows.

Example:

```text
Вот ваши молитвы.
Красным выделены места, где вы говорили с Богом.
Остальное — с последствиями.
```

Function:
- reveal old prayers;
- show bargaining hidden under devotion;
- make Vlad see his own words without commentary.

---

## 8. Holy Accountant of Self-Deception

Does not count sins.
Counts self-deception.

Example:

```text
Грехи вы иногда признавали.
Самообман — почти никогда.
А он, знаете ли, идёт отдельной строкой.
```

Function:
- separate morality from self-deception;
- show that the deepest problem is not always wrongdoing, but how Vlad narrates wrongdoing to preserve himself.

## 9. Earthly representatives of spiritual systems

They must not be cardboard frauds.

Each may contain both seed and packaging.

They may be:
- sincere but system-bound;
- kind but attached to form;
- intelligent but attached to being right;
- funny but not necessarily evil;
- useful but not absolute.

The task is not to expose everyone as liars.

The task is to show how the living easily grows into a system of owning the living.

---

## 10. God

God is not an ordinary character.

Not an old man.
Not a cloud voice.
Not the boss of heavenly tax administration.

In the novel God is:
- silence that does not evade the answer;
- presence not reduced to object;
- wholeness of existence;
- that in which seeker, search, pain, world, question, and perception are not finally separate.

By the end, God is not “found” as thing, address, figure, or boss.

Vlad stops separating God from existence, himself, the world, and the act of perception.

## 11. Poshpshekhon

Poshpshekhon appears once.

He is not a recurring mentor.
He is not a guru.
He is not a coach.
He is not a man with a spiritual consultation schedule.
He is not a seller of light.
He is not a method author.

He is an ordinary person through whom something living flashes once.

Formula:

**Poshpshekhon is a man who told Vlad the truth once. It would have been enough for Vlad’s whole life if he had been able to listen then.**

---

## 12. Poshpshekhon’s function

He is earthly proof of the Law of the Seed.

The seed may arrive:
- not from a church;
- not from a retreat;
- not from a book;
- not from an official spiritual source;
- but through an ordinary man in an ordinary place.

He does not look like a “proper spiritual teacher.”

He does not comfort.
He does not sell hope.
He does not create spiritual atmosphere.
He does not speak softly.
He speaks precisely, sharply, and inconveniently.

Vlad first receives his words as rudeness.
Later the Chancellery confirms almost the same sense in system language.

---

## 13. Poshpshekhon’s appearance

Poshpshekhon:
- blond;
- short-haired;
- outwardly almost unremarkable;
- not holy-looking;
- not a preacher;
- not a city madman with prophet eyes;
- ordinary enough for the eye to pass over;
- cuts not by appearance, but by the precision of words.

---

## 14. Donner Kebab scene

The first and last meeting with Poshpshekhon happens in **Donner Kebab**.

Not a temple.
Not a spiritual center.
Not a mentor’s office.
Not a library.
Not a retreat.
Not an esoteric shop.

An ordinary kebab place smelling of meat, onion, garlic sauce, fries, and city fatigue.

Function:
- truth comes without a signboard;
- living seed appears in an everyday place;
- Vlad is not ready to recognize the importance of the meeting;
- God again does not pass the dress code of expectation;
- the spiritual message arrives through kebab, not a golden microphone.

---

## 15. The silent second man

The second man sits with Poshpshekhon.

He must not be named Ashraellen in the text.

He is an authorial silhouette, silent witness, man without a signboard.

He is the one who first says the name “Poshpshekhon” in the line Vlad hears.

Appearance:
- larger than Poshpshekhon;
- broader in the shoulders;
- heavier in posture;
- close-shaved sides;
- long dark braid from the crown to the back of the head, tied at the neck;
- almost Viking-like, but without theatricality.

Function:
- does not explain;
- does not soften;
- does not translate;
- does not protect Vlad;
- does not act as a teacher;
- allows the scene to happen.

He may:
- dig fries out of his kebab portion;
- set onion aside;
- look out the window;
- wipe sauce from a finger;
- briefly smile;
- return to eating.

His silence matters.

## 16. Poshpshekhon must not know Vlad’s name

Important correction:
Poshpshekhon must not call Vlad by name if Vlad has not introduced himself.

If he knows Vlad’s name, the scene becomes a cheap mystical trick.

Poshpshekhon is not a clairvoyant in a kebab shop.
He is an ordinary person who hits the point without special effects.

Wrong:

```text
Да, Влад, ты страдал не зря...
```

Correct current formula:

```text
Да, гражданин, жалоба обоснована.
Да, вам недодали.
Да, виноваты все, кроме заявителя.
Да, теперь можно ничего не менять.
```

Why this is better:
- no cheap clairvoyance;
- the scene stays realistic;
- the phrase sounds bureaucratic;
- it links the kebab scene to future Heavenly support language;
- the blow becomes universal diagnosis, not personal magic.

## 18. Vlad’s reaction to Poshpshekhon

Vlad must leave not enlightened.

He leaves touched and offended.

His thoughts:
- “Грубо.”
- “Самоуверенно.”
- “Он меня не знает.”
- “Очередной циник.”
- “Интересные мысли, но подача мерзкая.”
- “Если человек прав, это ещё не даёт ему права так говорить.”

This is how he rejects the delivered message.

System summary later:

```text
Ранее пользователь уже получал ответ в человеческой форме.
Статус: отклонено как грубость.
Причина отклонения: отправитель не соответствовал ожиданиям пользователя о духовном авторитете.
```

## 19. Secondary characters as human chorus

Vlad must not carry all human ridiculousness alone.

Secondary characters form a chorus of human hunger, contradiction, ingratitude, envy, and need for recognition.

This is not to say “human beings are filth.”

It is to show that a human being is funny, hungry, wounded, contradictory, and often petty — and therefore the path to God is dramatic, recognizable, and possible.

Each secondary character should show one exact human failure, not a caricature.

---

## 20. Eternal Hunger

Eternal Hunger is the condition in which any gift immediately becomes a basis for a new complaint.

Examples:
- life was given — not enough;
- body was given — wrong model;
- chance was given — too late;
- help was given — insufficient;
- love came — wrong form;
- salvation came — without gift wrapping and promo code.

System formula:

```text
Внимание: пользователь предъявляет претензию к подарку, предварительно объявив его своей собственностью.
```

Another:

```text
Зафиксировано: пользователь принял дар, изменил контекст, объявил себя пострадавшей стороной и требует компенсации.
```

## 21. Secondary human types

### Богомольная сплетница

Lights a candle for health, then immediately discusses who lives with whom, who looks bad, who “has gone completely loose,” and whom “God sees.”

She does not call this gossip.
She calls it spiritual analysis of the situation.

### Почти святой сосед

Speaks of humility, forgiveness, and correct living, but can pee in an elevator, stick a match into a neighbor’s lock, or spoil a small thing on principle.

Not because he is a demon.
Because he is bored, offended, and needs significance.

### Вечный проситель

Prays like filling out a benefits application:

“Lord, give. But not like that. Not this. Faster. Without consequences. Preferably without needing to become different.”

### Обманутый благодарный

Received help, but offended that it came with the wrong face, not on time, not publicly enough, or too publicly.

### Духовный карьерист

Seeks God but always somehow ends up closer to:
- microphone;
- altar;
- mentor;
- front row;
- leadership;
- the right to explain salvation to others.

### Мистический завистник

Not worried that God may exist.
Worried that God may exist for others too.

Formula:

```text
Пользователь не запрашивает благо.
Пользователь запрашивает сокращение чужого блага до психологически переносимого уровня.
```

### Человек “возьмите меня / не трогайте меня”

Cries:
“Take me. Love me. Support me.”

When taken, says:
“Do not pressure me. Do not violate my boundaries. Why did you decide you can save me?”

### Любитель говнеца

Bored. Cannot stand silence. Wants emotions.

Calls drama development because drama is cheaper than inner work.

System formula:

```text
Обнаружен дефицит внутренней жизни.
Пользователь пытается компенсировать его внешним конфликтом.
```

## 22. Human contradiction

A human being says:

“Take me.”

They are taken.

“I do not want this.”

“Give me freedom.”

They are given freedom.

“Why was I abandoned?”

“Tell me the truth.”

Truth is told.

“Why are you hurting me?”

“I want God.”

They are shown silence.

“Could I have something more understandable?”

This is not an error of one person. It is a recognizable human mechanism.

The reader should recognize Vlad, secondary characters, neighbors, parishioners, relatives, colleagues, commenters, and the inner little prosecutor always ready to file a complaint against Creation.
